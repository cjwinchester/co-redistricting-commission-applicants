Organization and Advocacy Experience

In my varied aspects of community service through my sorority and other organizations, I have had the experience of organizing large group projects for the charitable benefit of black women and young girls as well as celebrating black male leadership in Colorado. In my affiliation with Facing History and Ourselves; I worked with the educational community to help foster learning the histories of various ethnic groups to obtain understanding of all of our struggles. This presented that we are more alike than different.

Statement

In today's challenging political climate; I realized that I have the skillset to assist in the data analysis for redistricting while understanding the various ethnic variables in the Denver Metro community. I have seen the results of gentrification; homelessness, and anxiety of our community and realize that representation matters.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Listening is the key to understanding. While I may not agree with those that have differing opinions; I find that listening first and seeking understanding allows for consensus building. Also, I contend that experience matters. Giving people the opportunity to experience and using tactile methods provide more perspective that can help to promote change. For example providing a group 'walk through' of a blighted area, introducing them to the people of the community and highlighting that it is a "food desert'' or has a lack of child care really brings home the reasons to help others that reside in the community. Experiential learning matters.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I was in data analysis for many years in my prior employment and understand the effect of variances, statistics and the results of 'skewness'. I also was a part of CPEX, Colorado Performance Excellence that engaged corporate audits that used the Baldrige methodology to help identify issues and gaps that may need to be addressed.
