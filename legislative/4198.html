Organization and Advocacy Experience

I worked to lead the education association's efforts in Ellicott and the Pikes Peak area. In that work, I advocated for fully funding public education in my community and at the state level so that staff and students have the resources needed to succeed.

Statement

I believe that establishing fair districts is the foundation that our state government is built on. The legislature should reflect the people and no party should have an unfair advantage derived from partisan gerrymandering. Communities of color and low-income communities deserve equal representation.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As an educator, I taught my students to disagree with compassion, something that I try to live out in my own interactions. I believe that consensus can be met by using fairly compiled data, adhering to the best practices of experts, and listening to the perspectives of others with empathy. In regards to my ability to be fair/impartial, I think that my experience as an educator also lends me a strength proven in practice. I often had to make difficult and emotional decisions in my roles but was able to step back from my own experiences to reach a fair decision, often based in compromise.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Having worked in school district administration, I was often faced with problems that didn't always have a 'right' solution. I would lean on well aggregated data and come to conclusions based on open analysis of said data. I would discuss possible courses of action with other experienced colleagues which would inform my decision.
