Background

Professional Background

Growing up, my mother was very strong in her world view, and beliefs. As an Indigenous Woman with migrant family from Mexico, she passed those cultural, and political beliefs down to me. It was not until 2016, at seventeen years old, that I became very politically active within my Pueblo Community- this was in response to the Standing Rock, Dakota Access Pipeline Resistance and the election of Donald Trump as President. Since then, I have committed to my passion for Social Justice, and equality for all Creation. I have been in numerous community led coalitions for organizing demonstrations, as well as community led contributions like handing out free school supplies.

Past Political Activity

I have not donated any money to campaigns nor political organizations. I turned 18 three years ago- at that age I briefly did some paid door to door petition work. That along with voting in the last presidential election, and local elections.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I first stepped foot in Community Organizing in 2016. I was seventeen years old. At that time, I organized with El Movimiento Sigue - a Chicano Activism group here in Pueblo, CO. Since then, I have continued to work with them, along with Pueblo Activism Exchange, Pueblo Indivisible, members of Faith Leaders In Action, Colorado Immigrants Rights Coalition, and many other community members of not only Pueblo, but Southern CO. I do not belong to any specific organization aside from my current employer, Young Invincibes.

Statement

I want to serve on this commission because I understand the importance of the demographic diversity in redistricting commissions. I want to represent not only Chicanos and Indigenous Community members during redistricting efforts, but I also aim to represent what the majority of my fellow Coloradoans stand for - fairness, and equality. If selected, I would put my best work into securing proportional representation.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Although I stand firm in my beliefs, I know and respect the importance of conversation - hearing others perspectives. Naturally, I always hear people out and do my best to understand why they may hold a certain opinion. I believe when people come together and civically engage with one another, amazing things can happen/be achieved! My own ethical stand point is being fair and respectful to all folks from all walks of life.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Research, communication, and problem solving are things that I really just love to do. Analytical skills are of the uttermost important in the political world we live in. It is impossible to fairly represent a group, or work for social justice if you do not apply logic and reason, and all that the two consist of, to your efforts.
