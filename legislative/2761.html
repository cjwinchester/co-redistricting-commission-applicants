Statement

I remember in 2002/03 there was an attempt to split a part of Douglas County into a legislative district in Jefferson County to help keep it in GOP hands. I opposed this effort, because the idea ignored the fact that Douglas County was a clearly separate community, and I advocated the DCGOP to formally oppose that idea, helping to draft the written response. I think legislative districts should be drawn with great respect for the social/political communities that have naturally developed in the past. The great amount of population growth in Douglas County, for example, may result in the need to draw new house and senate districts here, and I am concerned that such efforts may cause harm in disenfranchising some citizens who live in one social/political community but pulled out to be joined with other different communities into a legislative district for a statistical purpose without due regard of the impact upon these unique communities.

Statement on Working with Consensus

By focusing on the issues/areas where we can all agree - the basic underlying philosophies contained in our Declaration of Independence, the U.S. Constitution, and our American history, I would appeal to their sense of American patriotism. I would tell the other commissioners of my personal experience as a 19-year old U.S. Army soldier stationed in West Berlin, Germany in 1981, when I regularly crossed through Checkpoint Charlie into the Soviet Sector of occupied East Berlin. I spoke enough German to converse with many Berliners, and I learned what it was like for these humans to live in a society where their right to vote did not exist, where they did not have freedom, where their voice was not only not heard - but violently suppressed. I would appeal to the other commissioners to raise above the small narrow divisions and seek a larger picture of fairness and balance to promote our fundamental American principles that led to our democratic republic - the most unique and successful experiment in human history.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As an attorney, I have spent the last 30 years developing such skills and talents. As a prosecutor, I have had to present witness's testimony before juries and apply logic and common sense to the reality of the objective evidence, and persuade juries to agree with my analysis of those facts. I have worked with pro se defendants and legal defense counsel to seek possible avenues of resolution of cases in the pursuit of justice, helping them to see the advantages of settlement in a manner they understood was fair and just. I've had several cases appealed to higher courts for judicial review, and I have enjoyed a 100% rate of affirmation on appeal. Solving problems using logic and reason, analyzing the facts and applying the law, is what I do. I am very good at doing just this. I work very hard to achieve justice in a fair and transparent manner.
