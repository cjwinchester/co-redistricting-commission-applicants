Statement

A representative democracy is the bedrock of our country, and an opportunity to help in ensuring that Coloradans are being fairly represented is the first step. Drawing the district lines to reflect the changes in our demographics is something we only do every ten years. Talk about making an impact! This is why the work must be done well, thoroughly and carefully knowing that these maps will be how Colorado is represented at the federal level. I want to serve because I grasp the magnitude of this work. I am committed to public service and see this as another opportunity to serve.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The work of drawing our state's district lines to better reflect our demographics is not political. The point is to ensure that Coloradans are fairly represented based on the census data. The data should guide us, not one's political leanings. As commissioners, our charge will be to make the district lines reflect the demographics of the state and to foster equity. I have a history of impartiality in my work and in my volunteer work and look forward to employing these skills on the commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am an attorney and have been trained to approach complex problems through an analytical lens. I am also a practical and reasonable person with a contained temperament. All of these attributes will be helpful to the commission.
