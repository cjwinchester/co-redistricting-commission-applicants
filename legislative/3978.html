Organization and Advocacy Experience

In all my volunteer work listed above and as development director at San Isabel Land Protection Trust, I work to bring disparate viewpoints to a consensus that furthers the interests of the groups and the individuals involved. As an editorial page editor for eight years, I synthesized information from many viewpoints to come to a particular position on a public issue. I learned quickly that the best opinion pieces are based on independent reporting and fact-based advocacy. A strong factual basis is key.

Statement

At the root of our democracy is the individual vote. Nothing is more important. When doubt is cast on the efficacy of that vote, our democracy is weakened. I am a strong advocate of a nonpartisan, citizen-based redistricting. I want this process to succeed, and I think I can help.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As editorial page editor, I met weekly with our editorial board, comprising people with very different political and social views. Out of those discussions, I had to pull together the consensus view that represented the newspaper's position as an institution. My skill lies in listening to and synthesizing differing views and presenting those views accurately and fairly in a compelling way.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am able to question, listen and synthesize a wide range of views and topics. I spent my professional career taking information that I independently gathered through interviews, research and original documents to explain and educate complex subjects to our readers. I would bring those same skills to the Redistricting Commission.
