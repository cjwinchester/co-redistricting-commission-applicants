I believe the commission's work is important to making sure we all have fair representation in the legislature. Having grown up on the west side of Denver, I know how important it is to have the right people at the table advocating for you.

Statement on Working with Consensus

One of the most rewarding experiences as a mom has been to teach my children the value of fostering and developing relationships. As the saying goes "people don't care how much you know until they know how much you care". I absolutely believe that to be true. That's why I strive daily to be an active listener so I can better understand where people are coming from. If I can truly understand others' perspectives I can find ways to reach consensus. As far as impartiality goes, at Denver Human Services I have to weigh applications for benefits and make sure we are correctly and fairly assessing the eligibility for our clients. It is not my job to make subjective assumptions or to judge people for asking for help.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I analyse the overall work that we do in processing applications for assistance. It is extremely important that I pay attention to the details to make sure nothing falls through the cracks. Since the start of the pandemic our department has seen a significant increase in the number of applications. My working hours are spent being focused with an analytical mindset as I process applications but also when work with my colleagues to troubleshoot any questions that they have. I have played an integral role implementing the new rules and procedures that resulted from our entire department quickly shifting to working remotely from home. I coordinated and implemented our interdepartmental communication protocol so that we spend less time on the phone and more time helping clients. The protocols are dynamic and can change if, at any time, it logically makes sense to do so.
