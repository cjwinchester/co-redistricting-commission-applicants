Statement

I believe it is important to be involved in the political process as a citizen. I would like to ensure that the redistricting is completed in a fair way that serves the best interests of the people of Colorado. Although I do not have aspirations of running for public office, I do want to be a valuable member of the political process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Being a public school classroom teacher for the past twenty-three years, I have striven to create a learning environment that is fair, safe, and comfortable. I try to expose my students to "both sides" of every story, not swaying them one way or the other. With my colleagues, many of whom have a different political affiliation, I treat each one with integrity and consistency. I am not afraid to engage in a political conversation with someone who I know does not agree with me and how I vote. My goal is always to learn better why they choose to support a particular candidate or issue, while at the same time, clearly state where I stand on that particular matter. Recently, I was approached by a member of my community about running for an HOA board member position. They identified me because they felt I would be "honest, fair, and make decisions based with integrity in mind." Although I was humbled by their sentiments, I chose not to run for the HOA board.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

One of my best attributes is my ability to see the "big picture" in any given situation. I am governed by facts and logic, not by emotions and feelings. That is not to say I lack empathy or compassion. To survive in a classroom setting for 23 years, those two qualities are essential! However, when I am required to make a decision related to my family, I gather all the facts and details I need before moving forward; and when I make a decision for my classroom or school, I always ask the question, "What is best for the students?" That is the same approach I would bring to this position, if I have the honor of being selected.
