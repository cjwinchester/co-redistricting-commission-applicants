Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am used to working with tremendous amounts of data, both in my profession and volunteer work. The first and most important part of data analysis is making sure your data input is dependable. Once you are sure of this fact, you must then break down the data without assumptions. Sometimes a first glance tells one story when a deeper dive tells an entirely different one. The best example I can give for this is how I analyze and determine a biological parent for an adoptee. You start without a name of the bio parent and only have a list of DNA cousins that match that adoptee. You must then find the in common cousins to narrow down which line the adoptee is from. Sometimes endogamy (close relatives marrying within a line) will make it appear a cousin is closer than they are, leading you to an incorrect conclusion. Without fully researching each line, this would be missed. You must build complex family trees and take a deep dive into each line to be 100% sure your answer is correct. On occasion, a totally different outcome is found. As an example, someone who should have been the 1/2 sibling of an adoptee found out during my research that the person they thought was their father, was in fact, not their biological father. Going in with an open mind and allowing the data to speak to you while taking none of it just at face value and leaving room for surprises, allows one to really get the most out of the data that is available. Putting in the time and energy necessary is imperative to come to a conclusion you can count on.
