Organization and Advocacy Experience

Since my retirement about 5 years ago I have been participating in community organizing/engagement and advocacy through my church (Jefferson Unitarian Church in Golden) and Together Colorado. I am a member of the League of Women Voters of Colorado's Legislative Action Committee following mostly housing-related legislation for the past two years. I am on the Board of Golden United, a nonprofit (501(c)(3)), non-partisan, grassroots-driven effort to engage Golden area community members across all walks of life to find common ground and act with respect and dignity in strengthening the community. I serve as a co-chair of the Golden United Housing Task Force, promoting awareness and education about housing issues and solutions for Golden.

Statement

Fair districts are one of the foundations of democracy. I am a native Coloradan (2nd generation) and am very proud that Colorado is leading the nation in redistricting reform and want to help Colorado make history. As a retired scientific researcher, I am detail-oriented and find the concept of restricting fairness to be intriguing and extremely important.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have always tried to put democratic ideals, such as voter registration and informative candidate forums, above my personal partisan opinions. I had some facilitator training during my professional career that gave me tools for deep listening and consensus-building.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a retired research scientist so I used analytical skills and critical thinking throughout my 40+ year scientific career. Critical thinking and logic are essential to address difficult projects, such as redistricting.
