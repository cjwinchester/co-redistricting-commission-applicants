Background

Past Political Activity

During my work at a local nonprofit, we did a lot of education on local campaign and issues, held forums and panel talks. Currently I am volunteering for a Pueblo West Fire sales tax, and I've done some calling and texting for that and a local Colorado Springs school district and TABOR ballot issue.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Council of Neighbors and Organizations (CONO), Organization of Westside Neighbors (OWN), Old Colorado City Partnership, Commission on Aging, The Success Collective, City of Colorado Springs Brand Activation Team, City of Colorado Springs Public Engagement Team, El Paso County Community Outreach Group

Organization and Advocacy Experience

While serving on the Organization of Westside Neighbors (OWN) Board for the past 2 years, I implemented a first-annual strategic planning session, partnered with 3 organizations for a first-time grant to activate spaces, held community input meetings, executed monthly bike rides, basketball court paint parties, park and trail activation opportunities, communications channels and more. With this hands-on role I have applied my knowledge from CONO to pilot projects in my own neighborhood. Because of this momentum, OWN, partnering with other westside entities, is receiving larger grant funding to assist with merging efforts, business improvement district funding, and LART funding to assist with activation of pop- up street events to help create habits in an otherwise dark and under-utilized area. With OCCP we work with other Colorado business districts, learning and implementing things to support businesses and learn to create our own BID to help the community.

Statement

I would like to serve on the commission because I believe in public process and I also think I would bring a good perspective. With my extension background in neighborhood and grassroots organizing, I have an on-the-ground view of how neighborhoods and communities may look and the importance of districting. I would like to learn more about how this process works but I'd also like to be an advocate and help bring input and feedback about this process to the community where I live.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Because of my experience working for a nonprofit who contracted with local municipalities, I have had a lot of time working as the neutral middle party trying to help educate, and communicate facts vs opinions. I also believe in democracy and trained neighborhood boards on how to build consensus and have solution-based conversations. I believe in all voices being heard and coming to an agreement for the betterment of the whole. Better together!

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I work with several neighborhood organizations, HOA's, government officials, committees, community members, boards and more. Because of my ability to speak to and work with a diverse audience, I understand the importance of prioritizing, offering solutions, breaking down cause and effect and determining impact.
