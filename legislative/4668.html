Statement

I love Colorado, and I want to serve my state in a way that doesn't increase the Political divide that is so prevalent now. I believe that we are all more alike than different, and that a fair voting system combined with boundaries that make sense gives Colorado the best chance at electing leadership that best represents our local needs.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As stated above, I am affiliated with the Republican party but have actively served to help friends of all parties find success in Government. I have good friends from both the Republican party and the Democratic party, and I have enjoyed learning and celebrating the differences between the two parties. Both parties have strengths and weaknesses, the United States and Colorado benefits from having both parties working both oppositionally and cooperatively. I believe that good, fair leaders can come from all parties, and that having a cool, calm mindset can always yield a better outcome than joining hysteria and panic. As a Colorado native, I have loved seeing my state grow and contribute to the nation in so many significant ways. With that, I want to be a part of strategies to encourage growth and structure that makes sense for the Colorado electorate. Different communities have different needs, and as a Denver native, I feel I am in a unique position to evaluate the needs and demographics of different communities.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

With my educational background and years working in Public Accounting, I have a strong skill set with numbers and sets of data. I am good with deadlines, and I have learned how to multi task effectively (being a mom of 3 helps there too!). I am a good public speaker, and I tend to end up in leadership positions because of my level-headedness and ability to see problems from all sides. I believe in building coalitions, and in working cooperatively wherever possible. I also have learned that laughter and light-heartedness can be a valuable skill when facing tension and difficult decisions.
