Professional Background

Statement

I am 10-year resident of Colorado, and my experience living and voting in our great state has only been during this recent decade of change. The Colorado of 2020 is not the same as the Colorado of 2010 (or 1910); and El Paso County is not the same either. As a redistricting commission, we are going to need to be equipped to use today’s data to inform choices around district boundaries which fairly represent the all the people of Colorado. As one example, Colorado’s population of school-aged children has grown by 230,000 between 2010 and 2019. This is likely to be the fastest growing segment of Colorado residents as the 2020 Census reporting becomes available. As mother of school-aged children, I want to be sure the commission members analyze fairly the data put before them before drawing districts which have implications for both our current and next generations of voters and elected representatives.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I form my perspective based upon data. I also believe that listening to others and discussing differences are critical skills to building consensus. Part of my professional work is facilitating focus groups and small group discussions where I am asked to inquire actively, listen whole-heartly, understand empathetically, and manage time well. Working in the consumer behavior field requires me to establish rapport quickly across people of different ages, genders, ethnicities, and life experiences around the world. I believe these skills will serve me well in interacting with a diverse set of fellow commission members on a common set of objectives for the people of Colorado.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I organize, aggregate, and summarize multiple sources of information so that decisions makers can take action. I have 30 years of experience with quantitative data analysis and statistical software packages, including performing analysis on US Census data files. I have taught quantitative analysis methods to 100s of business professionals.
