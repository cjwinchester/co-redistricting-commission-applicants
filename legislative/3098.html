Background

Past Political Activity

My experience organizing has largely been within the University of Colorado ecosystem, particularly the engineering school at Boulder. I found the lack of community alarming and detrimental to the graduation and success of female and minority students from the department, and sought to battle it by creating a space of mentorship and community support via regular meetings of the Women in ECEE group, which accepted all members regardless of identity. I have also used my own social media platforms and personal time to campaign for Andrew Romanoff and amplify the problems faced by underserved communities in Colorado.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I have phonebanked for Andrew Romanoff. I donated money to Elizabeth Warren, Amy McGrath, Ossoff, Warnock, and the Colorado Democratic Senate Fund.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have been an organizer for the Women in Electrical, Computer, and Energy Engineering department at the University of Colorado at Boulder. I volunteer as a tutor and STEM mentor with Girls, Inc. of Metro Denver.

Statement

I read through Denverite/the Colorado Sun that the state is struggling to find a diverse enough pool of candidates for redistricting. I didn't know that this was a way that citizens could serve, and it reminded me of growing up in Maryland and feeling frustrated as a high schooler reading news about local gerrymandering that divided Latino and Asian populations into separate districts to boost Democratic support but also limit the chance that those districts might yield a Latino or Asian candidate. It's exciting to me that I would be able to do public service on behalf of fair representation rather than having to phone bank or campaign for ballot issues or partisan campaigns.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a female engineering student, I learned to create spaces of positivity and productive, educational conversation among students to battle the hostility that so many students (whether female, international, and/or non-typical demographic) faced. I am a woman who works in an extremely male-dominated field of radio frequency engineering at Lockheed Martin. Promoting consensus and agreement means maintaining a collegial tone and being unafraid to confront individuals on the team being overly aggressive and unproductively hostile. I am experienced in being the lone voice in the room disagreeing with unintentionally discriminatory, gendered statements and emotional biases (although I look forward to the day where I do not have to be alone in this).

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am an engineer who firmly believes in science and not only data, but also the importance of questioning data. Is the data wholly representative? What is the sample size? What methods did they use to collect this data? Who might be missing and how can we gather that missing data and/or deduce possible actions to remedy future attempts and improve our quality of information? I'm looking forward to being able to take a look and use my engineering skills for civic duty.
