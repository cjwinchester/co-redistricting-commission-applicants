Statement

want to serve on the commission to help the people of Colorado better realize the founding promise of our democracy of "one person, one vote." By this, I mean that I want to assist in analyzing the current Colorado legislature district boundaries in Colorado and eliminate bias in those boundaries that may tend to cause an impermissible overrepresentation or underrepresentation of certain voter groups.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I can promote consensus among the commissioners by utilizing by negotiation skills that I have built as a transactional attorney to bring commissioners to a mutually-agreeable result. These skills include (1) active listening, (2) assessment of each person's position, (3) finding acceptable common ground among each person, and (4) communicating such common ground to each person and asking them to concede non-critical positions to reach that common ground.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a lawyer by training, I apply logic and analytical skills daily to solve problems. Principally, this revolves around analyzing the terms of commercial contracts, communicating those analyses to clients, and negotiating those terms with opposing parties. Additionally, in applying logic and reason to a problem, I prefer to use data, speak to stakeholders, and consider applicable costs and benefits.
