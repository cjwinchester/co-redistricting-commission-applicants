Background

Past Political Activity

I have not been politically active. This is due mostly to the Hatch Act and limitations regarding federal employee involvement with political activities.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I volunteered through Denver Rescue Mission working with a Family Rescue client who was being resettled after homelessness (2018). I have been an active member at South Fellowship Church, Littleton, CO, for fifteen years; I have worked with our children's ministry and on a women's ministry leadership team. While I was still employed, I served on Gov. Hickenlooper's task force through the National Governors' Association Prescription Drug Policy Academy that initiated the creation of the Colorado Consortium for Prescription Drug Abuse Prevention. I served on the executive board for several years prior to my retirement.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

In 2012/2013 I served on Gov. Hickenlooper's task force through the National Governors' Association Prescription Drug Policy Academy that initiated the creation of the Colorado Consortium for Prescription Drug Abuse Prevention. At that time, Colorado was in the top 5 states in the nation inundated with prescription drug abuse. Both professionally and personally, I was concerned for the trend that was undercutting the health and well being of my home state. I became vested in the work being done by the Governor's Office, offering my professional expertise to help educate the team on regulatory requirements and the reasons behind them. I attended seminars and roundtable discussions to learn and discuss best practices for a wide range of professions including medical, education, behavioral health, and law enforcement. Together we created a path forward for Colorado to reduce the prescription drug abuse problems. During the same time frame, I was leading my group of investigators in one of the largest investigations conducted in DEA history against a major industry player, a drug distribution corporation, who was blatantly involved in the diversion of controlled pharmaceutical products across the United States, including Colorado. I was assigned the job related duty to coordinate and orchestrate the DEA's National Take Back Initiative (NTBI) for proper disposal of prescription drugs throughout Colorado. I built a coalition of federal and state partners and recruited local law enforcement agencies throughout Colorado to successfully host and attain twelve biannual events. Throughout my involvement in the NTBI, I coordinated with Division personnel in 4 states to achieve 100% environmentally friendly destruction of all collected drugs.

Statement

I have always been civic minded and interested in the "big picture" mechanics of what makes our social and judicial systems work or fail to work. I enjoyed the regulatory aspects of my paid profession as well as networking with a diverse cross section of law enforcement, industry, and regulatory agencies to ensure that there was a reliable supply of controlled substance medications available to meet the legitimate medical, scientific, and research needs of our country. I believe that serving on the Commission is an important duty and that I have skills and abilities to contribute towards the work being done.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I will make a concerted effort to be an informed participant. I am a good listener and I ask good questions in order to make sure that what I am hearing is, in fact, what is being said. Having been raised on the Western Slope and living there for many years before living on the front range, I care about the whole of Colorado, I understand some of the differences which exist between city dwellers and our rural residents. The job I held required that I study divergent points of view and yet gain a consensus or at least arrive at a solution which was acceptable to all parties. I believe through my years of working as well as years of being a parent, I learned the ability to be fair and impartial in my assessments of situations and to arrive at successful conclusions.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

One example: before I worked with the federal government, I was trained as an intelligence analyst for the Colorado Bureau of Investigation. I worked an unsolved murder case and had to review and analyze a wide range of potential evidentiary material. I had to review material and determine what was relevant evidence. I was tasked with creating a time line and inputting the relevant evidence; that process allowed me to see where things matched up or to discern when the evidence was at odds. Through this effort, I was able to offer the detectives another opinion of who may have committed the crime. I think this early training provided me with a good skill that served me, my agency, and my co-workers well throughout my career.
