Background

Professional Background

I was the Pitkin County representative to the Bar Association Board of Governors for approximately 6 years in the early 2000's. At about the same time I was a member of Governor Ritter's Domestic Violence Taskforce. I served as president of my local Carbondale Rotary Club in 2006. I have been a member of the Board of Directors of the Aspen/Pitkin County Employee Housing, Inc. organization for 30 years. It is a private nonprofit entity which constructs and manages rent restricted housing in the Aspen and Roarking Fork Valley. We've built then owned and operated a 32 unit multifamily rental housing development in Aspen for that entire period of time. We are now in the process of building a new 30 unit rent controlled development in Carbondale with tax credit financing. I was in the late 1990's on of the principal organizers and initial President of the Roaring Fork Valley affiliate of Habitat for Humanity.

Past Political Activity

Statewide and nationally, I have had fairly limited experience. I have been an active participant in local (town and county) political campaigns for over 20 years. I have contributed to various Democratic candidates for statewide or federal office in the election cycles since 2015, including Andrew Romanoff (2020), Joseph Biden (2020), Diane Mitsch Bush (2020), Beth McCann (Denver DA) 2018, 2020. Hilary Clinton 2016

Statement

I think that redistricting is one of the most important and overlooked functions of our government, which has a huge influence on governmental operations and individual representation over a ten year period. The importance of this process in actually implementing the 'one person, one vote' rule cannot be overstated.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I feel that I am generally a consensus builder and would certainly utilize those skills in the work of the commission. I feel that I have a very good geographic base and a pretty fair understanding of the state of Colorado and most, if not all, of its local watersheds and communities of interest. I think that this knowledge will be of great assistance in the commission's work. I have also spent a fair amount of time in several different periods looking at the re-establishment of county boundaries, specifically the boundary between Pitkin and Eagle Counties which unfortunately does not follow logical geographic and physical boundaries, but is instead a straight line, resulting in fairly large disasociation of a portion of the voting populace from the seat of county government.
