Statement

I wish to give back to my community and state as I have been most fortunate to live, build a wonderful family, and to establish successful businesses in the state. As a result of being a successful businessman, I have dealt with state and local government officials on both sides of the aisle and I bring unique insights into the negotiation of redistricting lines. My background includes working with people from both sides of the aisle and with those of different racial and sexual identities. I employ a diverse group of people from all over the country and of varying religious and political backgrounds. This has allowed me to work with and listen to people on both sides of the aisle and appreciate the importance of listening and processing how others think. As an independent voter I understand both sides of the aisle, which I will take into account as an agent for building and achieving consensus. Accordingly, I understand that the new redistricting map must account for the many differences in the state. Places like Colorado Springs and Denver are diverse in how they see the political world and this must be reflected in the new district maps. There must be fairness in determining the new districts, which requires listening to the input of all schools of political thought in the decision-making process. My educational, political, and business background has taught me methods in dealing with diverse opinions and thought processes in dealing with problem resolution in non-threatening ways. As an independent voter, I listen to both sides of the aisle and appreciate the need to be fair and equitable in establishing a thorough districting process. I can and will be impartial in voicing my thoughts to the committee as we draw the new district map.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My greatest strengths are in team building and problem solving through finding common ground among disagreeing participants. I have always gotten along with diverse people with differing beliefs and attitudes. One of my most effective techniques in gaining consensus is breaking groups into smaller units which encourages people to speak their minds. Once that’s achieved, and people see each other as people rather than as opponents, problems can be resolved once the small groups are brought back with their other participants in the larger group. An example of my consensus and team building is my Cable TV business. It comprises Accounting, HR, Marketing, Training, and Operations departments. Each department is composed of different types of people with differing perspectives on the business. My role was to develop each department into a cohesive unit that allowed the organization to grow into a national entity. This demonstrates my ability to build organizations in differing industries with diverse groups of people. In my role as board chair of the Marijuana Industry group, I dealt with small, medium, and large organizations with differing views and needs. I facilitated the members coming together to work with legislators and the Marijuana enforcement division in developing Cannabis legislation and rulemaking for the industry. I have always operated as a consensus builder. I have built various business that operate throughout the U.S. As such, I have worked with people of different religious and political backgrounds in order to be successful at business as well the boards I have served on. I pride myself as a negotiator and consensus builder which requires listening to diverse points of view. I am not a one-sided person; diversity lends to better decisions and outcomes!

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

In graduate school, I studied statistics and have used this background throughout my professional career. My statistical knowledge has been an important part of my success as a multiple business operator due to its emphasis on logic and reasoning when it comes to problem solving. I can bring this same approach to the commission and analyze problems with logic and reason. In addition, as a natural leader, I am confident that through good listening and using logic in understanding other points of view, consensus can be achieved. Through logic, reasoning, and consensus building, I am confident that I can work through difficult problems with the other redistricting commissioners and create a new district map that represents Colorado fairly.
