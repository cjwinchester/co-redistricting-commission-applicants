Background

Professional Background

My position within Jeffco Schools requires that I listen carefully to all stakeholders (parents/families, students, teachers, principals and student advocates) to resolve problems either in communication or a disagreement of core values while upholding educational policies at both the district and state level. In addition, it has been my responsibility to organize and facilitate these meetings in a way that balances the power between all stakeholders and requires speaking to each party beforehand to accomplish this. I also serve as a student and family advocate in situations where policy is not being upheld within school buildings, while maintaining an understanding of the capabilities of school personnel and funding to manage the expectations of those I represent.

Statement

I am a proponent of equality and want to be sure that all voices, particularly the underrepresented, are heard. I think it would be an opportunity to serve my community in a way that utilizes my personal strengths and would welcome the opportunity to learn more about the people within the community, and the challenge of working towards a civic agreement.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe that the best way to encourage thoughtful conversation and consideration is to ask good questions. Because I have worked for many years as a professional coach and teacher, I believe I am highly skilled in working with adults, including those that are reluctant to make concessions or work together. Because I am a person that is always interested in creating a plan that can be put into action without sabotage, I am dedicated to thinking impartially while working to understand systemic issues in addition to what each stakeholder believes to be fair.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

When considering all sides of an issue, it is important to ask relevant questions to get a better understanding of the situation through data and through each person/stakeholder's current viewpoint. This includes the big picture, as well as each stakeholder's perception of the situation. I have applied this skill professionally for over 20 years as a customer service manager, teacher, and professional coach and have learned both formally and informally how to analyze and apply logic to problem solving while considering people's emotions and values. While it would possibly be easier to look at a problem using data and logistics, part of what needs to be analyzed in a civic situation is the degree of violation in a group's values and the emotion attached to that.
