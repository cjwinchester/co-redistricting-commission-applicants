Past Political Activity

I have canvassed, made phone calls, written letters and postcards and texted for Democratic candidates, in 2016, 2018 and 2020. I'm a member of Indivisible NOCO and Indivisible. I'm a Precinct Committee Person for the Larimer County Democrats. I served as an Election Protection volunteer in 2004 and 2020.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I founded a local Indivisible chapter soon after the 2016 election, which later merged with Indivisible NOCO. I have been active with that group since 2017, advocating on a range of issues including healthcare, climate change and foreign policy.

Statement

I have seen the effects of gerrymandering and how it skews elections toward incumbents and takes away the voices of citizens who are in the minority party. I believe this contributes greatly to our polarized electorate, and I believe it's in the best interest of all of us to have fair and balanced state and congressional districts. As a Democrat living in Wyoming and Colorado, I’ve spent most of my adult life (until recently) being in the minority party in my state, congressional district and local districts. I have felt frustrated at the one-sided blocs that seem impenetrable, and the fact that my voice can so easily be ignored. On a personal level, I'm interested in politics and governing although I have never run for office myself. Serving on the commission would give me a better understanding of this aspect of our electoral system and a chance to serve my state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am interested in and willing to listen to opposing viewpoints, and have the capacity to understand both sides of an issue. I believe consensus should be the ultimate goal of most discussions, and I think that one of my strong points is coming up with creative solutions to difficult problems. The ultimate goal of redistricting is fairness, and I’m committed to being fair and impartial in this endeavor.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am able to think through problems in a logical manner and come up with creative and reasonable solutions. I have done this for years in my work, my volunteer activities and in raising my two daughters. In my work as a historic preservationist, I have often dealt with issues of private property rights versus the good of the greater community. In these situations, I carefully review the evidence and appropriate laws, ordinances, and in some cases judicial opinions in order to make an informed decision.
