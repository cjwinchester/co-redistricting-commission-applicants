Background

Past Political Activity

I was a delegate to the Texas democratic state convention in 1994. However, a far more important political activity is voting. I have voted in every election, for so long that my kids no longer even try to catch me on small local elections. Nothing is a better teacher than example. and nothing is a more important political activity than voting.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Current volunteer with Jefferson County Open Space. Previous volunteer for over ten years for Street Reach - an organization providing meals for the homeless in downtown Denver. Previously volunteered on several faith based committees.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

My experience in organizing and advocating for groups and organizations is primarily in a not for profit business setting where I advocated to regulators on behalf of my members. Also, I have served as a jury foreman on a criminal case in Colorado where my role was to initiate and encourage respectful, fact based conversation where everyone felt heard, including the two eighteen year olds on the jury panel. Then to work toward a decision - the verdict.

Statement

First, I have the time. Second, I have experience working with diverse groups in a not for profit environment and know how to contribute positively in group setting and even lead those discussions. Third, I think it's really important to get this right, other states may follow our lead. Fourth, I have the analytical skills and experience to reach conclusions on numbers based problems.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Having worked for two not for profits, I understand how non-top-down organizations work. I've chaired member committees for years and used the following approach successfully. First, listen, ensure everyone has their say, understand that not everyone sees things the same way, listen respectfully to everyone and actively encourage everyone's participation and then look for consensus solutions

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I've had a career in financial industry, have an MBA and a statistical background, so have the analytical skillset and dealt with numerical problems throughout my career. As to applying logic and reason to a problem in a group setting, what has worked for me is (1) Get the group to agree on the goals (some examples in this setting could be - as best as possible, ensure all voters have a voice, no strange shaped districts like Michigan or North Carolina; geographic areas with similar concerns should be in similar districts; and so on). (2( Get the group to agree to civil and open discourse, (3) Let the discussion take its course encouraging everyone to participate and raise questions or objections based on the agreed goals. (4) Get buy in to what was agreed to.
