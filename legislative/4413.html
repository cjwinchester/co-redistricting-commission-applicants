Statement

There is a wonderful phrase that I was introduced to years ago, and that has stuck with me since: " A generosity of spirit." this country has provided me with a wonderful life and career, and I am very proud to be an American citizen. I believe it is important to give back, and I believe that now, in retirement, I have been provided with the time to give back to my country, and specifically, to the State of Colorado. Democracy is not something that we can take for granted, and the creation of this commission says much about Colorado's willingness to take the lead in protecting democracy for the future. I grew up in Ireland during "The Troubles", caused in part by gerrymandering and discrimination, and I understand just how important a fair and equal system for election is.

Statement on Working with Consensus

It really does not matter whether it is a school, a company, or a commission, the key to success in any organization is trust. Only when trust has been firmly established can any group move onto those critical, hard conversations that need to happen. These conversations often involve conflict, and that is both to be expected and encouraged, for with trust in place, conversations can eventually move to a place of consensus. It is not a fast process, but it can be achieved. It is a process that can be easily pushed of the rails by an individual, and that is why selection process for the commission members is so important. There is an author called Lencioni. and he wrote a book called "The Five Dysfunctions of a Team" that is popular and that might be a great read for all commission members before they start their work.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Being a Head of School in the private sector is like being the CEO of a small (some large) company. You are responsible for all aspects of the school from governance to budget, to admissions, to future thinking and planning, from fund raising to creating product. Of greatest importance are two items: ensuring that all your efforts are mission-driven, and that you do not drift from that. and in ensuring the success and growth of your colleagues that are, also, your employees. To be successful, the leader must examine almost all aspects of the school in a logical, organized manner, and leave emotions to one side.
