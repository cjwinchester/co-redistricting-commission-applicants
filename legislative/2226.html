Statement

Commissions such as this tend to be heavy weighted with a tier of community activists, political operatives and agenda. Having worked in rural medicine for the 9 years prior to retirement and Front Range private practice 20 years prior, the divide between rural and urban interface culture and economies , the tendency for the Front Range to dismiss rural concerns and for rural communities to feel slighted, is amplified by the factors inherent in strictly political and/or population based apportionment. In my mind, this only hardens political divisiveness. A Democrat from Denver or a Republican from Grand Junction have very little motivation to compromise on partisan issues affecting the other disproportionately - gun rights and urban health centers come to mind. We have been fortunate thus far to have fair and reasonable legislators. I don't believe this will continue. This commission and its success is so important, many people need to step up so the panel can select suitable folks, not just expedient people. I have no clue who you seek but would like to give the selection committee choice.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Listening, negotiating, compromising and learning has been my professional life for almost 40 years in matters both critical and trivial. I have run a practice, worked on boards and commissions and believe I understand the need to set aside agenda and foster dialogue to achieve the optimal environment for constructive solutions.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission
