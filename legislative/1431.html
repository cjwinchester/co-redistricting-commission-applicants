Background

Professional Background

My experience comes from running for political office and a member of RTD's disability committee. While on the disability committee, I have advocated greatly that RTD does more. I have seen for three years now that RTD is very slow in making decisions. When I share my ideas and solutions, the disability committee members agree with me. Outside of that, nothing else.

Past Political Activity

2016 - GOP Candidate for Colorado's 6th Congressional District. I lost at the GOP 6th Congressional Convention in April 2016. 2020 - RTD Board of Directors District A. RTD is nonpartisan and, as such, I attended local Democrat and Republican meetings to let voters know they could vote for me. I lost the election. I have donated to the Trump Campaign since 2017. Maybe once to the Colorado State GOP party. I have paid entrance fees to GOP meetings for breakfast clubs and lunch clubs. For local and state campaigns, I don't think I have ever donated to an individual candidate campaign. Also, forgot to add a precinct person for my precinct for the 2020 election cycle.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

For the past three years, I have been a volunteer member on RTD's Advisory Committee for People with Disabilities (ACPD). I graduated from Denver Regional Council of Governments (DRCOG)'s Citizens Academy.

Statement

One, it is my duty to serve America. Just like President John F. Kennedy said: "Ask not what your country can do for you; ask what you can do for your country." Well, I am doing exactly what he said with serving on this Commission. Two, I enjoy challenges and solving puzzles. I was an accountant for almost 9 years. Everyday I had problems coming my way, but I soon discovered that many of these problems can be avoided with easy solutions and talking to my coworkers. And I did just that and it worked!

Statement on Working with Consensus

I can be fair and impartial easily. For example, while running for the nonpartisan race of RTD, I went to Democrat and Republican meetings. I went door-to-door during my campaign asking for people's votes. I did not care if they were Green, Socialist, Republican, Unaffiliated, etc. I care greatly about RTD and wanted everyone to know how I can make it better for everyone. I can promote consensus amongst the commissioners as I have patience and the ability to listen to people. I prefer to listen, first, before making any kind of judgement. That way, I can think carefully and provide great ideas and solutions that will work for my colleagues, such as the commissioners.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

For this answer, it comes from almost nine years as an accountant. As a former accountant, I had to apply logic and reason to problems every single day. If I didn't nothing would get done and the balance sheets and the GLs would be messed up allowing the potential for misuse of funds and runaway expenses. Thus, I can do the same with this Commission. There is only a set number of people that Colorado will have. This means we can easily piece the puzzle together and come to an amicable solution for everyone.
