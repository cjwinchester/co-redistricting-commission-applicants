Statement on Working with Consensus

Strategies for promoting consensus start with meeting in person and listening. Everyone present needs to be heard. When there is disagreement, clearly identify why people disagree, which can be a lack of information, different personal values or past experience. Process work is important to move disagreement subtly from subjectivity to objectivity. Seeing specifically where parties disagree (without making anyone wrong) is a start, then identifying the common goal and the areas where people agree on the path to reaching the goal is the next step. Continue building objectivity by identifying alternative scenarios to the common goal (in writing) breaking down the steps necessary in reaching the goal. In areas where parties still disagree, a rating strategy may help from most to least popular and eliminating those that appear unlikely to arrive at the most likely option. I've found that 9/10 of the work is done by this point. I would like to add that diplomacy, mutual respect and openness are critical for successful consensus building. People really need to feel like you have compassion and respect for them throughout the process, and beyond.
