Statement

Serving on the commission presents an incredibly unique opportunity to participate in the redistricting process in the state of Colorado, to ensure the process of the non-partisan commission goes smoothly and to redistrict with the ideals of democracy at stake. I would like to provide my analytic, reasoning and consensus-building skills to create voter districts that are accurately representative of the community. I want to work with people of different political persuasions to reach consensus on what is best for Colorado. I am also interested in hearing from individuals from different communities and political persuasions on the importance of the redistricting process for them in their individual communities. Not only do I want to participate in the re-districting commission, but I have the analytic and listening skills to be a successful and beneficial participant.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Every day I work in an environment where I need to balance the needs and interests of adverse parties. At its heart, my law practice requires me to build consensus amidst these competing interests. I must understand the goals and motives of my clients, my own team of attorneys, judges, and opposing counsel in order reach favorable outcomes. I must communicate, engage with and listen to differences of opinion on a daily basis, a skill that will help me on the commission. Creating consensus is a key driver in settlement negotiations which occur on a regular basis. I will use these analytic and consensus-building skills on the commission, using logic and reason to develop compatibility among commissioners who may have divergent political views. I will engage with the commissioners and most importantly, listen. I also believe I can be fair and impartial and take my emotions out of this process, which I must do every day in emotional and litigious cases.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My day job requires using analytical skills, applying logic and reason to a problem, and negotiating disputes on behalf of a diverse range of clients. This requires determining the facts of the case, interacting with all parties involved, understanding the legal framework that governs a particular type of dispute, and communicating with all parties involved in order to understand what the case is about. While I mainly practice commercial, land use and municipal litigation, often new cases require me to dive into a new set of law that I have never practiced before. Becoming familiar with a new legal doctrine requires analytic research and writing skills, which I do in order to solve my clients’ problems. My goal is to understand all the perspectives and underlying factual background, after which I must apply logic and reason to argue my case, whether in front of a judge, managing partner, client or opposing partner. Understanding where consensus could be built to solve a particular problem is a critical component of the job and crucial for settlement negotiations.
