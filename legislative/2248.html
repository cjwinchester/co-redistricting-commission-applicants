Background

Professional Background

I have spent a career working with both polical parties and a wide variety of interests groups and trade associations. I have worked closely with local school districts and their local communities in all parts of the state: conservative and liberal; rural, urban, and suburban; west slope, mountains, San Luis Valley, front range, northeastern plains, and southeast; oil and gas dependent, agricultural, resorts, rapidly growing and economic decline. I have a deep understanding of all parts of Colorado. I have traveled and worked in nearly every corner of the state for most of the last 40 years.

Past Political Activity

I have occasionally donated small sums of money to candidates in both parties running for the Colorado legislature and to Republican Party national Senate and Congressional Committees. I have donated money to campaigns for the state Board of Education and to candidates in local school board races who are personal friends.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I currently serve on the board of the Colorado Fiscal Institute. In my capacity as Executive Director of CASB I served on the Board of the School Finance Project and was active in several state wide ballot issue campaigns over the years.

Statement

I believe deeply in our form of government, a democrat republic. However, it works best if all groups have their voices effectively represented in the legislature. Gerrymandering tends not only to create safe districts for both parties and more safe districts for a majority party, but also to make legislators elected from those districts to be one dimensional in their views and votes. I want to participate in a process to create more balanced districts, fewer safe seats and a broader diversity of views in all districts.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have spent a 40 year career working with all communities across Colorado. For example, during our fall regional meetings at CASB, one night I would meet with a group of school board members in Montrose who strongly support TABOR and strongly oppose state mandates on sex education. A couple of nights later it would be a group of front range board members with equally strong views opposing TABOR and supporting sex education mandates. At statewide meetings I needed to address state and federal policy issues in a way that convinced both camps on a policy direction for CASB. In short, I have long experience in listening to diverse views and beliefs, acknowledging the merits of all arguments, and forging some agreement on how to proceed with all hands on deck.
