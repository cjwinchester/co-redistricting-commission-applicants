Background

Professional Background

Two experiences illustrate particularly well my abilities in organizing, representing, advocating for, and considering interests. The first is when I lead the Poudre Schools District Accountability Committee review of the first charter school application in Fort Collins. The second is successfully leading my homeowners’ association in changing the neighborhood covenants. As an unpaid volunteer, I was an active participant in the Poudre Schools District Accountability Committee when the first charter school application was submitted. At the time, charter schools were a political hot potato. Following state law, it fell to the District Accountability Committee to review the application and consider the interests of the applicant, the school district, and the community as a whole. I volunteered to head the review subcommittee and organized four other subcommittee members. Under my leadership, the subcommittee created a fair and impartial process by first identifying 28 application requirements specified by state law and the school district. The subcommittee then painstakingly reviewed the application against these standards and evaluated whether required information was provided, whether it was sufficiently detailed, and whether the applicant's proposal for each requirement was acceptable. Finally, I represented the subcommittee by presenting to the entire District Accountability Committee and to the school board our findings regarding the merits and deficiencies of the application. The subcommittee did a masterful job and, as a result, everybody was angered. The pro-charter people were upset because we did not unquestionably accept the application. The anti-charter people were upset that we did not flatly deny the application. These reactions are evidence of our impartiality. And the subcommittee? We had more than consensus. We were unanimous in our findings. By focusing on the standards set by law and first developing a fair and impartial process for review of the application against the standards, the subcommittee was able to stay above the political fray. In the end, the applicant was able to address deficiencies in the application and the charter school was approved. Since then other charter schools have come and gone in Fort Collins but this school remains strong and successful. I truly believe our subcommittee deserves some credit for that. I am proud of my efforts in organizing the subcommittee, representing the subcommittee before the school board, advocating for the subcommittee’s recommendations, and considering the interests of the various stakeholder groups. I envision a similar process for the redistricting commission. This effort has the potential to be politically destabilizing for some entrenched interests. After all, if the commission does its job well, some currently uncompetitive districts will made competitive... and some people are not going to like that. I believe if redistricting commission focuses on the standards set by law and develops and follows a fair and impartial process around those standards, it will be successful in achieving its mission. More recently, as an active participant in my homeowners’ association, I served on the Board of Directors when it sought to make a covenant change. Want to take on a challenge? Volunteer for your HOA! As President for much of this process, I organized most aspects of this effort. I sought to ensure that Board members were thoroughly informed of all available options. With impartially, I insisted that the Board patiently allow anyone to speak and that everyone be heard. In this way the Board was able to consider the interests of all our neighbors. I also stepped aside when others wanted to take a more active leadership role (and then stepped back into leadership when they decided leading really was not that much fun). In this way I was able promote consensus by building trust and creating buy-in. In the end, the Board reached unanimous agreement on a proposal to present to our homeowners. We were able to achieve consensus among our homeowners with 150 of 175 voting households in favor of the proposal. These numbers are truly amazing--a very high level of neighborhood engagement with 86% of households in agreement! Per state law, our final step was to obtain court approval of the changes. I represented my HOA in court and advocated on behalf of my neighbors. Our efforts were successful. Following approval by the court, the covenant changes went into effect. They had an immediate impact on our HOA, greatly strengthening its financial situation. This summer costly repairs were made to the asphalt path through our neighborhood common area. The funds for these repairs were made available because of the changes to our covenants. I am deeply satisfied to see the legacy of my efforts and the positive impact of effectively organizing this project, representing and considering the interests of my neighbors and our neighborhood, and advocating for them. These experiences give me confidence that I will serve well if selected to serve on this commission.

Statement

I want to serve on this commission for three reasons. First, I strongly support the mission of this commission. I know from personal experience that competitive districts benefit our communities. I seek to help the commission fulfill its mission. Second, I am uniquely qualified to serve on this commission. All of my experiences seem to be pointing to this particular opportunity. In other contexts, I have put into practice the skills and abilities called for—organizing, representing, advocating for, and considering interests. I have a proven track record of fairly and impartially creating agreement and promoting consensus. My experiences campaigning and as a candidate have provided me with analytical skills that are particularly relevant for this effort. Third, I desire to leave a legacy. If this commission does its job well, its impact will be no less than 10 years, the period until the next census. I believe, though, that the commission’s legacy could be much longer. The precedents set and procedures put into place are likely (with tweaks) to be used by future commissions. The echoes may be generational. In addition, Colorado is a leader in the nation in the use of citizen-based independent redistricting commissions. We may be able to show the rest of the country how to be successful in putting an end to partisan gerrymandering. I want to be a part of this legacy. I am applying for both the Legislative and Congressional Redistricting Commissions. My preference is to serve on the Legislative Redistricting Commission. However, I would consider it a privilege and honor to serve on either commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In my discussion of my experience in organizing, representing, advocating for, or considering the interests of groups, organizations, or associations in Colorado, I recount two challenging situations where I promoted consensus, created agreement, and was fair and impartial. Please carefully review that portion of my application. I am a process person. How we get to an outcome is often just as important as the outcome itself. I have found it is much easier to get to agreement and consensus when processes and procedures are hashed out beforehand. If selected to serve, I will urge my fellow commissioners to first develop processes and procedures for how business and hearings are conducted and how proposed district maps are evaluated relative to legal requirements and competitiveness. Building trusting on a one-on-one basis with the other commissioners facilitates agreement and consensus. I have done this in the past by: > Making and keeping promises, > Transparency, > Bending over backwards to elicit and listen to the input of others, and > Assumption of positive intent. In addition to creating agreement and promoting consensus, good processes help to assure fairness and impartiality.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My two engineering degrees certainly speak to my analytical skills. More importantly, I have relevant analytical skills developed by working on campaigns and being a candidate. I am experienced digging into voter and demographic data to determine precinct and district composition and to assess likely electorate behavior. I am a people-oriented analyst. This means that I have the soft skills to work effectively with others while possessing strong logic and reasoning skills. I have great confidence in my ability to serve well on a redistricting commission. It is my sincere hope that I will have the opportunity to do so. Respectfully submitted, Eric Kronwall
