Organization and Advocacy Experience

I work at a nonprofit, bipartisan advocacy organization aiming to make transportation more equitable across Colorado. Specifically, we focus on bicycles and making biking safer and more accessible for anyone who chooses to ride, but that overlaps with a much wider set of roadway safety issues.

Statement

My goal in being a part of the commission would be to ensure an equitable redistricting that is truly representative of Colorado's population. I've been troubled by accusations of gerrymandering in Colorado and elsewhere, and would do my part to ensure that doesn't happen. I do my best to be an empathetic and active listener and take identities other than my own into account when making any decisions, and would bring that to the commission. 

Statement on Working with Consensus

I seek multiple perspectives in everything I do. I do not believe that I am a monolith or that my opinions are the only ones that should be valued, and I work to make space for marginalized voices in my personal and professional lives. I believe that consensus, empathetic decision-making and active listening are vital in any process, political or otherwise, and that decisions can't be made in a vacuum or with only one perspective represented. I live my life with the expectation that we are stronger when we remove barriers and work together, and that outcomes driven by that thinking are better for everyone. I would proudly bring that to the commission. 

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a critical thinker who takes time to reflect and analyze before making my opinion known. I try not to be the first person to raise his thoughts in a meeting or discussion, and believe that allows for more insightful decision-making in the long run. I am a problem solver and a person who likes to get things done on deadline and with the highest quality of work.
