Statement

I want to be a part of the commission because I believe this process will serve Colorado well by giving a voice to the citizens instead of the political parties. I feel I will be a good choice since I believe in fairness and creating a balanced legislative body that represents the diverse constituents of Colorado. I believe that a Member of the legislature at the state or national level should have to listen to the views of the entire district. If you have a district with a sense of balance of views, then the legislator will need to moderate their views and be consensus driven to keep their seat. I think this would be a step to moderate and calm the political climate in the state. I feel it is essential for Colorado legislators to be responsive and represent their constituents. Political parties should not control the citizen legislator. While coalitions and caucuses are needed to move legislation, legislators must first listen to constituents. I have been exposed to a wide range of views based on my personal experiences. I have lived around the world and coexisted with people of all cultures. I have worked in both the statehouse here in Colorado and the US Capitol. I have spent most of my life working for constituents in association management and with volunteer leaders. I understand the need of serving the greater good from my 20+ years as an Active Duty Navy spouse and now a Veteran's spouse. I feel I represent the views of women, military members, agriculture, rural citizens, and Native Americans.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am a good listener. I think listening is the most important part of communication. I do not believe in sidebar deals but think the committee should come to consensus. I can be fair and impartial, and I would approach this with an open mind to all views. I am interested in serving on this Commission for the best outcome possible. This only happens every 10 years, and it needs to be right. Colorado must be districted to keep communities together and to award legislators that will represent the varied views of the District. No District should be known as a Democrat or Republican District, but a District of a location of Colorado citizens that need fair and equal representation. I hope this is possible with the Commission that is chosen to complete this important job.
