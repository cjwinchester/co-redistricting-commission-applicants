Statement on Working with Consensus

I am a person who can see all sides of an issue. I once had a s student who had been expelled from numerous schools and he did well in my class. One day he asked me if I knew why he was kicked out of the other schools and I truthfully told him "No, It doesn't matter why you were kicked out- you have a fresh start here, with me." I never had any problems with him and he was a great Biology student. My family and friends range all along the political spectrum! I have taken a workshop on how to communicate with people different viewpoints and I learned several valuable tools from that. I do believe that one of the most important outcomes of the Colorado Redistricting Committee is to guarantee the fair and balanced representation of voters.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a Medical Laboratory Scientist I was able to help develop and streamline new processes for data management. One of my key skills is to be able to see potential problems once familiar with a system. For example: In our Hospital system I suspected that there would be a problem with employee barcodes scanning correctly when we changed to a new middleware and a new type of analyzer. As I worked on the issue it turned out that the barcode scanning issue and programming was huge and overlooked by all other sites. But it wasn't an issue because I had seen this problem and communicated the need for programming to IT just a week before successful implementation. As a scientist I live for data, analysis, logic and reason to solve problems! This fits me to a tee! Give me numbers, graphs, spreadsheets, charts, and maps. The more data you've got the happier I'll be.
