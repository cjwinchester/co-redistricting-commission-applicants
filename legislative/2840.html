Organization and Advocacy Experience

My only experience was during the 2016 election, when I campaigned actively for the proposed Amendment 69 to the Colorado Constitution and served on various committees that planned and coordinated the campaign.

Statement

I have long been opposed to the political gerrymandering of legislative districts. This commission represents all that is right with the process of making reapportionment a non-political process. I have analytical and consensus building skills developed over forty years of legal wrangling and international work and I would like to use those skills in furthering this process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I chaired a committee of people with conflicting ideas to draft the proposed Amendment 69 and was able to bring about a consensus for the final draft. This work took almost a full year. I also worked for then Senator Irene Aguilar and helped draft universal health care legislation beginning in 2013, working closely with attorneys at the Colorado Legislative Council. I also worked with attorneys at the Legislative Council in 2015 and 2016 on similar legislation, and in 2016 on the language of the Amendment 69 initiative and in 2018-2019 on legislation to create a health care cost study commission. I've helped organize and coordinate the unsuccessful campaign for Amendment 69 and have served in leadership positions with two American NGOs working in Kenya and with Rotary International. I've worked internationally with Americans and citizens of foreign countries in a variety of settings that required consensus building and ultimate agreements on projects. I'm good at building consensus and bringing people together in the search for the means to a common goal. Through all this work I've learned that people can legitimately disagree about a great many things even though they have a common goal. I've learned to listen to competing ideas and bring them together to further those goals.
