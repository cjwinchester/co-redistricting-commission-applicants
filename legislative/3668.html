Statement

I am driven by the desire to serve, and I recognize the value of the work that the commission will do. I believe that my professional and personal experience in combination with my education and skills will add unique value to the commission, and it is an experience I will find meaningful. The timing of the commitment to the commission works very well for me at this transitional time in my life.

Statement on Working with Consensus

While studying negotiation and mediation in law school, I practiced open-ended questioning and active listening. These skills are crucial in promoting consensus. Instead of digging my heels into a position and fighting "the other side," I try to understand what my so-called adversary is trying to achieve, and look for opportunities where we agree. From there, we can build creative solutions. This was at the core of the work I did when I mediated disputes in Chicago-area small claims and eviction courts, in which I settled an above-average number of cases. In this work, it was necessary for me to be fair and impartial, and I developed skills and practices to reflect on my impartiality and continually improve. I continue to be aware of unconscious biases and I actively practice identifying them, naming them, and rejecting them.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Everyone analyzes problems and reasons through logical responses multiple times a day. My ability to do so as a higher education professional resulted in me establishing a sterling reputation and being promoted three times, ultimately to a senior supervisor position, in my ten year career. Applied logic and analytical skills are among the most fundamental in legal education and practice, and my law degree and license provide verification of my success in applying logic and reason to problems. Notably, I am exceptional in high-pressure situations. When there is a very clear problem that needs to be addressed quickly, I am able to stay calm by focusing my energy toward innovating a solution.
