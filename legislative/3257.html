Statement

I want to serve on this commission because I believe it is my civic duty and responsibility. I voted for this. I wanted there to be some sort of oversight to avoid the kind of gerrymandering that happens in other states. When I read in the Colorado Sun that the commission I voted for was having difficulty finding people that would represent Colorado's diversity, I felt like it would be hypocritical for me not to apply, as I can make the time available that this very important commission needs.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In conflict resolution training, the key to being successful is understanding what the people think the conflict is. That means my first response to any situation is to listen and ask: Do they have any common ground? If they have common ground, then is there a solution that straddles that? If they don't have common ground, then can I point out some from what they have said? Then, is that common ground enough to build on? Can I help them make it firmer? If I think and feel and examined all the evidence points to the best decision for the most people, then that's the one I will make. Regarding fairness and impartiality: I will in good faith listen to the concerns of others, examine their sources and try to find a solution that is good for as many people as possible. I think that is fair. Impartiality is another matter. I am not impartial. I don't believe anyone is impartial because districting/redistricting affects everyone in the state. But I don't have a conflict of interest and I want solutions to be equitable for everyone involved.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have a Technical Writing certification, so I have an understanding of picking apart a problem and communicating the important parts of it effectively to different audiences. Similarly, my journalism background emphasizes the ability to analyze complex situations and understand which parts are important.
