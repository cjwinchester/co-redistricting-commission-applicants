Statement on Working with Consensus

I primarily use inductive reasoning to reach decisions. I start by identifying measurable concretes then integrate them into generalizations. I try to make judgments based on evidence or concretes, not value judgments on my preconceived notions.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a business owner, I utilize a Cost/Benefit Analysis model every day in multiple scenarios. This requires identification and consideration of multiple inputs, development of strategies or processes that help me calculate correctly, analysis of known data, accumulation of new data, and decision making. Similarly, as an upper-level coach, I have to evaluate a crop of new players every season and analyze their skills, goals, desired outcomes, and personalities and then identify an appropriate placement that takes into account the available teams and clubs and the various aspects of both of those entities. I follow a process, described above of inductive reasoning. When working as a team member, this process involves 1) Defining the problem 2) Agreeing on the definition 3) Looking to the identity of the problem for direction in what types of processes need to be included in searching for a solution 4) Looking at measurables (data, numbers, evidence, etc) 5) Taking measurements where needed, then 6) Plugging the measurables into the process to arrive at the solution
