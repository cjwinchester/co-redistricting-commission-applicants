Statement

When I voted for the Colorado Independent Redistricting Commissions, I said to myself that I wanted to be a part of the process if possible. Voting is fundamental to a functioning democracy and is a right that has been hard fought for many people in this country. As a nation, and as a state, we are in an ongoing process to improve our ability to have free and fair elections. I am proud to live in state like Colorado which has made great strides in that area with tools like same-day voter registration and mail-in balloting. With the new Commissions, we are continuing to improve the value of an individual vote by allowing for process that can potentially reduce the impact of gerrymandering and create voting districts that are not geared to make sure one party always wins. Every politician should earn their election by listening to and representing the people of their district. But if districts are drawn so that one party has an advantage, it can lead to politicians catering solely to their base and ignoring dissenting views. I have seen over the past 20 years a hardening in our political environment with less dialogue and compromise which is not in the best interests of our democracy. I would be proud to be a part of this new initiative to see if we in Colorado can maximize the effectiveness of our political process so each citizen of this state truly feels represented.
