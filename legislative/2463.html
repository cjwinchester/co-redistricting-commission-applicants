Professional Background

I am a founding member of the Pueblo Education Coalition that was created as an advocacy group to represent teachers, students, parents, and community members in public education matters. I have organized city wide events such as the Cinco de Mayo event in Pueblo, school supply drives and give-aways, as well as demonstrations for various local issues.

Statement

I want to serve on this commission because fair representation is the cornerstone of a working democracy. As a member of a disenfranchised minority group, I fully understand the ramifications of not feeling represented. I also realize that representation reflects an understanding of the needs, especially funding needs, of a district.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have served on several committees for my local school district. I often wanted to just push for my agenda based on my role as a teacher; however, I had to be open to all the factors involved such as finances, space, staffing and be able to come to a compromise or consensus with other stakeholders. I believe that listening to other stakeholders and understanding different perspectives in critical in good policy and decision making.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As an elementary school teacher, one doesn't think analytical skills and logic are necessary. However, because each student is different with different needs, a good teacher has to constantly review data and information in order to provide the best education to each individual. The same could be applied to statewide districts and communities. One must be open to analyzing different factors and base decisions on such information.
