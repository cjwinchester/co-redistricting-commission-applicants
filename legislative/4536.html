Statement

Colorado has a rare opportunity to discard the intense partisan gamesmanship familiar to the redistricting process. Living in Colorado for most of my life, I remember being frustrated that unnamed parties seemed to be influencing the final outcome. I was relieved that Amendments Y and Z passed with bipartisan support. As Secretary Buescher indicated, this work is historic. As a Coloradan interested in protecting our democratic form of government, I feel a duty to help.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I'm kind and I enjoy people. I'm open and will be proactive about communicating with the other commission members including the Republicans and Independents. I will strive to ensure every person is heard, and issues are fairly resolved. I can be fair and impartial by focusing on the objective criteria that need to be used such as preserving whole communities of interest, and not drawing districts to favor a candidate or party or dilute the electoral influence of racial or language minority groups.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As an attorney, I've been trained to issue spot, identify relevant rules and facts, apply the law to the facts, and make conclusions. The Commission is faced with several difficult priorities to balance. I believe I can help keep the process fair, be proactive and assertive to solve problems, and to be a good listener that incorporates feedback from all participants.
