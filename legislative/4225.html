Statement

I want to serve on this commission to ensure all Coloradans are accurately and equitably represented by their government. It is paramount to the preservation of democracy and a representative government that citizens engage with their elected officials to safeguard their equal representation. I feel it is my civic duty to apply to serve on a commission which so greatly affects myself and other Coloradans' lives on a local, state, or federal level.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a citizen who supports many but not all platforms held by the Republican party and one who supports many but not all platforms held by the Democratic party, I find myself uniquely qualified to understand the merits and aims of both the major political parties as well as the value of impartiality toward both. I believe the desire to find compromise, even in the most contentious of circumstances is what makes discussing politics worthwhile. So long as all parties feel heard and not belittled, understood and not misconstrued, taken seriously and not dismissed, promoting a well-earned consensus is easily attainable. It may take lots of time and lots of patience, but nothing is so difficult as to be impossible if we remember our intentions are to promote the most amount of good for the most amount of people.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Everyone knows Aristotle's famous quote "the law is reason free from passion" but I think reason free from passion can be applied to data analysis as well. When scrutinizing information relevant to any problem, I use my background in sociological thinking to apply a frame of reference. Often, data can appear abstract when analyzing political or voting preferences. Filtering information into a framework greatly reduces the likelihood of misrepresenting a data set and increasing the likelihood of finding one or multiple solution sets. Additionally, I have been taught to reframe and reframe a problem until the best solution is found. In many cases, a solution may not be perfect, but filtering information through many different "lenses" can be the most efficient way to find the best solution.
