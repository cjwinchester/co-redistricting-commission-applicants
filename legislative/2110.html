Background

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have planned, organized many events, to include, food & clothing drives, health drives, with focus on diseases affecting People of Color, economic empowerment seminars, school safety forums, Veteran Day celebrations, MLK Jr. March, Program & Luncheon, Black History programs, NAACP Freedom Fund Banquet and Voter's Registration. Most recently, I organized and planned a Community Justice Rally, a Conversation with Law Enforcement, completed a School Sanitation Drive, where five of the Title 1, elementary schools were provide hard to get sanitized items. I participated in the District 60 Teacher's Strike and the Pueblo Women's March.

Statement

I want to be apart of creating a nonpartisan, fair, honest system of representation of the people of Colorado; being a lending voice representing Southern Colorado and its diversity; and ensuring the people are put first and not a political party. This is an important work, that will affect Coloradans for a decade, I want to ensure boundaries are correctly made with the people being the priority.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I'm a strong believer in formulating positive relationship through mutual respect and communication. I'm a fact gathering person, and tend to go where the information leads, not afraid of difference of opinions, as long as the end result is realized. As president of the NAACP, there's a process called Legal Redress, where individuals bring complaints/issue to our attention they want to address. Policy dictates, a clear, concise statement of issue is provided, then a discussion takes place with the individual, determination is made based solely on facts presented, which determines if we proceed, refer or end discussion. This fair and impartial process is completed for every complaint brought to the Branch.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

During the recent Black Lives Matter protest, during the Pandemic, I felt a need to bring the Pueblo community together as one. I contacted the Law Enforcement officials, City & County officials, Organizational Leaders, Community Leaders, Young & Old Community Activists and organized a "Community Justice Rally" in coordination with the Juneteenth Celebration. This event brought together hundreds of people in the park to celebrate our likeness, our differences, to talk, to share and to give out information and to understand, as a community, we were in this together. It brought about unity and awareness and was very successful, we followed up with a "Conversation with Law Enforcement" forum, which represented all segments of the community, asking questions of our law enforcement officials, the Police Chief, District Attorney and Sheriff. This event was televised on the government channel several times for public viewing.
