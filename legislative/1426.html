Statement

Redistricting is a vital activity for making sure that all voices will be heard in the electoral process. As Colorado grows, I think I have a unique vision and experiences to share. I have lived in Colorado for over 26 years. I live in a rural area and know the special challenges we face when policies are made centrally. I have a background in human population dynamics after 17 years with a major environmental organization.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a psychologist and consensus builder, I have had a lot of experience in working with diverse individual and groups to come to a common agreement. Working with the State Parole Board, it was often necessary to agree on the release of specific people. We were further trained on the most modern techniques for listening to all sides and impartially making decisions about the information. I have hosted several meetings on civil discourse, including a bipartisan discussion co-hosted by Iliff School of Theology. I believe I can hear all sides and weigh the merits of their position.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have researched and written theses and dissertation on the problems of intersecting our activism with a deep commitment to values. I believe that I use the same skills in building consensus among disparate groups by finding commonalities and values touch points. Group dynamics are part of all the work I have done, especially where there are central decisions that affect local groups and individuals. It begins by outlining the process that will lead to a legal and fair decision. Then backing down to the individual and group interests and listening to their concerns and the challenges they see. Whether deciding the level of assistance to a developing country, creating a matrix for funding projects, working on documents at the international, Federal or State level that represent consensus-building, I believe that I have the necessary skills and experience to add to the commission's mission.
