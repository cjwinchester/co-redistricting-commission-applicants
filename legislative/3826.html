Statement

The reason I most want to serve as a part of the commission is that I am very interested in civics and believe strongly in the democratic process. I also want to be a part of creating a more equitable and representative system of government for the state of Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have worked in education for over 20 years and believe strongly in impartiality. As an educator I have to deal with students and families who come from a very diverse background, where impartiality and fairness is essential to ensure that the educational message is transmitted in its entirety and as equitably as possible. My goal is to ensure all students can learn and succeed regardless of their backgrounds, a philosophy and approach that should translate well to sitting on a commission that has to find common ground between competing interests.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am currently an educational leader within a charter school where I am being asked to repeatedly apply my analytical skills to solve a myriad of problems, usually involving several steps, including: identifying topics, problems or issues and then taking what I identify and addressing any needs that arise. I often have to gather information through testing and observation, and I must develop solutions or contribute to a deepening in the understanding of the topics at hand. My leadership position requires me to find solutions to problems where there are often competing interests, but through logic and weighing multiple options, I can come up with the most sensible solutions.
