Background

Professional Background

Throughout my career I consistently served on boards and committees of trade associations, chambers of commerce and community groups. Frequently, I was selected to serve as chair/president of these organizations. I believe this was because of my ability to come to meetings prepared, see both sides of an issue and find areas of agreement or compromise. Involvement with many of these organizations is detailed in the previous section. In addition, I participated with Building a Better Colorado since it started in 2015. Being a part of this process showed me how to be part of important discussions with individuals holding diverse opinions. At the federal level, I participated in ‘Hike the Hill’ events in Washington, DC for over two dozen years. Credit union advocates would visit Colorado senate and congressional offices to discuss credit union issues with the senators, congressmen and/or their staffs.

Past Political Activity

Ran unsuccessfully for Westminster City Council in 2005 and 2013; have donated over $10,000 to different PACs and political campaigns; most to credit union focused PACs [detail attached].

Statement

To make sure that democracy functions properly requires citizens to be engaged, become educated and actively participate in the process. For me, participation with the Redistricting Commission would be a once in a lifetime opportunity to influence the future of democracy in Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Most of my life I have participated in different organizations and boards. This has provided the chance to work with groups of individuals with differing opinions and values. Words matter. A statement that a metro mayor made a couple of years ago has stuck with me: “Say what you mean, but don’t say it mean.” Consensus is more likely to be reached when discussions are civil. For the past few months, I have been part of a Chamber of Commerce civility council. Its focus is to develop guidelines for civil interactions in the community.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am committed to approach the redistricting process in an unbiased manner, with the objective of finding fair solutions. Being able to understand issues from others’ viewpoints helps to envision the big picture and see consequences, sometimes unintended, of decisions. My mind tends to function logically and I work to solve problems from an analytical perspective.
