Statement

I want to serve on the commission because I believe in representative democracy, unencumbered by private or party interests. I'm an unaffiliated voter because I don't believe a democratic society should be guided by a small handful of loud voices leading parties, but should instead be guided by the real issues facing individuals and their neighbors. It would be an immense honor to be able to contribute a rational, data-driven, and non-partisan voice to the conversation of redistricting to properly represent Coloradoans and to prevent gerrymandering.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Perhaps it is the middle child in me, but I have forever been a consensus-builder and equity advocate. As someone who consistently votes for the best candidate to represent me (regardless of party affiliation), I will very likely have common ground to lean on with all members of this committee. In working towards agreement in challenging problems such as these, I feel like it is more important to promote listening than it is to promote an agenda. I take pride in being able to speak the languages of "liberalism" and "conservatism", with loved ones who identify strongly in either group. It would be a pleasure to be able to do the same for this committee.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

A number of my professional roles (including my current positions) and my academic background have demanded heavy emphasis on analytics and logic. I have expert proficiency at Excel and have intermediate proficiency with GIS systems and using SQL. Effective analysis of data can be incredibly effective tools in promoting fairness and impartiality.
