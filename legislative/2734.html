Background

Past Political Activity

I registered as a Democrat only to vote in that party's primaries (when that was required) and have not un-affiliated myself since that requirement was removed. I registered to vote when I turned 18 in Old Lyme, Connecticut, and have voted in every national election since then and in every election (I think) since moving to Colorado in 1995 (certainly since mail-in voting). I have donated to candidates in CO and elsewhere (Elizabeth Warren, Hillary Clinton) and to organizations such as the SPLC, ACLU, Planned Parenthood. I began direct, frequent political participation beginning with letter-writing for Howard Dean in 2004 (to the best of my recollection); I don't remember anything regular before that.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

South City Park Neighborhood Association and Denver Public Library. I have volunteered at the DPL but do not belong to its Friends organization nor to any other professional organization.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I attend neighborhood meetings to listen to and query folks about zoning and other issues in South City Park in Denver. I do not speak well, being neither rhetorically skilled off the cuff nor eloquent, so I write.

Statement

I believe in the Census, in fair representation, and in civic involvement. I have the privileges of time and education and livelihood. The Census is non-partisan and mathy. I used Census data while at NCSL. I have been interested in the Census since the age of 12, when I listened to my mother place calls as a Census taker in 1980. I have a bone to pick with Elbridge Gerry.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Because I do not speak much, I listen well. By listening well and needing time to formulate a response, I make contributions more measured than those on the fly. I wouldn't call myself the voice of reason, but I do try to be disinterested.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a research analyst at NCSL, I sifted through Census data to represent quantitative information visually. I appreciate the difficult balance between the accuracy of person-by-person counting and the truth of statistical sampling.
