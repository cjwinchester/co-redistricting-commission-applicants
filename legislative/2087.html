Statement

My first experience in drawing district lines was as a staff member for a Democratic congressman from New Jersey after the 1970 Census. I have been fascinated with the process ever since. Since then, I have studied and written about the redistricting process in all 50 states. In retirement, I would like to contribute directly to this new and exciting process in Colorado. I am mindful of the fact that the new constitutional requirement for maximizing political competitiveness adds to the already complex, sometimes conflicting requirements for equal population, compactness, contiguity, the rights of minorities and respecting communities of interest. I welcome the challenge of striking a balance that serves the voters of Colorado and is fair to both parties.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I spent 42 years working for NCSL as a nonpartisan, impartial analyst of public policies and legislative organization and procedure. I was trusted by thousands of Democratic and Republican state legislators alike. In my volunteer work, I have served on numerous Boulder County and state government boards and commissions. In all instances, I was chosen to serve as chair (except when blocked by rule or law) because of my demonstrated fairness and commitment to consensus building. I have trained American legislators and parliamentarians in other countries on effective committee management, emphasizing the value and importance of consensus building, fairness to all sides and respect for the public.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a PhD in political science and in my career as a government practitioner, I am highly trained and experienced in policy analysis. I have co-authored or edited two books on state legislatures, published many articles for refereed journals, and written countless stories for State Legislatures, the magazine of state politics and policy. I have taught policy analysis to legislators, legislative staff and students of politics. I have led consulting teams that have addressed management problems in several state legislatures, using in-depth analysis of problems to develop recommendations for management improvement.
