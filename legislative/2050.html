Application for Legislative Commission for Katherine J Barriga

Contact Information

Full Name

Katherine J Barriga

Other Names
Kathy Barriga, Katherine Jean Barriga

Party Affiliation

Democrat

Occupation

I am currently retired and have been since 2013. From 1997 to 2013 I worked first as a Professional Research Assistant and then as a Senior Professional Research Assistant at the University of Colorado. My primary responsibilities were data management, staff management, and data analyses for the Diabetes Autoimmunity Study in the Young (DAISY). DAISY is a longitudinal study of genetic and environmental factors that may increase or decrease the risk of the development of Type 1 diabetes in children.

Educational Background

I have a Masters of Science in Public Health (MSPH) and a Master's degree in Sport Sciences.

Zip Code

80224

Background

Past Political Activity

I am a regular voter. I always research candidates and ballot issues before voting. I contributed money to Hillary Clinton's primary and general election campaigns in 2016. I also worked as a volunteer on her campaign - making phone calls, canvassing neighborhoods, and helping run a local field office on election day. I contributed money and canvassed for Ashley Wheeland in her bid for a seat in the State House. I contributed money to Kamala Harris' primary campaign and to Joe Biden's general election campaign. I give a small monthly amount to the Colorado Democratic Party.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I am a member of my local YMCA where I volunteer to teach Aqua Fitness classes. The classes are made up mostly of senior women. I have taken an active role in keeping this group in contact with each other via Zoom meetings and emails while access to the Y has been limited due to the pandemic. Following the 2016 election I participated in the Women's March in Denver. Shortly thereafter, I brought together a small group of women for political engagement. We work together to stay informed about issues, write or call our representatives, and participate in voter registration efforts. This group includes women from 20 -73 years of age and about half of us are Latina/Chicana.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I do not have any experience in organizing, representing, advocating for, or considering the interests of groups, organizations, or associations in Colorado.

Statement

I believe in our democracy, I take voting seriously, and I think that it is important for our state to be divided into logical units for representation. These decisions should not be based on trying to give an advantage to a particular political party. I have an interest in seeing that this work is done and I think that I have communication and analytic skills that would make me an asset to this process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I will listen to the other commissioners and I will listen to the public input and make sure that I understand any issues or concerns that they have. I believe the first step is to understand the concerns, then to evaluate how to best address the concerns. I will express my own ideas in a clear, organized, and concise manner. I will promote a systematic and organized approach to evaluating the preliminary maps against the guidelines that we have been given for their formation. I think that my success at staff management speaks to my ability to be fair and impartial. I am by nature a consensus builder.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I spent 20 years involved in epidemiologic research - a career that requires logic and analytic problem solving. I am able to break down complex issues into simpler, more understandable units. I am able to devise impartial rules and algorithms as a problem solving tool.
