Statement

I have long believed that independent redistricting is one of the single most important ways to promote democracy. No politician or political group should have a say in who will vote for them. Growing up in NC I saw some of the worst and egregious cases of political gerrymandering anywhere in the country. This was especially utilized to isolate large demographic blocks like African-Americans and immigrant communities to under-represent their interests in government. This action is both unethical and, in my opinion, counter to the core values of America.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My background within the project management space has given me lots of experience on how to manage both interactions with broad groups of stakeholders. I've had to explain major issues both to clients and also to employees. This has trained me to handle people's expectations and also how to have challenging conversations in an open and honest method. Making sure that multiple people come together in a mutually beneficial way is a major part of meeting project goals successfully. I also feel that my experience in project management has made me be fair or impartial. Many times it was part of my job to implement decisions or processes that I may not have agreed with. But recognizing that everyone has to contribute in a meaningful manner and that individual preferences cannot be the sole driving factor in decisions.

My background in telecommunications means I have experience specifically in optimizing maps. Large portions of my work experience has dealt with creating compact, concise, and efficient maps and also having the technical skill to explain my teams design decisions to non-technical stakeholders. I have an engineering background so have an analytical mindset about breaking problems into smaller components and then tackling each individual component. My current field is in automation of problem solving so it has given me more appreciation for iteration and review processes to make sure designs are accurate and precise.
