Statement

I have long felt that there should be few if any "safe districts", reflecting the fact that I have been registered as an Independent ("unaffiliated" in Colorado) since 2003. I believe that districts should be compact, regular in shape and configuration, and not designed to favor one party or the other. Serving on this commission would give me an opportunity to see if I can carry this preference into actual practice.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Regarding fairness and impartiality, I have extensive experience serving as a court-appointed mediator and arbitrator. Professionally as both a non-profit executive and as a former public official I have put the techniques used as a mediator to use in many different settings. As just one example, in Ohio I chaired a two-year community based risk assessment and strategic planning project by appointment of the Mayor of Columbus, involving 200 volunteers. We agreed at the outset that all decisions would be made by consensus. Despite the wide variety of stakeholders represented in that group, we were able to reach consensus on the identification and ranking of risks, and over 150 recommendations on how that city should address them. I have had little involvement with the Colorado General Assembly, which in this setting I believe would actually contribute to my ability to be fair and impartial.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Whether as a practicing attorney (22 years), a non-profit executive (9 year), or as a public official (7 years), I have had to bring substantial skills to bear in multiple disciplines. Much of this work has involved logic and reason. I learned as a trial attorney and as a mediator, however, that you cannot limit yourself to logic and reason; feelings and emotions must be considered and accommodated as well. The key to a successful outcome, particularly one based more on consensus than on majority rule, it to balance both types of reasoning.
