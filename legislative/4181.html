Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have always believed in giving back to my community and have served on multiple boards and professional associations. In everything I do, I incorporated efforts to ensure equitable and inclusive support of women, girls, and other under-represented populations. I served on the CU Boulder Outreach Awards Committee through 2019: composed of faculty and staff from many disciplines, the committee reviews applications and awards up to $460,000 annually for projects that address things like education disparities, community mental health, and rural economic development. I am a founder of the Rocky Mountain Science & Sustainability Network which strives to develop a diverse population of students into leaders ready to address emerging issues associated with sustainability, biodiversity and community engagement in the protection of natural resources. Each Spring over the last ten years (until COVID), I work with small teams of underrepresented college students in the settings of National Parks or public lands. My pro bono work includes counseling for discrimination/sexual harassment support groups, and representing victims of domestic violence seeking permanent restraining orders. I was an instructor for Emerge; a candidate reviewer for the University of Colorado Presidential Leadership Scholars; and led sessions of Leadership Challenge – mock legislative proceedings for high schoolers. I was very engaged board member of the YMCA of Boulder Valley (2004 – 2013), a mentor for The White House Project (2009 – 2010), and an early board member of Center for Resource Conservation (2005 – 2008), now called Resource Central).

Statement

Recent events following the presidential election have only heightened the importance of constantly safeguarding our democracy. Fairly drawn legislative districts are a foundational piece of that protection. For example, people of color or communities of interest should never be unfairly concentrated or diluted to help a particular party attain power. Fair maps make for a balance of leadership that truly reflects each district. I would be proud to part of that process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a lawyer, I believe it is my duty to follow both the spirit and the letter of the law. I have a reputation of being able to work with diverse groups of stakeholders, to listen and digest their concerns, and respect differing opinions. I know how to keep discussions calm but productive, how to politely ask for clarifications, when to inject humor, when to be quiet and listen.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have been doing this my entire career: through 9 years of practicing law, 8 years in the State House, and advocating for various energy policies. I always want my solutions to be sustainable which means I make deductions based on data and other relevant facts.
