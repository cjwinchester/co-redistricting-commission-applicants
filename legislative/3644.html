Organization and Advocacy Experience

As a CDOT employee I oversaw the removal and replacement of legacy CDOT program and regional software tools with enterprise wide centrally managed solutions. Statement

I believe being counted and represented is foundational for our democratic political system. The practical working of this system can be divisive and messy and requires a lot of effort. Colorado has the opportunity to demonstrate a modern, professional, and, transparent methodology for the decennial redistricting process. I would be honored to participate in the inaugural commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am a lifelong unaffiliated voter.I can seek consensus by acting as a balance between contrasting opinions. Some of the commissioners may not have a cartographic background. I believe my experience with cartography, geographic analysis,, and teaching GIS software provides a good set of skills to communicate why the maps appear as they do. Creating agreement should be easier if we understand what we are looking at. I will listen to and respect a variety of opinions while understanding there may be concealed or overt intention. My fairness and impartiality derive from a sense of duty to the people and State of Colorado.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a trained data analyst and physical geographer. While employed at U.S. BLM NSTC and CDOT DTD I performed geospatial analysis in support of business and policy decision making. I am a 25+ year rock climber. A fundamental aspect of climbing is applying logic and reason to the challenges of route finding and physically ascending a cliff face. There is real and potential mortal danger involved. I am comfortable acquiring information, judging it, and acting upon my decisions while in a stressful situation.
