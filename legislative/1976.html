Application for Legislative Commission for Marianne Z. Wamboldt

Contact Information

Full Name

Marianne Z. Wamboldt

Other Names
Marianne Wamboldt

Party Affiliation

Democrat

Occupation

I am currently a Professor Emerita from the University of Colorado. I was a Professor of Psychiatry at UC, served as the Chair of Child Psychiatry at the University as well as Children's Hospital, and as the head of Pediatric Psychosocial Medicine at National Jewish. I retired in July 2020. I continue to mentor junior colleagues, consult to a federally funded grant that works on improving mental health access for depressed adolescents. I am now ready to serve my community in different ways.

Educational Background

I received a BS, Magna Cum Laude, from Marquette University in 1976. I had been a Philosophy and Biology double major. I then went to University of Wisconsin Madison for my MD degree, and received that in 1981. I did a residency in Psychiatry at UW, then went to the National Institute of Mental Health to learn clinical research. Finally, I came to University of Colorado to get my specialty in Child and Adolescent Psychiatry, which I accomplished in 1990. Since then, I have worked at National Jewish Health, Children's Hospital of Colorado, and the University Hospital until my retirement. In 2014, I received my teacher training in Yoga, as I was using this intervention to help teens with depression and anxiety.

Zip Code

80220

Background

Professional Background

I have said this above. I have not done much with political groups, focussing more of my time on professional groups, for my career.

Past Political Activity

Most of the political donations I have made have gone to Andrew Romanoff. I also donate small amounts to Act Blue to support a number of Democratic candidates, including Biden. I was a founding member of the AACAP PAC, which was the American Academy of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry' s PAC. We donate to bipartisan members of Congress who support issues that will improve mental health in adolescents.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I am the leader of the Colorado Solidarity Sunday group which was founded in 2016. We met monthly until the pandemic, helped support Jason Crow's election, and have focussed this year on getting out the vote. We are loosely connected with Indivisible, lead in our state by Jody Rein. I had been involved with St. Elizabeth Catholic Church, working on the Faith Formation Committee as well as in the Choir, until our pastor was transferred to Regis University. Since then, I attend Catholic Mass there. I mostly was involved with professional organizations, including the American Psychiatric Assn., the Academy of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry, The Society fo Behavioral Genetics, The American Family Therapy Assn. I held offices and chaired committees in these various groups. I was a board member and president of the Family Process Institute (https://www.familyprocess.org) for 10 years and consult to them now. "The Family Process Institute oversees the Family Process journal and creates initiatives that promote family and systems theory, practice, research, and policy, with a commitment to social justice."

Statement

Now that I am retired, I want to focus my skills on improving our state and community for my children and grandchildren, who all live here.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As I trained psychiatrist, trained in the family therapy approach more than a Freudian approach, I have spent my career learning to bring groups together. I have training to help people express their thoughts and feelings in a way that does not attack others, to find common ground, and to make progress toward mutually agreed upon goals.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As an academic researcher, I have been an investigator on numerous grants and research projects. I know statistics, and loved doing my own data analysis. I use a number of statistical programs. As a philosophy major in college, I learned how to think through issues logically. I am also a classical pianist, where I focus on Bach, who is a "logical" composer. This way of thinking comes to me naturally.
