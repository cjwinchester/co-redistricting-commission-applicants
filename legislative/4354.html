Statement

I think it is an exciting process, to put this important work in the hands of the citizens, to create equal districts so that our vote, so precious in a Democratic society can be protected in as nonpartisan way as possible. One person, one vote is critical to people feeling that their vote has power and in a district equitably divided, it gives people reason to think their vote counts.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe in actively listening to people and make sure I understand their concerns with as little bias as I can bring to a situation. It's important not to make snap judgments and to gather all the information possible to make the best decisions possible.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am currently on our Pastor Nominating Committee. We must collect information from each applicant, decide if that applicant is a match to our congregation, come up with relevant questions, and discuss and come to consensus. Similar skills were needed when I was on the Historic Preservation Commission, since we were required to analyze information to see if a particular site met the criteria for a designation. We had certain metrics that we had to apply. When I am mentoring Young Chautauquans, I must teach them in their research to read to answer three guiding questions and use only that information which will shape their presentation most effectively. The questions I ask of them will, hopefully, help them to become the most successful.
