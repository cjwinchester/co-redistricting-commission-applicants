Statement

The Colorado Sun stated in a recent article this commission lacks diversity. Applicants, so far, are primarily white males over the age of 60 which is not representative of Colorado's population. I do add to the count of the white male applicants, but I am also a military veteran and college student that will bring a different perspective to the commission. It is extremely important that the commission reflects Colorado's population, and I can help reflect that with underrepresented groups such as students and military veterans. This is an extremely important decision that will affect colorado for the next decade and we need to get this right.

Statement on Working with Consensus

First and foremost, I believe everyone needs to feel heard. I know talking about political issues can lead to heated disputes. Since the majority of applicants to this commission belong to the older generation, this could lead to many disputes because people could feel they know better or do not want to hear what someone else has to say. Given how important redistricting is, this could be detrimental to the outcome. To avoid this, we need to find common ground. I have been living between two worlds. My entire family is all republicans and I am the lone Democrat, and we all have found common ground in the past. Also, I am about facts and data. I follow the facts regardless of where it may lead.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have experience using Python and R to make sense out of noisy datasets. I have applied data mining, machine learning, and data visualization techniques to various datasets to attempt to understand what the data are telling us.
