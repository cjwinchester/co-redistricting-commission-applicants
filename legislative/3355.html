Statement

In my time working with the CU Alumni board as a chapter president, I've gleaned a great deal of insight into our community and state, but moreover, insight into my goals for civil engagement. My overarching sense is that pouring my energy into football watch parties is not where I am going to make any meaningful difference. I want to contribute my time, skills, and energy into action that makes actual growth in our state and country.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My expertise is working as a couple's therapist. In that work, I spend a great deal of time sitting with people who vehemently disagree, yet share a goal. In this work it is absolutely crucial that I am fair and impartial in order for there to be any success. Seeing different perspectives, encouraging productive and respectful dialogue, and staying focused on a common goal, are skills that I practice every day as a matter of professional integrity and necessity.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My training as a therapist is all about searching below the surface details and looking for the core conflicts/fears/motivating factors. I am keenly observing and tracking those underlying motives at all times. The model of couple's counseling I practice is rooted in a logical and systemic way of seeing any conflict. Being able to zoom-out from the details and conceptualize an overarching theme/model/system is the skill I work at every day. Visual aids such as drawings, as well as metaphors are also ways that I am able to improve my skills and convey my understanding.
