Statement on Working with Consensus

Arriving at consensus is often a difficult task. However if we start from a place of shared norms and common goals these guide the work and facilitate agreement. While communicating, it is important to confirm understanding by restating comments or asking for clarification. Using I statements will keep conversations neutral. Build rapport in working relationships is essential, something like getting a coffee or lunch while at work can be valuable to form relationships. As a scientist, we take many steps to be impartial in conducting and reporting on our research. Removing bias is impossible, but analyzing problems or situations when there is a common goal makes decision making as close to impartial as one can be.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am comfortable using qualitative and quantitative research methods to solve problems. Both sectors of research are rooted in logic and reason, but approach problems with slightly different lens. Both are informative and valuable in sound decision making.
