Statement

I feel that districting procedures in Colorado as in many states has been subjective, based on partisanship rather than objective, based on natural geographic and political (meaning county and municipal lines) boundaries. By serving on this commission, I would like to be a part of a collaborative process that draws lines that will more fairly reflect the will of the people of Colorado in future elections. I believe that "voters should choose politicians rather than politicians choosing voters."

Statement on Working with Consensus

I pride myself on my ability and willingness to listen to others. I try to listen without formulating a response while the other person is speaking and waiting for my chance to respond. I try to be open-minded and not assume that I have "the answer." I am very mathematically-oriented. (My major in college was actuarial science). Consequently, I am partial to data and science rather than intuition and being entrenched in a position. I am also not confrontational, but prefer to be collegial.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As mentioned above, I am math-oriented and have studied actuarial science in college. I like to solve puzzles and have since before I ever attended elementary school. I do solve (usually) approximately 40 puzzles a week including New York Times daily crossword, Sudokus, acrostics, cryptograms, jumbles, and Wall Street Journal crosswords and Weekend addition puzzles. To me, the districting of Colorado represents a complex and challenging puzzle. I enjoy setting up spreadsheets to analyze complex problems.
