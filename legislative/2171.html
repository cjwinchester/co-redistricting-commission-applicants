Professional Background

The best recent example would be the 13 years that I spent as an irrigation canal representative on the Rio Grande Water Conservation District Sub District 1 Board, who was charged with developing a water plan that would reduce pumping and revive the rapidly declining aquifer that is the lifeblood of the San Luis Valley. We had nearly endless meetings and public hearings where we would listen to concerns and suggestions on how to attempt to meet our goal. After developing a compromise plan that was approved by several entities, including the State Engineer and District Water Court, we are currently working towards our goal. With the current drought and increasing efforts from outside our river basin to export water the issue of underground water is still a huge one. The County Planning Commission is also a board that has to deal with sometimes very contentious issues concerning the growth and development of the community. As a commission member I always listened and tried to do what seemed to be best for the entire community. I also served 10 years on our local school board.

Past Political Activity

I have attended Party State Conventions. We held a fundraiser for Senator Gardner both times he ran. To the best of my memory I have donated from $100 to $500 to several local, state, land federal candidates during each election cycle.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Rio Grande County Republicans, Colorado Republican Party, Alamosa Christian Science Society (Church Board Chairman), Rio Grande County Planning and Zoning Commission, Rio Grande Water Conservation/Sub District 1 Board of Directors, and Colorado Farm Bureau State Board of Directors.

Statement

Over the years I have watched and felt the effects of the Legislature and the Courts control the way we deal with growth in Colorado. Recently it seems like changes have been made in boundaries that have made it nearly impossible for a given candidate to win a specific office.

Statement on Working with Consensus

It is my hope that fairness and balance in government is still important to Colorado voters. Previous comments to me on my votes and service on other boards for the most part have been positive and supportive. As I move on from most of these boards I was thanked by others who in some cases may have disagreed with my view on a specific issue.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

When you start from a severe deficit and attempt to develop a plan that protects the economy and improves the eco system you must review a lot of data and form an opinion that you should not be afraid to defend. And when new data supports corrective action you should not be afraid to make changes.
