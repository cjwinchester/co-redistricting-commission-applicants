Statement on Working with Consensus

Consensus is about the second best solution for everyone. That is, everyone gives something and everyone gets something, no single person or party gets everything. This leads to looking further into the future, examining why we did things in the past, and a slew of who has benefited and gained or lost arguments.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a firm believer in finding the facts of a situation, discussing the opinions that surround those facts, and coming to a compromise that benefits both parties. This can only be done through the use of logic and reason, though emotion must be examined for the influences it brings. I served in the military as a Nuclear Electrician's Mate which required details and problem solving to achieve specific goals in groups. You had to come up with clear plans to troubleshoot electrical problems, often non-locally with second parties. You had to communicate clearly or the task would fail. I would bring this same problem solving ethos to the redistricting process.
