Organization and Advocacy Experience

In my role with Clínica Tepeyac, I advocated for healthcare access, especially within Colorado’s Latino/a community. I retired from Clínica in 2017 and became Executive Director of the Chicano Humanities and Arts Council (CHAC). There I ran the art gallery and worked to promote Latino/a artists and provide a community space for the arts in the broader Latino/a community in metro Denver. I am currently serving on the Northglenn Humanities & Foundation and the Chicano Humanities Arts Board of Directors to better connect local artists within both Northglenn and Denver. I also sit on the Northglenn Diversity, Inclusivity & Social Equity Board to advise the city council on matters of diversity, racial equity and social justice issues within the city of Northglenn.

Statement

Living my entire life in the Denver metro area and being heavily involved in the Latino/a community locally, I believe I can bring my own unique life experience and viewpoints to the vital issue of representation of our communities. The commission should strive to reflect the diversity of our state, both ethnically and geographically. Living in Adams County, we are often neglected by more “vote rich” areas so I understand the need to have districts that allow communities to have their needs reflected in their representation

Statement on Working with Consensus

My professional life has been entirely non-partisan, so I do not come at the process from a partisan lense. I am, however, used to working among large groups that often have opposing interests. Both at Clínica Tepeyac and CHAC, I excelled at leading groups of people with different viewpoints to a solution that works for the group as a whole. I do not believe that outcomes need to have winners and losers when there are broad common goals. I believe the commission is similar since we all have an interest in seeing Coloradans are well represented over the next decade.
