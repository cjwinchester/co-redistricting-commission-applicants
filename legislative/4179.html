Statement

This gives some one like me, a “regular person” the opportunity to help create fair maps. In other words, we the people and not your regular politicians get to create maps and chose our representatives.

Statement on Working with Consensus

One of the critical skills one must have as an attorney is to often get to a consensus. Whether that is representing a large corporation or an individual. Often times the majority of case matters get resolved because both sides understand that in order to move forward both sides may have to give up something. I am comfortable working in those situations because I believe that there is always middle ground to be found. Further, I have the privilege of serving as a part time municipal judge for a couple of jurisdictions. I have to be impartial and give both sides an opportunity to present. I am confident that I can do that as a commission member.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a lawyer, I must us logic and reasoning to identify the strengths and weaknesses of alternative solutions, conclusions or approaches to problems. Further, I have to give my full attention to what other people are saying, taking time to understand the points being made, asking questions as appropriate, and not interrupting at inappropriate times. I bring a background of identifying complex problems and reviewing related information to develop and evaluate options and implement solutions. I believe this is what will be required to be successful in this position.
