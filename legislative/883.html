Professional Background

I am a native of Southern Colorado; born and raised in Baca County, high school in Trinidad, worked for a U.S. Congressman and lived in Pueblo, newspaper editor in Lamar and Alamosa, now living in Alamosa. I am very familiar with the demographics of the entire region because of my position with the San Luis Valley Development Resources Group. I most recently organized the San Luis Valley Complete Count Committee to promote participation in the 2020 Census and was a member of the statewide Colorado Complete Count Committee.

Statement

I am particularly concerned about the fair and equitable representation of rural Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Finding common ground is essential to developing districts that share economies, interests, and demographics. Using and agreeing upon factual data is the first step to finding consensus. Most importantly, listen other opinions to identify agreed-upon and shared views, then build from those positions of agreements.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Daily I deal with demographic and economy data. Using those facts is how to build a path for decision-making. Coming from a background in journalism, I am able to understand and analyze issues quickly. I can convey those issues to others in a clear and succinct manner.
