Background

Professional Background

My organizational work in the political sphere was largely based in Illinois in the 1980's and 90's. I lead a team of three to organize of 1000 seat dinner/fundraiser for the Democratic National Comm and multiple other fundraisers for candidates. In Colorado, I have focused more on advocating and working for nonprofits in the areas of climate and the arts.

Past Political Activity

In the 1980's and 1990's I worked in democratic politics in Chicago. I was a media buyer and writer at Axelrod and Associates. I was a contract fundraiser for the Democratic Party. I was a policy analyst on Adlai Stevenson's governor's race in 1986. I have not donated money to a political race since 2015.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I was a neighborhood canvasser for the Boulder Democratic Party in 2008 and a caucus organizer.

Statement

I have a very strong interest in how district maps are configured. Embedded in that interest is a concern for how the boundary lines correlate to gerrymandering, voter suppression and bipartisanship. I want fair and appropriately representative maps that have the interest and vote of every resident of the state top of mind.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My training as a lawyer, mediator and arbitrator have utterly configured how I view an issue. Every side and every voice has a right to be heard and considered. I believe in thoughtful, respectful and empathetic listening. To be empathetic you have to be fair and to be impartial you have to want to hear every story. I am a level-headed person not prone to any rage or easy discountenance. I believe that I have learned through training and practice to identify quickly areas of agreement, areas of disagreement and options for paths forward. This is not a one side take all situation. Rather, this is Colorado's opportunity to show the country that we have the ability to reach consensus on highly contentious issue. Our democracy is predicated on bipartisanship along with an actively involved citizenry. I would like to do more to help maintain and strengthen our democracy.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am not shy about my ability to ground my thinking in logic, reality and fact. I am widely known to be a truly rational person. As a mediator, we are trained to use analysis to reason our way to a viable result. This happens after every side has spoken and every concern is registered. Not every situation or decision will be made based solely on what is logical but every decision should be based on solid judgement that is grounded in solid analysis. My varied work and life experience have honed my problem solving skills to a place where I can be proud of the successes I've achieved in untenable circumstances. I am not narrow minded, I do not want only Democrats to rule the land. I want balanced and appropriate representation and fair district boundaries is how we get there.
