Background

Past Political Activity

I have mostly been politically active in local public school issues. I have run for office (2005) and have volunteered time to knock on doors for a few local candidates running for school board. I don’t, as a rule, donate to political campaigns. I have however donated symbolic amounts (less than $20) to friends who have run for local office. I have been both a registered Republican, registered Democrat, and now a true unaffiliated voter. I appreciate the political process but really don’t agree with either political party as a whole

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I have volunteered my time in public schools for years. I served on SAC committees at Sierra Middle School and Chaparral High School. I have served on the board of the Chaparral Diamond Club - a non-profit baseball booster club.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

Besides serving with the organizations above, since 2015, I have been a advocate for public schools for years. I served as a school board member in Douglas County from 2005-2009.

Statement

I am a policy and process nerd. I love analysis and troubleshooting problems. I moved to Colorado in 1971, at the age of 4, and love my home state. I grew up with one parent living on the front range and the other on the western slope. I have experience with both rural and suburban areas of the state and how their interests differ as well as how they are aligned. I believe my skill-set, neutrality, and my knowledge about many areas of the state would be beneficial for this work. I enjoy meetings and the work of public decision making.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Reaching consensus is a process that happens when people with diverse ideas and options come together to make decisions. It is important, in discussions, to explore differences and find areas of common agreement. My strengths in leadership lies in my ability to listen and understand the different needs of others. I have goodwill towards others and always assume positive intent from others. I strive to make processes equitable and fair. I also believe that checking for understanding and agreement along the way of reaching consensus is important for having an outcome that all participants can feel good about. I have used these skills as a school board member in working with both citizen committees as well as with fellow board members.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have worked analytical jobs most of my adult life. I have strong troubleshooting skills and approach problems systematically. I keep the who and the why in mind when coming up with solutions. It isn’t enough to know a solution, you must know why it’s the right decision and how it will be implemented in practice. I think it’s important to have a process for reaching decisions that involves logical problem solving, self-control, compassion and a strong ability to reason
