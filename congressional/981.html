Professional Background

I donate money regularly, primarily to Democratic candidates. Statement

I am a mediator and a lawyer, but my instincts have always been towards the mediation side. I believe in bringing parties together to talk about issues and find mutually beneficial solutions to difficult problems. Our country has drifted apart lately and I want to help find ways to move us closer together so that we can function as one country and one state. This commission is an opportunity to have substantive discussions with people on the other side of issues. To find our commonalities and to work through our differences so that we can have fair districts for future elections.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I will use my mediation skills to listen to everyone, especially those with views that differ from my own. I will search for issues and concerns that we have in common and use them as the basis for reaching a consensus. I also believe that competitive districts give the voters more choices in candidates and help to move both sides towards the middle, where most America resides. I have the ability and disposition to work with people on the other side of issues. I showed that when I represented EPA employees in the union and actively worked with management to find solutions that worked for both sides. I grew up in rural America, but have spent most of my adult life in urban communities, but I remember my roots and have a deep understanding of the wants and needs of rural communities. I believe that this urban/rural divide drives much of the conflict in our country and that we need to find a way to bridge the understanding gap between these two groups.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a trained attorney and practiced for 30 years. I currently teach Environmental Law at DU as an adjunct professor. The skills I developed in my profession require one to deeply analyze issues and to apply reason and logic. I have always considered myself a problem solver, not a bomb thrower. I welcome creative solutions to complex issues and do not have a desire to win at all costs. I believe that win/win solutions are possible and necessary to move our state foward.
