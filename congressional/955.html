Statement

I am very engaged at the State and County level and have a solid understanding of redistricting and what it means. I believe in a system that creates fair lines for representation and is inclusive of communities and local interests around the state. I also understand that this is a very important piece of our Democracy. I have nearly two decades of experience working with voter files and have a deep understanding of the math that goes into this work. I would like to use my knowledge to help the commission achieve its goals. I believe my experience and macro-level knowledge of our political system, district lines, and representation would make me a great teammate for others on the commission. I believe it is of great importance that we have some folks on this team that understand the consequences of this work and that this is not just a matter of community, but also a complicated math problem with long-term effects on all people in the State of Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The redistricting commission needs to determine new districts for the next decade of representation. It's essential there is an outcome and the commission succeeds in its work. I have political bias like most other Coloradans, but I am a Coloradan first. I believe it would be easy to reach consensus when we stick to the principles that guide us. This is not an opportunity to pursue an agenda, this is a privilege with a lot of responsibility. I work well with others, I seek resolutions to problems, not to make them worse. I believe my leadership style is well aligned with consensus building. If given the opportunity, I'd be happy to further describe my ability to be fair and impartial - I can promise as an applicant that my commitment is to the process and to transparency and fairness.
