Background

Professional Background

I was employed for 11 years by Colorado State University Extension (CSU Extension), the last 9 of which in the role of County Director. CSU Extension operates with federal, state and county funding to identify local educational needs/opportunities. My responsibilities in that position included representing, organizing and advocating for broad-reaching community education programs . Community needs were assessed and identified by local Advisory Boards. As County Director, I coordinated with Advisory Boards to engage local resources in supporting the programs offered by CSU Extension. These programs included 4-H Youth Development, Colorado Master Gardener, Colorado Building Farmers program, SNAP education, etc. Many CSU Extension programs are volunteer based. I lead efforts in volunteer recruitment, training and program evaluation. Critical to program success was engaging community members such as non-profit organizations, schools, parents, business owners, city, county and state agencies and citizen volunteers. I have experience engaging these groups , presenting to community groups, and gathering their input.

Past Political Activity

No donations that I can recall to political candidates or organizations. I have signed some petitions and infrequently emailed Senators regarding issues of concern to me.

Statement

I feel that the work this committee will do is very relevant and significantly important for years to come. I voted in favor of utilizing the citizen based process to redistrict. As a retired person, I have the time, energy and interest to support this process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

as a CSU Extension employee, I received training in communication skills, listening skills, building consensus and diversity awareness. I have experience working with community groups to build consensus. As a state employee, it was my responsibility to have communities determine their own needs, not to impose needs from state or federal agencies. To this end, it is critical that representation from all relevant sectors of the community be at the table, with all voices being heard and given consideration. Quieter voices sometimes need elevation, while louder voices sometimes need moderation to bring balance to a discussion.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My academic training in science focused on fact based information gathering and the scientific method. As part of this methodology, situations are viewed from several perspectives, considering many possibilities before reaching a conclusion. Professional development at CSU Extension included personality assessments to determine our strengths. On these assessments I scored highest in traits relating to logic, reason and analytical skills. Colleagues describe me as a creative, resourceful person. These traits help me to see things others sometimes do not.
