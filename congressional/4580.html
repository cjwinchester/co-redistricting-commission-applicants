Statement

I understand that representation needs to be divided equally on the basis of population, but I also believe it is vitally important to maintain the representation of various interest groups, whether political, geographical, economic or racial. Representing all of these competing interests poses a fascinating, yet vitally important challenge. We must insure that all voices are heard to the greatest extent possible.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The first step is to develop common agreement about the goal of redistricting. To me, the goal should be community representation. I have lived in a district that was divided right down the middle, separating common interests based solely on demographics. We have to try maintain community integrity, so that local interests, as determined a majority of the voting public, are maintained. Nonetheless, if a single redistricting objective cannot be agreed upon, then a set of objectives can be established and used to evaluate each districting decision. I would hope that an objective process could be agreed on that would not require compromising community integrity to satisfy partisan interests. I wold work toward maximizing the value of each individual vote, and that starts on the local level.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I see the process as over-laying a number of different maps based on community interests as developed by relevant social, geographic, demographic, economic, and racial interests. Community interest is the most important. How can we promote the political decisions of a community to the greatest extent.
