Statement

As Americans' political views have become increasingly polarized, I have worked to avoid "echo chambers." I'm a registered Democrat but seek news daily that represents a broad range of voices and opinions. I was SO EXCITED when Colorado voted to take away politicians' power to draw their own election maps and put that power in the hands of voters by creating a fair, impartial, and transparent redistricting process. As the 2020 election is proving, there is NOTHING more important than ensuring voters' confidence in elections and in having a process we can trust as fair. With the new "one person, one vote" legislation pending, our redistricting lines become even more important. I would like to be part of the process to eliminate gerrymandering and improve voter confidence.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I’ve spent my professional career building expertise as a communicator, listener and consensus builder. In the corporate world, those skills have helped prioritize multi-million dollar budgets and resource allocation. In the non-profit realm, those skills have been critical for community building and fundraising. I’d like to put some of my hard-won skills to work for something that matters as much as the redistricting commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a marketing professional, my livelihood depends on my ability to review data, build models, forecast trends and recommend actions unemotionally, based on whatever the data tells us, no matter how unwelcome the information. My friends and co-workers call me a "spreadsheet ninja." One of my favorite phrases is "data doesn't care." I believe you have to follow where it leads.
