Organization and Advocacy Experience

As an education and non-profit professional, a large part of my work is advocacy and organization of stakeholder groups. In Colorado my most public engagement in such work was staffing the State Council for Educator Effectiveness (SCEE) from 2010-2011. The governor established the SCEE to deliver recommendations for a statewide educator evaluation system. The legislation was highly contested and the members of the SCEE represented stakeholder groups that were deeply divided in their opinions. In my role I was charged with getting to know the values of the different members; design a process for dialogue and debate informed by experts, data and research, but which also enabled the group to identify and align around shared values. This meant organizing and managing various working groups through which we were able to get the input of community members and advocates for different perspectives. I am proud of the fact that the SCEE provided unanimous recommendations that were recognized nationally for both their content as well as the process that allowed them to emerge. As a trained attorney who has done work in negotiation and mediation, I am committed to the principle that it is usually possible to expand the sphere of shared values and design outcomes that maximize them, even when we also have deeply held personal convictions.

Statement

I was very pleased when Colorado approved independent redistricting commissions. I believe a functioning democracy requires a system that meaningfully allows voters to express their desires, and does so in a way that promotes community engagement, debate, the free flow of ideas and the capacity to hold elected officials accountable. This applies to all elected officials. Election cycles over the last two decades have demonstrated what happens when these elements of a democratic system are not functioning properly. Fewer elected officials are in truly competitive races; the cost of campaigns increases; the barriers for new voices and candidates to enter become greater; transparency, accountability and engagement decrease. None of these are healthy. I more closely identify with the overarching values of the Democratic party at this moment in time. However, as a citizen, a mother and a professional I am even more committed to ensuring that we have a democratic republican system that is functional. I believe re-districting is a critical part of making that happen and will require all of us to put principles and values above political affiliation and loyalty.
