Statement on Working with Consensus

Consensus is sometimes a complex task, but it is never impossible. In the beginning of this process it is essential that there are shared norms of conduct among the committee and a shared common goal. These two things function as the common ground the committee returns to when there is disagreement, frames our collaborative work, and if need be, holds people accountable for inappropriate actions. Additionally, remaining impartial can also be embedded in the work of the redistricting committee. As a scientist I am constantly asked to examine situations fairly and impartially to make good decisions. Although no one can escape their won bias, I do believe that if we evaluate a situation with equanimity and within the context of shared norms of impartiality, the chances of the bias affecting decisions are minimal.
