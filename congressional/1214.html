While I continue to hold out for finishing my university degree one day, over the course of my life I have educated myself, being well read and well mentored. Statement

I want to serve on the commission for multiple reasons. First, I was asked to apply by someone I respect and admire as a leader in the city. I want to honor that request, and their recognition of me in the community, as a trusted person. Also, I love Colorado. This is my home, where I was born, and raised and have worked and built my life. I have benefited from this state and have on purpose made this my home. In different professional roles I have had the opportunity to see the majority of the state with my own heart and eyes. As a child, being a native of Colorado, I traveled and stayed with family in different parts in most of Southern Colorado and along what is now known as the Front Range. I feel connected to rural and frontier Colorado as well as urban centers because of my time in and connections with colleagues, friends, and family who span all of Colorado. I come from a multi-racial, economically and politically diverse family. This helps me to aim for options that serve the well-being of all of us. I also know what it is like to move through social tiers and change one’s life trajectory through hard work and participation in the system. I am a testament to that. Finally, having built my career and gained professional respect through what some would call, my own gumption, it is also through the network of generous mentors and knowledgeable guides. Many people of many walks have invested in my success. It is my purpose and choice to continue to invest in our collective good and my beloved Colorful Colorado. This commission has a worthy purpose, and I believe I will be able to contribute meaningfully to the commission’s work.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Differing perspectives are important and valuable in building good solutions, so disagreement is not a problem, but rather an indication that indeed people are putting forward their contribution. Moving forward once many views have been expressed is then the work of respectful dialogue, listening and considering as well as speaking. When others at the table feel heard and respected, we can talk about solutions or compromises without any personal disrespect. Keeping our eyes on the values and the goals that the commission is tasked with can help in seeing that we are all working on behalf of something none of us can achieve individually. Fairness and impartiality in terms of respectful inclusion, while being values-led, is a cornerstone of much of the work that I do in my professional life.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My natural perspective tends to be strategy and reason. As a person who has had a life full of diversity of social conditions and variety of people and locations, I find that my logical mind is well-balanced with the human relationships that surround me in life. In my work I have worked on research teams as support and as co-investigator. I have the ability to connect, value and evaluate the data that research provides. DAta helps to shape policy. In my work, I have also provided connection and support for many victims of violence, having to provide a compassionate connection and support people in learning about and maneuvering with some sense of capacity, the various systems with which a victim of crime can come into contact. I have developed over the years a fair balance of logic and reason with humanity and fairness. Data cannot live in a vacuum, it must be applied to the society in which we all live. Anecdote alone cannot be that which creates guidelines and policy, and it has a place to inform the rules of engagement.
