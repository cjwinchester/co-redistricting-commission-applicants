Statement

I want to see a united Colorado that residents from all political backgrounds can believe in and support, regardless of party, race, gender, social status, or any other differences we may have. I hope to ensure the commission is transparent and answerable to all of our voters - if they do not understand our charter and decisions, they will not trust or respect them. It is the commission's duty to EARN the trust of the Coloradans that we serve. I believe I can bring patience and unity to the commission and will help bring a unifying message to all of Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have dedicated my life to developing leaders of character and integrity - first in the military to include time as a USAFA professor and now in the corporate world. I recognized the difference between management and leadership and the vital role each plays in shepherding the process we've been tasked with and making sound decisions. Management is about dealing with the complexity the commission will face in planning and problem solving. Good management involves knowing and valuing the unique abilities and even the eccentricities of the commission members and how best to integrate them into a coordinated plan. Leadership is about producing useful change and obtaining buy-in - Aligning people with a vision through communication. Commission leadership is about discovering what is universal and capitalize on it but focusing on what unites us and the values we all share. We will need integrity and both solid management and leadership in the commission. One of my favorite quotes is from Dr. Peter Pronovost - “Things done to others, rather than with them, are highly resisted, they are usually never implemented locally and, if they do, they rarely work.” I intend to work WITH my fellow commissioners to come up with a plan that works for everyone.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a former director of the Modeling and Simulation Cell at the USAFA, I am fluent in statistical analysis methods and terminology. In a former role a software product manager, I led the development of a performance analysis tool that leveraged data collection from front-line workers to provide intuitive insight into compliance and performance for managers using Tableau analytics. My consultancy often uses advances analytics and surveys in the field to tell "data stories" regarding organizational performance and culture. While I believe in the importance of data, I know that we must collect data with a purpose and that it must be actionable. The data will tell a story, but that story must be confirmed in the field through engagement with our citizens to be valid.
