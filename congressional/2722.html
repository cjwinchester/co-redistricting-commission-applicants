Statement

I believe that Gerrymandering is one of the greatest threats to our democracy and has stifled the voices of those who are most affected by poor districting. I'm not affiliated with any political party and am willing to work cooperatively with anyone as long as it's in the name of fair and equitable practices in our governmental system.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I was born in southeastern Colorado, lived most of my life in Greeley, work in Fort Morgan, but have also spent a significant amount of time in the Metro area as well. I have experienced many of Colorado's demographics first-hand and seen their concerns, commonalities, and their differences as well. I believe I have a deep understanding because of a deep interaction with a large cross-section of our community from farmers and rural workers to the underprivileged and the at-risk. I believe my knowledge of history comes with it approaches and strategies that will help build consensus and my work in history demonstrates an ability to look at facts objectively, rather than subjectively.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a historian, statistics and demographic data are things I have interacted with and applied in my work, particularly in my graduate school papers. But I also understand there is a qualitative element to problems as well. Numbers tell us a large part of the story but especially when it comes to creating districts that serve a real cohesive population of the state, there is a qualitative element as well. Which communities feel tied to eachother, which ones seem further apart. These are all notions that need to be factored into the discussion. However, I do also believe in the power of numbers and want to draw numbers that allow voices to be heard proportionally and not get watered down in strange district lines.
