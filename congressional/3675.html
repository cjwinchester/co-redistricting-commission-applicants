Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have spent 30 plus years working in the human services field. During this time, I have spent many hours participating in meetings at the state and federal level with Senators and Representatives to both educate and advocate for issues pertaining to the behavioral health field. I annually attend Hill Day in Washington D.C. , arranged by The National Council to meet with members of the Legislative Branch. I also attend the Colorado Behavioral Health Care Council's annual Day at the Capital to meet with Senators and Representatives who serve The Center for Mental Health's service region. I regularly attend or listen to the Colorado Joint Budget Committee hearings to fully understand the challenges with financing. I have just completed a three-year term as the President of The Colorado Behavioral Healthcare Council. Part of my responsibilities included meeting monthly with members of the Governor's Cabinet and to work collaboratively to weave together solutions that benefited both the provider community and the State. I worked to build consensus within the different membership categories to ensure all views were being adequately represented in legislative efforts. As the CEO for The Center for Mental Health, I'm responsible for the oversight of behavioral health services in Gunnison, Hinsdale, Delta, Montrose, Ouray and San Miguel counties. In this role, I work daily with a variety of public servants, elected officials, and private sector leaders. This six-county region is very diverse politically and economically. I often find myself in a convening and organizing role.

Statement

I have lived in both the Front Range area and now in the rural Western Slope of Colorado. The needs and issues from these two distinct regions are very different. I want to serve on this commission to ensure the needs of all Coloradans are effectively represented in our voting districts.

Statement on Working with Consensus

While I am registered as a Democrat, I do not vote exclusively any one ticket. I feel Colorado is extremely diverse and think our voting districts need to represent that uniqueness. With the growth occurring in more metropolitan areas, I think it is important to represent the needs of rural ranchers, farmers, miner, and others living in smaller communities. As the CEO of a company, I am tested every day to be impartial and ensure decisions take into account all sides of an issue. Throughout my lifespan, I have been in many leadership positions (paid and volunteer) that require consensus building. This is an area of strength for me and I've received accolades through the year for bringing about win-win situations.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I tend to make data-driven decisions after weighing all of the potential gains and risks of a situation. I'm required to analyze complex contracts as part of my job. When unsure, I regularly seek legal and other consultation.
