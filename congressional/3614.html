Statement

I want to serve on the commission because I believe that it is an important civil duty for people with strong backgrounds in mathematics and data to serve their community. It would be ideal if we could leverage data and computing to ensure that all Coloradans have representation, not just those affiliated with the current dominant party. While I am not an expert on the necessary mathematics or computations to accomplish this goal (I don't even know if it is a pragmatic goal), I feel that my experience could help guide the process towards investigating and hopefully implementing such tools.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe that in order to promote consensus and create agreement, we have to agree on our goals at the beginning of the process as well as a set of axioms upon which to operate. Too often in American political discourse this step is skipped, and we look past where we agree straight to where we disagree. Instead, if we focus on where we do agree, and build from there, we could have more success. My efforts to promote consensus and contribute to creating agreement will follow this workflow: building from where we agree. For example, I believe we can agree that all Americans should have representation and that even though we disagree, that we are trying to what is best for our country.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My job is applying analytical skills, logic, and reason to difficult problems in renewable energy research. Specifically, I develop algorithms and write code for high-performance computers to model power grid operations with high penetrations of renewable energy. This involves advanced probability, optimization, programming, and uncertainty quantification. I believe that the probability and statistics knowledge I use on a daily basis, along with my analytical and logical skills in general, could be useful solving redistricting problems.
