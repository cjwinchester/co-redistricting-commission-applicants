Background

Professional Background

As a teacher of teenagers, I organize and consider the interests of various groups on a regular basis. Teaching, especially currently, involves multiple stakeholders with a variety of priorities. Finding the right path that meets the needs of the variety of families involved takes patience, communication skills, creative problem solving and planning. I am not afraid to speak up to help to make sure the best solution is found.

Past Political Activity

I am politically active in that I keep myself and my neighbors politically informed. As a secondary social studies teacher, I also keep my students informed of current and historical political trends. I have not belonged to any political organizations since college in the late 1990s.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Since I have two sons, I have been active in the local scouting community. I was Treasurer for Pack 548 from 2015-2017, and during this time we increased our membership from 12 scouts in grades 1-5 to over 30. Other community organizations that I have been involved in in the last five years have been my local PTA, and Jeffco Eats, an organization that helps with food insecurity among school age children. I also have taken part in the annual Point in Time count of the homeless population for the last several years. I have been active at meetings for these groups but have not held a leadership position in them.

Statement

Primarily, I want to serve on the commission because of my deep felt belief in "one man, one vote". I have taught citizenship to middle schoolers and high schoolers who a required to take it as well as adults who are voluntarily taking their US citizenship test. I am proud to be a part of this process. I am a first generation American, and my immigrant mother instilled in me her belief of the privilege of voting and the responsibilities that come with it. I agree with Winston Churchill when he said, "Democracy is the worst form of government, except for all the others." It may be messy, but the voting process in the US is the best way to balance all the competing ideas and interests while still maintaining both freedom and order.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I teach history, politics and government during a time when people are afraid to discuss these topics with their friends, much less strangers. I pride myself on the fact that my students regularly ask me whether I am conservative or liberal because they can not figure it out based on my comments in class. Just as I create an environment of fair and moderated discussion in a classroom, I believe I would be able to do the same on the commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a person, I am very logical and analytical. When I give my students an assessment, I also assess myself as a teacher. Using the data of both the content of the questions missed as well as the type of question missed, I can assess whether it was a topic that I need to cover more versus a type of question that the students struggled with. In addition to gathering data after the fact, I use pre-tests to help guide curriculum choices. Collecting, collating, and analyzing data is a large part of the educational environment today, and I use those skills regularly.
