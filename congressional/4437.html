Statement

As a bus operator I became acutely aware of the need for advocates for those who have the least in this world. Having been previously homeless due to a workplace injury which kept getting aggravated and re-injured, I couldn't keep a job. Having entered into an unemployment office to gain assistance, I was met with such distinct and unequivocal derision from the very beginning, I left and faced being homeless rather than be humiliated repeatedly. I think I understand well those who are disenfranchised and under represented.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The wisest man I ever met said there are four things all living beings have in common: we eat, we sleep, we poop and we argue. My mother, ranking right up there with some of the wisest people I've ever met, asked me once as a child, "What do you think the difference is between kids and adults?" I sat with it and could not come up with an answer. After a moment of consideration, I said I didn't know. She said, "Adults have bigger bodies so we make bigger messes." These two things have leveled the playing field in all directions regardless of affiliations, loyalties, facades, or ulterior motives. I find the best thing I can do when a group is getting distracted by rabbits running down holes and building castles in the sky is to remind folks that the most chic, most sophisticated, most enduring of legacies regardless of arena are always simple. We're all in this together standing on the shoulders of giants, but it only takes one person to say no. Provided willful ignorance is absent, direct, sincere consideration can bring consensus.
