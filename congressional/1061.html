Statement

Back in 2018 I did in-fact vote to pass both Amendments Y and Z because I liked the positive change that could come from them. As an unaffiliated voter, I feel I would be able to look at what is best for our communities as a whole, as opposed to looking at what would benefit a specific political party. I love Colorado, and definitely love the community in which I live and since both Amendments passed with such a large majority, it means I would be helping enact change that Colorado truly wants. I would be honored to be able to serve on a commission that I voted to create.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The commission was designed to be a group of people, not one individual or one individual's opinion. In my career, and in life in general, I have found that multiple minds and perspectives are far better at solving problems than one single mind. A group also helps keep each other accountable. I feel the best way to create agreement and promote a fair consensus is to first agree upon a set of standards and guidelines (be it geographical, socio economic, an areas specific needs, or by a political majority of an area) and then stick to it, whatever guidelines are agreed upon, we should then prioritize them before starting. I would take into account how the majority of a district votes, and make sure gerrymandering isn't taking place. As for being fair and impartial, I am not affiliated with either party. I would always want to keep each communities voices as a top priority, not the benefit of a specific party. I understand Colorado is an extremely diverse state and I would definitely want to respect the best interest of each community and their specific needs. I am a fair citizen, and tend to fall back on Stephen Coveys book "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People". Habit 1: Be Proactive Habit 2: Begin With the End in Mind Habit 3: Put First Things First Habit 4: Think Win-Win Habit 5: Seek First to Understand, Then to Be Understood Habit 6: Synergize Habit 7: Sharpen the Saw This would be my exact approach to a position on this commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

This required critical thinking, team work, analysis of the information we were getting, in depth research, clear communication and constant feedback from our doctors, and of course problem solving. The outcome was a successful Cardiac PET-CT program, one of the only two in the state, that quickly and accurately diagnoses coronary artery disease for southern Colorado. I use these methods to approach and solve any problems that need my attention.
