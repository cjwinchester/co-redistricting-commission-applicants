Background

Past Political Activity

I have made donations to local candidates in the democratic party (I truly could not recall all of them), and I have contributed to the Biden campaign and to Diane Degette's campaign.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I am on the Colorado Supreme Court's Law Committee (Bar Examiners) since 2016; I was formerly on the Federal Magistrate Selection Committee from 2013-2016; and am a candidate for the Faculty of Federal Advocates. My resume has a more complete list of these activities.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have attended fundraisers for candidate for Denver Mayor (Lisa Calderon), and my local representatives (Leslie Harod, and City Counsel member Candi CdeBaca). I have volunteered with the Colorado Spanish Speaking Lawyers committee to help folks with access to justice.

Statement

We need folks who can understand the rural/urban divide. My folks live in the San Luis Valley; they ranch there and have been active community members with regard to water rights and land rights issues. I believe strongly that there are voices in rural areas that are not heard; it is time to change that. I also live in Denver; I understand and try to challenge "gente"-fication (that is, displacement of black and brown people from their own neighborhoods by upwardly mobile black and brown folks - it is not common but class is also changing our populations). I think redistricting should be about fairness in our changing communities, regardless if the community is rural or urban. We also have a huge problem with underrepresentation of black and brown people on these types of committees - we bring our perspectives, we bring our experiences, and we have something to offer here to make the process more equal.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My job is centered entirely around ordinary people - I am a plaintiff's trial lawyer. I talk to clients all day who have never had to hire a lawyer until they were injured. I like to hear what people have to say; what they have to offer; and, overall, I like to resolve conflicts. I litigate and try a lot of cases; this is a scary thing for clients, and I believe strongly in doing everything I can to resolve disputes outside of our courts. I mediate cases, I call other lawyers and we have to try to resolve cases on our own sometimes; I deal with adjusters as well who will try to resolve cases. My job is to create consensus and the best result for my client - which sometimes includes a bit of disappointment for all involved. I have applied in the past to be a judge in the 12th Judicial District in Southern Colorado. I have clerked for a judge (Justice Mullarkey of the Colo. Supreme Court). I believe in and advocate for fair outcomes in our judicial systems, and I'd like to see that put to work in a political system that is in-fighting and tearing each other down. I am 40 years old, I have a young child who is 6, and I am married to an immigrant of a different faith. I resolve disputes and conflict at home as well! I think we could use more working parents on these types of committees - there is nothing like arguing with a 6 year old about bed time that can prepare you for deeply angry political conflict. I have learned more about conflict management since having a child, and I think it has made me more empathetic and willing to hear someone out completely to try to see our common ground. I'd like to be part of this mission to promote consensus on this very delicate issue.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a lawyer all I do is pull the emotion out of everything I touch - it is like an inverted Midas touch. To make logical and reasoned arguments, the passion and emotion is drained, but it is nonetheless important to understand someone's perspective - facts are not without agendas. Persuasiveness is not without its rhetoric. I like to think I know how to do both as I try cases before juries and part of my work is to use both facts and emotion to convey meaning - yet remain professional.
