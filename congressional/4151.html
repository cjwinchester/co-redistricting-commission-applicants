Organization and Advocacy Experience

A small group of us based in the North Fork Valley has organized a loose group who has met weekly, occasionally working in conjunction with the Dems for the past 2 years helping to organize and galvanize support for Democratic candidates. For instance, we were donated an RV which we took around to Paonia, Cedaredge, Hotchkiss and Delta, handing out yard signs and registering voters. We do not have a name, but we are very politically active in our communities. More even than the local Dems. I have also volunteered for D3 Colorado, which was formed in 2017 by locals around Ridgway, CO.

Statement

I believe my voice would be a benefit to the process, given that I am politically active and live in rural Western Colorado. In my 20+ years living in CO I have become very proud of the state. I feel I represent it's centrist middle, liberal but pragmatic. My neighbors are ranchers, miners, hippies, musicians, all types of people. I feel very in tune with our part of the state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In my time in Western CO I have learned that everyone wants the same things... safety for their families, prosperity and their voices to be heard. We differ on so much (abortion views, gun laws, etc) but we can always find common ground. Although I lean toward the Dems, I have plenty to say about how they could approach our communities differently. A few years ago I supported a centrist Republican. I feel I am a solid centrist in how I approach politics.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a good listener. I am not afraid to make suggestions. Using reason to solve problems is paramount to successful outcomes. I tend to remain unattached to most things, rarely holding any sort of agenda, other than moving toward equality and fairness.
