Statement

I want to serve on the commission in order to work for my community with a fair and balanced approach to redistricting. As a leader in our country on redistricting issues, Colorado - and its citizens - have the rare opportunity to show that this new way of redistricting can work for everyone. I am particularly proud of and hopeful about the verbiage included in our new laws that add language from the federal Voting Rights Act into the Colorado Constitution. I am equally proud to have letters of recommendation from two elected officials with whom I have worked: one a Democrat and one a Republican. I believe this supports my commitment to a bipartisan approach to serving on the Commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The various personality and strengths/skills tests I have taken over the course of my career have validated what I have always been told by others: I am a consensus builder, a mediator and a connector by nature. Listening well, respecting others' opinions and serving as "the reminder of the higher purpose of the committee" are things I will commit to. As a political science major and daughter of a U.S. Foreign Service officer, I am committed to our democracy and the hard work it takes to preserve it. With that knowledge and experience in mind, fairness will be my highest priority.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have been called an exceptional problem solver, organizer and consensus builder. I believe that thorough examination of the information available, a willingness to ask questions and listen to those who do not agree with you, and then a focus on how the solution will affect others/be applied in reality, are all important parts of solving society's big problems. I believe I have the skills and respect for logic and reason that will help me be a focused and productive commissioner.
