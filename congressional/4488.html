Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe that consensus is built through a number of key strategies - all of which start with listening, validation and respect. My personal style is non-confrontational and supportive and I utilize evidenced based methods for bringing viewpoints to the table including models such as Motivational Interviewing. However, building consensus and a plan goes well beyond the starting point of listening - it has to move to action and the building a cohesive plan or output. To do this, I focus on both the ground rules for moving forward and the tactics for bringing ideas to the table. The ground rules determine how far we are willing to go as a group with each decision or step in the process. I utilize a scaling or rating system where each participate can identify where they are in their support (or disagreement) with a step and we determine ahead of time and what levels or scales of consensus we are willing to move forward. For the tactics of actually developing consensus, I most often utilize visual mapping where participants put ideas on small pieces of paper and we start to sort and prioritize them together - without attribution. This allows groups to see first the areas where we agree - both through the topics and the volume of pieces of paper. By owning and starting from points of agreement, we can then work outwards and draw ourselves wider and deeper. I view consensus building as trying to move hundreds of marbles across a room - as you move them forward, some will skirt off to the side (or ahead or backward) and you have to stop and nudge them back to the center before you can move the entire group of marbles forward. It is an iterative process that I love to lead and be a part of.
