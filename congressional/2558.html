Statement on Working with Consensus

I have worked most of my career in either IT or software development for Colorado companies, large and small. I have managed hundreds of people in my roles within these organizations. My employees counted on me to represent their interests to upper management. Conversely, I needed employees to understand the goals of the company. I always work from the position of being authentic. Anything less wouldn't be fair to either side. Additionally, it's important to run and participate in meetings with specific goals in mind. If those goals are not understood by the team, you can't reach a consensus. Everyone's voices should be heard. My experience in meeting management and team management could be valuable to help reach the objectives of the commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As noted in the previous question, I have spent my career in IT and software development. For me, the best way to diffuse disagreements and resolve issues is to remove the emotions and focus on the problem and how to solve it. There are always compromises required. For example, in software development, at times sales people would make promises to customers in order to close a deal. However, when those requirements would reach the development team, I would often be confronted with the need to balance the customer's expectations with reality. A customer may have expected a certain feature to be delivered in 3 months but reality was closer to 5 months. Could I deliver enough of the feature in 3 months and use other workarounds to achieve the same results? Those decisions required a great deal of logic and reason to determine the best course of action and keep a happy customer.
