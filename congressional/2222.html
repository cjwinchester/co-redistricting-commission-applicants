Organization and Advocacy Experience

I volunteer with The GrowHaus, a local food justice organization that supports the Globeville and Elyrea-Swansea neighborhoods of Denver to ensure healthy food access for all. I have marched for women's rights and in support of the Movement for Black Lives here in Colorado. I also work for ActionAid USA, an international human rights organization dedicated to building a just, equitable, and sustainable world, in solidarity with communities on the frontlines of poverty and injustice. I am currently leading my organization in undergoing antiracism training through a consulting group who specializes in this work.

Statement

I believe in human rights for all. Every person deserves the right to vote in a way that enables their vote to count as fully as their neighbor's. Fair maps are important to enabling communities to elect representatives of their choice and to have equal access to political representation. I would be an effective member of the commission due to my deep knowledge of food systems (including urban development as it relates to food justice), my years of experience fighting for human rights through international development, my time in New York working for the social service agency Henry Street Settlement, and my passion for a fair political system that represents all people. The state of Colorado is a beautiful, diverse place, and I want to do my part to ensure that each person has their voice heard in the most equitable way.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have dedicated my career to fundraising for issues that are important to me. As a fundraiser I have the ability to relate to wealthy individuals aiming to make meaningful philanthropic contributions, and the individuals they wish to support, who are often the most vulnerable and marginalized members of society. Fundraising is all about bringing together one group's needs with another group's philanthropic goals, so compromise and consensus is part of everything I do. I pride myself in being a former Girl Scout and Model UN participant and leader. Both of these groups taught me how to build consensus with those with whom you may not automatically agree. I am a Jewish woman, and as such, I have a deep understanding of how inequity can impact individuals, communities, and entire groups of people. I will bring this insight to my work. Additionally, as a new mother, I strive every day to show my baby kindness and fairness, and I will bring this mentality into my work on the commission, as I aim to make him proud.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

In my daily work, I manage a team of five, sit on a Senior Management Team, write complex budgets and grant proposals, manage yearly projections for a small and scrappy non-profit organization, work with international colleagues from over 50 countries, and negotiate multi-million dollar grants with donors. I constantly use analytical skills, logic, and consensus-building to accomplish all of this and succeed in my career.
