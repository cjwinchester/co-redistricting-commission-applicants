Statement

I want to serve Colorado. Our state has an outstanding tradition of involving Colorado's citizens in the political process, from ballot initiatives to this independent redistricting commission, and I would be honored to be a part of this process. I look forward to the opportunity to learn about how congressional districts are drawn, and how exactly citizens will be involved in making such important decisions for Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Last year I was selected as a juror in a four-day trial here in the 9th Judicial District. It was a profound experience. I saw firsthand the process that enables citizens to be a part of the decisions we make for our community. It felt as though we were serving the defendant as well, and I listened intently to every single word that was spoken during the trial. During deliberations, it was our responsibility to sort through the information we had been given, set any emotions aside, and make a gravely important decision regarding the future of one of our fellow Americans. When presented with the responsibility to be fair and impartial to a man who was facing prison time for his alleged involvement in the aftermath of a murder, I found that it was easy to look objectively at the facts presented. We were all the same race, but we were all different ages and from all different walks of life. The 12 of us did not agree when we first began deliberations, but we but we went around the room, giving each person the opportunity to speak, as we worked through the juror's instructions. I was amazed at how smoothly it went, but it also made sense, as we took our responsibility to the defendant very seriously. Serving on this re-districting committee would also be a sacred civic responsibility, and I would commit to be fair, impartial, and agreeable to my fellow citizens serving on the committee.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

With 12 years of experience as an EMT, I have learned to analyze situations without using emotion to impair my judgment. The dynamic nature of running a small business has also taught me to problem-solve while thinking on my feet. As a ski patroller I served as an Accident Investigator for three years. In my training, and the workshops I attended, I learned to gather facts from as many witnesses as possible, take the time to measure every angle and waypoint, and generate a formal, written investigation of the incident for use by the ski area's legal team in courts of law. It was my responsibility to produce these investigations in such a way that I showed no partiality to the ski area, nor to the guest affected by the incident. The best way to do this is apply logic and reason to the problem of generating a fair report.
