Background

Past Political Activity

I donated to the Biden campaign in 2020. I also wrote and sent 200 letters to citizens of Texas and South Carolina unlikely to vote in the 2020 election through the Vote Forward campaign.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

While completing my master's, I founded the Association of Women in Mathematics (AWM) CU Boulder chapter. During that time I taught welding through the Mechanical Engineering department, and volunteered with the Girl Scouts to teach welding workshops in our shop. I also worked as a long-term, weekly volunteer with Colorado Horse Rescue helping to maintain and rehabilitate horses. I currently serve as a graduate student mentor at CU Boulder. I was recently elected to the Research Applications Lab (RAL) representative council at NCAR, an internal group that works toward diversity, equity, and inclusion. Within RAL I've also served as a summer intern mentor.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

Founding and serving on the board of AWM I learned to organize social events and colloquiums geared toward equitable representation of women in the field of mathematics. I continue that work on the RAL representative council, focusing mainly on the inequities faced by female and other minority scientists. This work includes surveying the workforce to identify persistent issues, communicating findings to other decision-makers, and advocating for solutions that produce equitable outcomes. I find that most of the work on the representative council boils down to promoting awareness and communicating clearly (a sense of empathy and humor goes a long way).

Statement

I am a stickler for fairness. I hope to serve on the commission to ensure that our congressional district boundaries are draw in the most unbiased way possible.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I feel fortunate to share Colorado with an amazing, diverse group of people; everyone from Nobel laureates to ranchers. As a scientist I've had the opportunity to work with a broad range of stakeholders, each with their own needs and (sometimes conflicting) interests. It is my responsibility to hear and meet all those needs, with finite resources, within a set timeframe. I've learned that it is best to approach differing opinions with level-headedness, empathy, a willingness to listen and ask questions, and a strong understanding of my own biases. A kind word or a small joke can be very effective at dissipating a heated moment. I strongly believe that the goal of discourse is not about determining who is right, but what determining what is right.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I've worked with demographic data through my master's, PhD, and my day job. I understand its limitations, sources, and how to use it appropriately. My everyday work requires me to develop research questions, and to determine the methods and data necessary to answer those questions. My work also requires me to be proficient in spatial statistics and the use of mapping and mathematics software, such as ArcGIS, QGIS, R, and Python.
