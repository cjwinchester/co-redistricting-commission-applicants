Statement

I'm a fourth generation Coloradoan. I grew up in family committed to public service. My grandfather served as a Jackson County Commissioner, my mother was a precinct committee woman in Denver, my father was defeated in a State Senate race and later served as a Municipal Judge in Lakewood. I worked for the National Conference of State Legislatures (NCSL) for nearly 30 years in numerous capacities including policy analyst, program manager and senior fellow. I'm a political scientist at heart, committed to maintaining strong legislative institutions at all levels of government. One of the basic pillars of a healthy, functioning democracy is balanced government. I understand the importance of a fair redistricting and reapportionment process to meeting that goal.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I've spent my career working for a bipartisan organization that provides unbiased information, research and training to legislators and legislative staff in the 50 states and territories. In working with people of all parties and ideologies, I am able to view both sides of issues and to appreciate compromise. I am not an advocate for any issue, political party or organization. I am a stickler for fairness.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I rely on facts to inform my decisions. I weigh both sides of an argument or issue and where evidence is unclear, I try to minimize negative impacts.
