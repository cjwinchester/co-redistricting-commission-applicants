Statement

I want to serve on the Independent Redistricting Commission because I believe it is my civic duty. Due to my political science education and observing current events in the United States, I am urged to break out of my precedence of living in my local community as a spectator. I am urged to involve myself in any possible civic engagements that allow me to be part of the change and maintenance of our congressional elections.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe in the truth and I believe in maintaining what's just and fair. I am not driven by my ego and perception and I perceive that my own world views are ultimately inconsequential. I do not identify as a Democrat. I do not identify as a Republican. I am just an American. I do not let the tribalistic nature of American politics to influence how I seek the truth in society. I only care about finding the best possible terms and consensus amongst commissioners to determine congressional redistricting in Colorado.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am known in my local community as a fair and critical thinker. I am a current writer/contributor for OUT FRONT Magazine. I am a writer and my critical thinking skills are often used within my writing to explain to my readers' current events and news occurring in the United States and within my local community in Denver. A large component in my political science academic journey utilizes the ability to analyze and apply logic and reason to the social, political, and governmental obstacles we experience in the United States.
