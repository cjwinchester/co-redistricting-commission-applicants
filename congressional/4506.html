Statement

I am interesting in seeing Congressional Districts drawn in a manner that results in coherent districts that combine members of different political parties to as great extent as possible. It is unfortunate that Colorado has three Congressional Districts that are safely Republican, three that are safely Democratic, and only one (CD-6) that has traditionally been a swing district. The political geography of Colorado admittedly makes this challenging. I think creating more districts that are safe for neither party make it more likely that centrist candidates who can appeal to Republicans and Democrats can get elected. This can help ease the partisan divisions that have paralyzed our national government.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I would seek to understand other commissioners' viewpoints and try to identify common ground.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a good analyst with strong quantitative skills and consider myself a bit of a political junkie. I presume the commission will work with mapping software. I think we need to see what combinations of districts can maximize seats that both Republicans and Democrats can reasonably expect they have a chance to win. We may need to test out many combinations of districts to find those that retain geographic coherence and create as many swing districts as possible.
