Statement

I believe drawing redistricting lines in a fair and impartial manner is one of the most effective ways to ensure that we all get a vote that matters.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In my current role, I spend the majority of my time managing groups of people towards consensus. Our organization consists of individuals from 10 independent organizations who come together to create science-based policy that affects how resources are deployed in the upper Colorado River basin. Each stakeholder comes to the table with their own ideas and their own priorities, which all must be considered in the final decision. It is rare that all parties get exactly what they are seeking in the result, but very commonly, everyone gets 80% of what they were looking for. Most importantly, all members walk away from the table as partners, fully willing to engage in discussions on the next important topic. During each of these discussions, there is always an important viewpoint to consider from each of the partners, and commonly they are closer to each other than they realize. I find it is my job to dig in to those viewpoints and find the common ground to help the partnership move forward. I like to think that I am fair and impartial - though I imagine most do. In seeking fairness, I often try to get past what people are saying to the fundamental truth that is driving them to say what they are saying. It is often there that we can find more collective understanding than in what is said initially.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have a long history of compiling and using data to assist decision makers in making rational decisions. I am extremely logical and always begin with the end in mind, and work through issues in a sequential manner until a logical conclusion is reached. I am often asked for advice specifically because I can cut through emotions and personal feelings to find the underlying problem.
