Organization and Advocacy Experience

My past professional experience at The Pew Charitable Trusts and the Southwest Energy Efficiency Project taught me a great deal about using fact-based advocacy to achieve policy goals that promote the public interest. This background includes state budgets, transportation funding, clean energy and energy efficiency, wildfire mitigation, water policy and many other topics. In addition, my long-time work as a reporter, columnist and editorial writer for The Denver Post enabled me to learn about communities and issues statewide.

Statement

I want Colorado to have a U.S. House delegation that can serve as effective voice for our state in the nation's Capitol. Loud rhetoric and calcified political views won't solve the myriad problems that Colorado, and the country, face. The redistricting process offers an opportunity for Colorado to make elections more open to new candidates and ideas.

Statement on Working with Consensus

At the Pew Charitable Trusts, I watched the clean energy team negotiate with the Big Three Car makers about how to create reasonable but improved gas mileage standards. The car companies trusted the Pew team because we always kept our word. At a long-time journalist, I also know the importance of listening to all sides of a story. In particular, as a columnist and editorial writer I gained a deep appreciation for the complexities of public policy -- problems can't be solved with bumper-sticker slogans.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My journalism background enables me to collect and comprehend information. My academic work in intercultural communications helps me understand the values and needs of various communities, including people of color. My extensive travels to rural parts of the state opened my eyes to the economic struggles, lack of job prospects and budget restrictions that smaller towns confront. And my secondary job at a retailer whose clientele skews to the right has helped me understand the values embraced by most mainline conservatives -- while I may not agree with them, I always remain civil.
