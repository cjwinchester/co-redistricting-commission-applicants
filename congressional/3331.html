Organization and Advocacy Experience

While in the Denver CAO I realized that disparities in pay due to race and gender may have existed. I gathered a group of diverse attorneys and wrote a request to our management to research the issue and rectify it as necessary – along with making more objective and transparent polices re: pay generally. As a result, pay was adjusted throughout the CAO. The Sickle-Cell Anemia Advisory Committee consults with and provides direction to staff and faculty of the University of Colorado School of Medicine, Sickle Cell Treatment and Research Center, on the establishment of programs and research initiatives for the care and treatment of persons who have sickle- cell anemia. While on the Advisory Committee, I have successfully advocated for increased resources for the medical team and their work, and increased transparency on blood transfusions from Children’s Hospital Colorado to their minor patients and families.

Statement

A friend sent me an article outlining the need for a more diverse applicant pool. I have always been deeply committed to community/public service and related work – particularly as it relates to addressing racial, ethnic, health, and socioeconomic barriers and disparities. This work is vitally important and when done honestly and in a transparent manner – will make a lasting positive impact for Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Redistricting can be a highly political process, and everyone has their own biases. The foundation of effectively working together hinges on knowing each other and understanding the values that drive me and those that drive my fellow commissioners. We have to start from a place of agreement and agree to follow the correct process for decision-making. I am more interested in an effective process and outcome than a political "win." Unless you are not affected by a decision - no one is completely impartial. To be fair we must first acknowledge that and agree to set of accountability guidelines to base our decisions on the merits and not on our own biases.
