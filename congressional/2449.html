Statement

While not perfect, the American system of democratic government is rare and precious. As we have just witnessed this year, the right to vote and the ability to hold fair elections is the cornerstone of this democracy. Voting districts should reflect changes in populations and ensure that everyone is fairly represented. It is my belief that politicians should not be able to design districts to gain partisan advantage. I voted for Amendments Y and Z in 2018 and am proud to live in a State that has removed that power and put it in the hands of an impartial panel of citizens. While I have no preconceived notions or specific experience in drawing political districts, I would consider it a privilege and a distinct challenge to serve with a group of fellow citizens to devise fair and representative voting districts.

Statement on Working with Consensus

The first step with my fellow commissioners would be to understand what we know and what we need to learn. What are best practices? What works and what doesn't? What is the competitive landscape? What about Colorado's history or geography is particularly important to this task? Hopefully we can bring in nonpartisan experts to answer our questions and provide perspectives. Once we are on the same starting page, key is listening to each other, making sure everyone communicates their views, laying out our preconceptions and biases, and slowly determining and listing essential goals and priorities that need to be met. I encourage thoughtful exchange, a careful and deliberative process, and focus on making progress. A dollop of humor greatly helps. Fairness is central to this process. I would not want to serve on a commission that is not grounded in an impartial and fair outcome.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As creative director and CEO of a 40-year old communications firm, I essentially am tasked every day with solving problems. Marketing or branding problems of our clients. Interpersonal and turf problems of my employees. Creative and positioning problems of our campaigns. Some of the problems are functional, some emotional, some intuitive, and all are complex with competing agendas. As a steward of the business for these past four decades, I have found logic and reason to be anchor points and the very ground of our decision processes.
