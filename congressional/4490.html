Background

Past Political Activity

I have not been involved in political activity in more than 50 years. In the past election (2020), I donated money (totaling less than $400) to several congressional candidates (John Hickenlooper, Jamie Harrison, Amy McGrath).

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I do volunteer work for the Boulder Public Library and have done volunteer work for "Special Transit," now called VIA.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I worked for the Boulder city government for many years ending in 2002. I have not done any public organizing or advocacy.

Statement

My understanding is that, at this point, only white males have applied to serve on this commission. I am interested in fair representation for all residents of the state of Colorado where I have lived for 50 years and as a female I would add just a bit of diversity to the commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

While I am a registered Democrat, I believe in fair representation for all the residents of Colorado. I would work to arrive at this goal in my efforts on the commission. As an employee of city government for over 20 years, I was exposed to a multitude of viewpoints of the residents of my city and observed the City Council and various city boards as they grappled with solving the city's problems.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As I mentioned, in my employment with the city of Boulder government, I work for at least 10 years as a research assistant, writing surveys and analyzing survey data and writing the results. Prior to being a research assistant, I also served as the recording secretary for the city's Planning Board where I heard and understood many of the concerns of active Boulder residents. My studies in Anthropology at the Master's level also included analysis and understanding of social issues, viewed with logic and insight into the history and etiology of social conditions.
