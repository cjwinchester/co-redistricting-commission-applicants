Statement

I believe the work of this commission is critical to assuring that our representatives in government truly represent us in a manner consistent with the "one-man/one-vote" principle. Otherwise, our elections are unnecessarily exercises in political machinations rather than attempts to identify the will of the people. When the partisan distribution of the election of representatives is dramatically different from the partisan distribution of the actual votes, that is a sign that districting is unrepresentative. Given that the Supreme Court designated this as clearly a state-level decision, I would very much like to be part of that decision-making at our state level as I believe I have skills and knowledge and understanding of the issues to be a valuable member. I was late in coming to this decision as I was distracted by the recent election. Then, a friend made me deal with my responsibility to at least apply, given many past discussions about this issue. As a result, I have no letters of recommendation but would be happy to obtain them if I am selected through the random selection process to be in the pool of candidates.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have spent most of my adult life in positions that required me to build consensus and create agreement among colleagues, subordinates, and constituents. My service as a member of the Colorado Standards and Assessment Development and Implementation Council required doing this on a regular basis as did the various leadership positions I held throughout my career. One of my strongest skills in this area is the ability to listen carefully, accurately summarize various positions of group members, identify areas of agreement and disagreement, and suggest strategies to resolve those differences. I have learned how not to impose my biases on those interactions while also knowing how to be clear when I am advocating rather than facilitating. I know that there are many ways to achieve a goal. I find that often what appears to be argument about how we should achieve our group goal is actually a lack of agreement regarding that goal. I have demonstrated the ability to identify this difference and help a group step back and reclarify its purpose. Being fair and impartial about how we achieve an agreed upon goal is much easier once the goal is agreed upon. I have worked hard to cultivate the habits identified by Stephen Covey, especially "seek first to understand" and "begin with the end in mind". I am applying for this appointment because I am strongly committed to the goal of the commission to redistrict in a manner that is fair and consistent with the principle of "one-man/one-vote". I don't think you are looking for people who are un-biased or impartial on the importance of that goal. But as I mentioned above, I have the group participation skills needed to be impartial and unbiased in discussing how we achieve this goal.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I know that advanced degrees are not a criterion for selection to serve on this commission. However, the analytical and problem-solving skills I have acquired through these advanced studies, combined with my experience, make me well equipped to join in solving the problems associated with this commission's mission. I know how to break problems down so that they can be seen more clearly; I have knowledge frameworks that give me tools to apply logic and reason; and, I can help other members of the group see the logic of each other's arguments.
