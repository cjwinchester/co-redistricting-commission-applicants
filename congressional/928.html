Statement

For as long as I can remember, I have been fascinated by our government's history and how it functions. Through school my favorite topics were American History and Government and I have sustained this interest throughout my adult life. I have always viewed the government through a lens of fairness. I try to consider how equitable and fair our government is to the people it represents. The very basic level of fairness can be seen through the context of the electoral maps and how they are drawn. When districts are manipulated in certain ways, the map can benefit those already in power, at the expense of the constituents in gerrymandered districts. If maps are drawn unfairly, it can be difficult to vote out elected officials who are not performing satisfactorily or advancing the will of the people who they are elected to serve. I believe it is important to create fairness in how our government is run and in the selection process. Fair maps elevate the principle of “one person, one vote.” Fair maps also hold elected officials accountable to the people they serve. The best way to create fair electoral maps is to take into account a multitude of different factors, including an area's demographics. It would be an honor to be a part of this process. I know I have the skills and passion to help our great state. This would also set an example, for the rest of our country, on how to create fair maps. I have always strived to incorporate fairness in my daily life and I have taught my children about fairness and equality. Being a part of this process would be a pivotal way to set those beliefs into action.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In this tumultuous time in our union, I still believe there is more that binds us than divides us. I also believe that on topics that I differ with someone else, I cannot ask them to accept my opinion or point of view if I am not willing to listen to their beliefs or experiences. In this thread, there can be commonalities with every person you meet. There may not be agreement on everything, but there is always some common ground. Once that common ground is found, you can build from there and connect the dots to other places of agreement. When I was the chair of the Young Professionals Committee at ACLU, we had a diverse group of committee members. Our committee members came from a multitude of backgrounds; because of this, everyone brought different strengths and ideas. This committee was very impactful on my life. Not only did listening to the committee members make us all smarter and give us a better product for our monthly events, it made me more compassionate and empathetic to people who were very different than me. My tendency for inclusivity was one of the reasons I was given the chair position after one month on the committee. I always listened intently, believed in our collective group and was proud of the outcome. Everyone who will be on this committee will be there for the reason of making Colorado more fair through the Legislative and Congressional maps. We are Democrats, Republicans and Unaffiliated individuals who come from a multitude of backgrounds. We have all applied and will have been accepted to make a positive impact on our state. I fully intend to use the tools from my previous work and volunteer positions to work with all the other commissioners to make a positive impact on our state maps.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

For the 2016-2017 school year, I was the lab manager for the afterschool STEM Scouts at Stargate Charter School. The STEM Scouts program was designed for third to fifth grade students as an intro to STEM curriculum. To expand this program to second graders, I adapted the entire program to meet the needs of this age group. I created weekly labs to educate the kids and foster love and curiosity for science. I researched and brainstormed each topic to create age appropriate labs and activities to engage fifteen 7–8 year olds. I developed multiple station labs to keep them involved and motivated for a 90-minute science lab. After each week's lab, I analyzed how the lab went and would come up with ideas as to how to improve the lab for the students. Not only was I in charge of running the experiments; I also mediated disagreements between students in lab groups to help foster habits of sharing, taking turns and working towards a common goal in each lab. When I worked at Enterprise Rent-A-Car from 2003–2009, I worked in the Loss Control Department, where I held a broad range of roles and responsibilities. My main job focused on risk management assessment and prevention at the car rental locations and property damage claims for the vehicles. I analyzed data and helped with strategic planning for long term budgeting based on damage losses and risk assessment. As a member of the management staff in my department, I created, administered, and reviewed audits and training for Rental Branch and Loss Control Department employees. I also assisted in plan development for rental branch locations and employees not meeting adequate standards, to reach or exceed compliance and training. As the lab manager of the STEM Scouts program, I had no formal science or teaching background. I did, however, have a background in finding and analyzing data and training employees at Enterprise Rent-A-Car. I was able to take that knowledge and use it in a completely different situation. I would like to be able to apply that same data analysis skill set on the commission to ensure that we have fair and equitable maps for the next 10 years.
