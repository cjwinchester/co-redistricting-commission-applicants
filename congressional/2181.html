Background

Professional Background

I am a 2018-2019 Albert Einstein Distinguished Educator Fellow. I served my 11 month placement at the National Science Foundation in Alexandria, VA. My primary responsibility was to advocate for high quality STEM teachers for underrepresented rural, Hispanic, and low-socioeconomic students in southern Colorado for staff at the NSF, EHR, DUE, Noyce-HSI-ATE programs. I brought my experience in DC back to my community and have successfully assisted Adams State University in their development of grant applications for NSF funding and quality STEM development programs.

Statement

I want to ensure that the unique nature of the San Luis Valley in Southern Colorado is represented in this process. Significant topics include the historical context of settlement in Colorado, water rights, and access to high quality education and educators for all students in Colorado.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am a lifelong member of the Southern Colorado area. My family homesteaded in the Trinidad area before Colorado became a State. I understand the nuances of population differences in the Colorado population. As a professional educator, I often have to hear challenging positions and untangle opposing views. My time spent in DC involved intensive training in conflict resolution and consensus building. I passionately believe in allowing all voices to be heard and contribute to the process.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am working on my PhD in Applied Mathematics through UCCS. I have extensive experience in statistics, logic and analysis. Proof based logic is an essential skill I teach in middle school, high school, community college, GED, and university courses.
