Statement on Working with Consensus

I have experience with leading teams of software engineers and have grown greatly over the years in doing so. There are hundreds of ways to build software and everyone will do it differently. I've used many different techniques to gain agreement from others, whether it is simple pro/con lists, or a lot of times just walking through a problem step by step on how each person views it so that we all can better understand where they are coming from. A large piece of it is just trying to step back and listen to all sides. I once served on a 6 or 7 person jury, largely older men, in Denver that was a civil lawsuit for someone that got into a car accident and was suing the insurance company. When we went into deliberations they elected me as a foreman. I started off by going around the room and asking where everyone stood. Three of them were on the plaintiff's side, I was on on the defendant's, and the others were undecided. I spent time just listening to everyone's arguments, and we looked through all of the documentation. The man had pre-existing conditions in his sacrum region. We drew a timeline on the whiteboard of all of his shots he received for pain, when the accident was, how long the shots lasted, etc. We worked out as a team that it was over a month after his accident that he started feeling pain, this also coincided with the same duration of time when his shot would normally wear out. In the end, we all sided with the defendant.
