Statement

Every elected official represents a community of people. The challenge, the problem to be solved, is how to divide the state population into logical smaller communities in a way that is fair - in a way that is not contrived to benefit one group over another. I think this is an important task and I think I have qualities that will benefit this process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I think it is important for the group to start by defining the overall goals - to be sure that we all agree on what we are trying to do. On a broad level, it shouldn't be too hard to agree on the overarching goals - the conflict will come in the details. To promote consensus I would advocate that we start by each person putting forward ideas and concerns without comment from others. Discussion can then be directed at addressing these concerns. In group discussions where there is disagreement I like to try and listen to and acknowledge people's emotional response and use that to identify issues that can be addressed impartially. I recognize my own partiality, I recognize that the political outcomes that I want are not what everyone wants. I can feel strongly about my own political agenda and still be strongly committed to finding a way to fairly and impartially divide up the state into logical communities for representation.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My career in research points to my strengths in logic and analysis. I enjoy solving problems that include complex relationships and caveats.
