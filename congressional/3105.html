Statement on Working with Consensus

If we all put Colorado voters first, as individuals with unique needs and stories, then we should be able to have an open and honest discourse about how to best represent Colorado communities. In my professional career, I often work to align different organizations and departments on a set of shared goals. It is critical to understand each organization's or departments' needs, but when problem solving is approached with a shared goal in mind, the outcomes can often benefit everyone involved. I also have a long history of understanding each person or departments mandates, which fosters a respectful environment where people feel heard. Listening and respecting one another is the foundation required in order to promote consensus and understanding. My background in Journalism and my love for ethical leadership have primed me to be an objective and reasonable team player. It starts with listening and working together on identifying the goals, then understanding each person's unique strengths, and delegating the necessary tactics so we complete our goals. This is a commission that should remain fair and objective and I am committed to contributing to these foundational values.

I feel confident in my ability to assess all the variables and create a prioritization based on the group's goals and objectives.
