Statement

Our representative democracy is founded on the principle of elected officials representing a group of people. When districts are created, they should, to the extent possible, represent all people who live in that area not just those of a particular affiliation. This work is incredibly important to moving forward with civility, bipartisanship, and compromise which should be more representative all persons living in a geographic area. Efforts toward making that a reality could impact our state for many years. If I can serve the commission by applying my analytical expertise, open-mindedness, and a deep sense of fairness, it would be a great honor as well as a remarkable responsibility.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My work as an Application Architect involved the evaluation of hundreds of installed software applications identifying redundant functionality, performing cost/benefit analysis, recommending a single platform for consolidation, then working with the corporate constituents to gain consensus for the recommendation. The work requires a high attention to detail, excellent research skills, ability to communicate pros and cons succinctly, and conduct focused workshops. Staying open to new information, fact-based concerns, as well as the human factors that go into decision making allowed me to be successful moving projects forward. These skills are all relevant to working on the commission and building consensus for the redistricting efforts. Using data to drive the work while being sensitive to possible biases enhances transparency which, I believe, leads to fair, impartial decisions.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My 36 year career as a software engineer was successful because of my ability to apply logic and reason to problem solving. My unique contribution was to be able to approach a problem from multiple perspectives yielding more than one approach to a find a credible solution. In my realm, a solution is one that not only meets the functional criteria but also optimizes other factors such as ease-of-use, cost, flexibility, and extensibility. Each factor requires research to understand the problem space, doing data collection and analysis, and finally, recommending the best solution. To apply these skills to the redistricting work would be both fascinating and rewarding.
