Statement on Working with Consensus

I have succeeded as a consensus builder by following several basic steps. First, it is necessary to establish ground rules ensuring that all participants in the process will have a full opportunity to contribute. Early on, participants should focus their efforts on identifying and understanding the objective or goal that we are trying to achieve. When the goal is clear, it is helpful to look at a wide spectrum of alternatives that will advance us toward the goal. Preliminary analysis will help refine and narrow the focus on a handful of potential resolutions. As the group drills deeper, the objectivity and accuracy of the analysis takes on added importance. Group members should challenge the analysis and should respectfully challenge each other. In my experience, it is much easier to advocate for a position that is supported by objective analysis, and I often find myself doing so. Trade-offs and compromises are often necessary, but as they are negotiated, the ultimate goal or objective of the exercise should always be kept in mind. A goal-oriented, fact-based process promotes fairness and impartiality. When these ideals are threatened, I try to steer the group back to the goal, and back to the facts. One of the challenges I face at the helm of a cash-strapped small utility is compensating a highly skilled work force in a manner that is fair to both the employees and the rate-payers that bear the burden of this expense. Using the process outlined above, I have built consensus in connection with union negotiations that achieved this delicate balance. As a utility executive, I am charged to develop and apply programs, policies and rates that are fair and nondiscriminatory. Nondiscriminatory rate making requires intensive analysis of the cost to serve our rate-paying member base. This kind of intensive analysis can lead to a lack of innovation, as incumbents tend to rely on systems that have been in place for years, for fear that new approaches would lead to regulatory challenges based on discrimination. I have led innovative changes that benefit vulnerable rate-payers, while still maintaining fair and nondiscriminatory rates.
