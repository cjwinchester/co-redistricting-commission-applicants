Statement

This is an opportunity to protect and strengthen our representative democracy so that all persons feel their vote counts. Colorado is becoming more diverse and it is important that our political districts reflect the diverse demographics and values of our state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe in fact driven conversations and recognize that this will require preparation and knowledge. I hope that these commissions are representative so that there are differences of opinions requiring negotiation and compromise. Levity helps people feel comfortable and trust each other. When representing a client I am expected to be biased and loyal to them, even when I disagree with their goals or actions. Consequently, I am accustomed to being able to consider different or unpopular views and I am practiced in applying bias and, conversely, managing for my own bias. As a manager of other people, I have to be balanced and fair. I also believe most decision making is best done with adequate time for the deciders to deliberate on the various viewpoints, rather than being forced to a conclusion. Finally, my experience is that quality decisions often are better made in increments; particularly if the group has agreed to overriding principles (where consensus is usually first easiest to reach) and then the group relies on those principles to stay focused and ensure respectful and productive interactions.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

The LSAT is just one big logic game and law school trains people to reason through complicated questions and support their answers. My work requires me to apply legal reasoning almost daily; and also requires me to maintain a working knowledge of financing and budgeting for government organizations, the legislative process, staff management principles, and currently (unfortunately) epidemiology. Some days I must be able to pull together all these different competencies into decision-making on critical issues for our agency.
