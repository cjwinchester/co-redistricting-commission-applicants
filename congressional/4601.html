Statement on Working with Consensus

I have built cultures of collaboration and consensus throughout my entire career. As a leader and organizer, I have been able to get all people to make meaningful contributions to the process and obtain their buy-in. The first method that I use is called appreciative inquiry. I actively seek the input of others by setting ground rules for an honest and open conversations and asking a series of questions. What's important? What's working? What would be ideal? What's not quite working yet? What resources can we find to work together to move toward the ideal? When conflict arises, I am able to point back to the ground rules that we agreed on and manage a process that everyone believes is fair. I have found that doing "rounds" is helpful. Everyone is given an opportunity to speak and we go around the room. Generally, I ask them to follow another series of questions in their presentation so that people can express themselves freely (one of the ground rules is that we will provide room for disagreement). What are the facts that are relevant to you? What are your feelings? What judgements are you able to make? What actions do you think we should take? I have had great success at obtaining consensus, even when there is broad disagreement.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am trained as a scientist and use data science in my work every day. I use scientific method in the way I approach life and have expertise in statistics. I believe scientists don't generate conclusions, but rather, they test hypotheses in a set of experiments designed to rule out alternatives. I find the scientific method is a good way to describe the experiments (Background, Hypothesis, Methods and Procedures, Results, Analysis, Conclusion).
