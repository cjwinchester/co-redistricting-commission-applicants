Statement

Some decades back, my State Senator (Ken Gordon) started talking/writing about redistricting and how it should not be partisan or gerrymandered and should lead to competitive districts as much as possible. It hit a chord with me and I exchanged some occasional correspondence with Sen. Gordon around the time of at least a couple of redistricting periods. The last time was after he was gone from office and I had attended an early public meeting about it and didn't find him there. Surprised at that, I got in contact with him and encouraged him to attend a subsequent public meeting. He did, and got to make his presentation again. While he has since died, I suggested once that legislators consider naming any bill that adopts what he suggested after him.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I strongly believe that congressional districts (as well as the follow-along legislative districts) be as competitive as possible so as to promote candidates running towards the middle of various idealogies. In other words, no or very few "safe" districts, and hopefully no radical or extremist viewpoints getting elected. Of either 'side'. Both primaries and generals should favor candidates who head to the middle from one side or the other. I don't consider myself to be a hothead, but do worry that I might be too quiet. An awful lot will depend on the rules and procedures that commissioners are either presented with or adopt. It will be very interesting to see how so much data and factions can be brought together and hashed over to come up with a valid workable map. While I have long been a registered Republican, I wasn't the first time or two I voted, and there are GOP ideas and philosophies that I don't agree with.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have a math minor and worked as a processing geophysicist, staff geophysicist, and programmer.
