Statement

A friend in who is a Rotarian asked me if I was interested. I did not know or understand what this was all about until a second person, also in Rotary, (in our class that is studying racism), made a comment that the need still exists for commissioners for redistricting. They went on to say that it would be a good opportunity to actually do something towards fighting racism in Colorado. I believe this to be one of our Country's biggest issues at present and I would like to gain a better understanding of all marginalized citizens and try to make an impact on this. I researched this and attended the Q&A and became even more interested in this opportunity.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a social worker my work was to offer emotional support and help people solve their problems, look for resources, point out and/or look for strengths and weakness that could either help or impede their progress toward change. As a counselor I have active listening skills that are essential when people are trying to be heard. With these skills I can hear a person’s point of view, value that point of view without judgement and remain impartial and strive for fairness with the other commissioners. Being heard develops a sense of trust that facilitates open communication with each other. I bring to the table a calm demeanor which often helps diffuse tense situations.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My analytical skill set includes prioritizing the issues, seeking a deeper understanding of the root causes. Gather facts in a non- threatening way. Then action can be taken towards resolving the issue or problem. Brainstorming with the team, being creative and exploring available options would be the teams next step. Or, recognizing an impasse and move to another issue temporarily. Often, just time, can be helpful as the team can then return to an issue with more clarity.
