Statement

To restore politics in America to a level in which "compromise" is no longer considered to be a "dirty" word. This can be done by making congressional districts, so far as it possible, competitive. This would draw politics back to the center which is where most Coloradans are politically.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I served as a Peace Corps Volunteer in Africa. At that time I was elected by my fellow volunteers to act as Co-Chair for the Volunteer Council which liaised with the Country Director. This involved building consensus among the volunteers before taking an issue to the staff and then negotiating with the country staff in order to try to get the volunteer's desires met. The most important lesson which my Peace Corps service taught me was that if you do not understand another person's ideas it is because you are using your own paradigm instead of theirs. Only with an understanding of the positions of others can there be any possibility of achieving a shared goal.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My Certificate in Safety and Systems Management was awarded at the completion of the USAF Aviation Accident Investigation School. This course taught how to apply logic to the evidence in reaching a conclusion about an event which may not have been witnessed.
