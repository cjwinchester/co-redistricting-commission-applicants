Statement

I'm a strong proponent and advocate for our federal system and its three branches. In my 20 year naval career (intelligence field) and 20+ year follow-on career in the defense industry here in Colorado, I've lived, committed and worked using my personal mantra of 4P's: purpose, passion, performance and persistence. I am a proud citizen, husband, father, grandfather. My parents inspired and guided their six kids. I live the values of the 'greatest generation' - family, faith, and country. Assisting in redistricting efforts is a high calling. I believe my educational training and career background, civic engagement and commitment to service mark me as a well-qualified candidate.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In my civic and community service in multiple organizations, I have been guided by personal ethic of 'service before self', and tireless commitment to finding and building 'common ground'. I am a good and discerning listener, an experienced analyst and project manager, a people-person committed to respectful discussions and evaluations. In my naval career, I was presiding officer for seven summary courts martial proceedings, gaining direct personal experience in pursuing facts, listening and hearing both sides, and in carefully weighing the body of evidence before making disciplinary recommendations. That youthful Navy experience has stayed with me and will guide me in serving as a commissioner: careful examination, objective analysis, careful and respectful discussion, and personal commitment to honest and objective service.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Analytical and problem solving skills were developed during successful and challenging naval career as intelligence officer (Vietnam, Cold War, Desert Shield): close attention to detail, careful evaluation of past and present situations and conditions, discernment and identification of facts, objective prioritization and case presentation, etc. Successful career in defense industry supporting US Space Command and US Northern Command required logical discussion and evaluation of complex issues and programs, extensive written and oral presentations of multi-layered and interconnected program elements and issues, and professional discussions and direct conversations with senior officers and senior civilian officials. In my naval and civilian careers, as well as in multiple civic and non-profit organizations, my focus has been on careful weighing of facts and perspectives on difficult and contentious issues, and on logical analysis and objective discussion, always striving for common ground and what's best for the organization.
