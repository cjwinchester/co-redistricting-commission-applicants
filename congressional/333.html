Statement

I believe that the right to vote is the most important Constitutional guarantee to every citizen. It should be the single equalizing factor across all demographics. I was encouraged to learn Colorado was forming an independent redistricting commission made up of citizens, because this should remove politics from the analysis. I have been disheartened by gerrymandering efforts in other states, including several I have lived and voted in. I believe I can contribute a reasoned approach to the redistricting process that will benefit every Colorado citizen.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am analytical in my approach to fact gathering. I enjoy productive discussions that are intended to reach a mutual goal. I am organized in my thinking, speaking and drafting. I listen before I speak. I am respectful and will seek knowledge to share with my fellow commissioners. As an attorney who has been practicing for more than 20 years, I often must negotiate terms with adverse, opposing, or other interested third parties. I make ethical proposals and I look for reasonable options. I keep the best interests of my *client* centered. As a member of the commission, I will view every Coloradan as a client, and my effort will be focused on drawing a map that provides every voter a fair and equal path to having his or her vote counted.

I have managed large teams of people, leading by example by displaying my values. I have regularly identified opportunities for improvement, founded practice groups, and established new roles to serve my clients. I have successfully worked on cross-functional task forces to grow consensus to implement and deploy policies. I have drafted operational standards, set and measured objectives against results, built alliances, and persuaded C-level corporate leadership to make enterprise-wide improvements.
