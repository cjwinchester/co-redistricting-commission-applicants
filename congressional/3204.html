Organization and Advocacy Experience

I developed a public opinion measurement tool using Google searches to predict elections and inform campaign strategy. It is a more accurate approach to political polling because while people may lie to friends, family, canvassers, or pollsters about their political intentions because of social desirability bias, nobody lies to Google because they don't fear being judged. That makes it a more honest window into peoples' motivations for voting. I set out to find this data source after realizing that survey polling is inadequately ascribing cognitive processes for voting, when they are in fact emotional processes. Using this new data source, we can consider the interests of groups empirically in a way that portends with modern advances in political theory related to evolutionary psychology and behavioral psychology that surveys cannot examine.
