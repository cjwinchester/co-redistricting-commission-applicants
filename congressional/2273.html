Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe consensus and collaboration are partners in decision making. Consensus is the give-and-take of information sharing. It's the ability to discuss differences calmly and come to some form of an agreement. By discussing differences in opinion, new ideas are free to surface. Consensus involves reaching an acceptable agreement in a manner that respects others. It involves appraising competing ideas, contradictory statements, and dissimilar points of views; however, by the end, a satisfactory agreement is reached honoring the differing ideas without degrading others involved in the process. I employ something called the 4 agreement: #1-Be impeccable with your words (use your word choice wisely & never question a person motives or honor) #2- Don't take anything personally #3- Don't make assumptions (for this leads to conflict) #4- Always do your best (& know that others are trying to do their best too). I use the 4 agreements to begin meetings, which helps all members know the expectations that spring out of a foundation of respect. I add humor to my approach and usually people will come together because they know we all are working to make a positive difference.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Let's first begin with my definition of logical reasoning. It is the process of analyzing a situation and coming up with a sensible solution. It requires one to study a problem objectively, make rational conclusions (based on evidence) and then use the fact that are available to address the situation or problem. I have led many educational teams with competing ideas and diverse backgrounds. I like to use discussion protocols to get every voice in the room and then begin to identify common linkages or areas of common ground. In the areas where there are difference, I like to question to better understand the why behind ideas. Again, I apply the 4 agreements to manage feelings and talking points with the goal of honoring all voices in the room. In the end, by developing professional relationships based on a sense of trust and rapport, people will come together as they feel honored and heard.
