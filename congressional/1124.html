Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have served as an officer and board member of several organizations representing legal, historic, educational, conservation, county and municipal entities. Before my retirement, I served on several legal, judicial, and other committees and boards, advocating for or against various issues which I believed were helpful to my community, for example, the Jefferson County Jail Advisory committee, which dealt with jail issues such as over-crowding.

Statement

For years I believed there was a better solution to re-districting, both legislative and congressional, than leaving it up to the CO Legislature. When the commission proposals passed in 2018, I was excited to serve on either commission if asked. I believe my background as a judge has helped prepare me to be able to reach fair and impartial decisions, and I intend to carry on that ability if I am selected to serve.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have many friends from both political parties and all walks of life, young and old. I have also served on many appointed or volunteer boards and committees which contained diverse persons holding differing opinions and beliefs. I believe that I am able to bridge disagreements by remaining open to their ideas, by contributing my own, and by remaining a valued friend or committee member whether or not my view or belief prevailed. As I already mentioned, I believe that I was a fair and impartial municipal judge before I retired, and I assure you that will remain a force with me if selected.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My legal career has included five years as a prosecutor, thirteen years in private legal practice, and twenty-one years as a municipal judge. As a prosecutor, I tried numerous criminal cases, from traffic to felonies, and I interviewed many witnesses and those accused, before deciding how to proceed. As a sole practitioner, I represented many clients either in criminal, civil, and domestic cases, and advised others dealing with writing wills or handling estates. I also served as a court-appointed attorney for indigent defendants and individuals dealing with domestic abuse or mental illness, including many juveniles. As a municipal judge, my most important attribute was listening. I believe that I listen carefully to other views before making my own decisions, after considering all other alternatives or solutions.
