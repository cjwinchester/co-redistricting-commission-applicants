Statement

Political parties do not talk anymore and no longer compromise. They have litigated legislative boundaries as a win lose proposisition for their parties and their constituents, as they see it. These boudaries affect far more than political parties and need neutrality in the process to serve all people for all purposes.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I suspect a good place to start in drawing boundaries is to determine what the most important criteria is or should be. I would propose trying to create a list of priorities and then see if we could rank them or at least group them by order of priority. If this didn't work for the commission, then it is time to listen to what others think may be more appropriate. Should jurisdictional boundaries be more important than related issues? If a group can agree upon and floow an agreed upon approach, much disagreement on potentially more divisive issues can hopefully be avoided. The process must start with listening to what others most care about. Specific ideas can follow.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have been a licensed attorney since October of 1994. The profession requires an ability to think through complex issues and try to reach a reasonable conclusion and often to reach a consensus or compromise between competing interests. I have helped resolve many issues but the most common type as applicable here may be in employment law. It is important to understand the law, the parties, the competing interests and to try to determine where overlap, and therefore compromise can occur. This requires analysis, logic, reasoning and continually adjustment. These skills could translate very well to the commission.
