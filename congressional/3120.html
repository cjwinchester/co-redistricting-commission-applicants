Organization and Advocacy Experience

I try and educate the people on various family friendly initiatives in the state of Colorado. First and foremost my passions are improving birth outcomes for families as well as curing monolingualism. I do this by training more women to become labor Doulas, teaching Spanish and participating in work groups, conference calls and planning sessions here in metro Denver, Colorado. I have been a Birth worker since 1999 and an Educator since 1997.

Statement

I am very passionate about preserving the integrity of physiologic birth and bring the information to the masses of families who need education on all things birth and breastfeeding. I believe that putting families first is the cure this Nation needs to improve birth outcomes, improve education and diversification of women and POC in all areas of civic duty. I would love to be a change advocate in Colorado and serve to make life great here in the Rocky Mountains. I recently learned that my Paternal Grandmother's entire family is from this area. My roots are strong here and it makes me proud. (I was born and raised in West Virginia and have called Colorado my home for the last 6 years). Thank you very much for your consideration to make this state a leader in the Nation and for my consideration to join the Commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As an educator, I understand how important it is to be impartial and fair. I also think it is important to look at all sides of a problem. I proud myself on being a solution oriented human. I have been practicing consensus voting methods since my position on the school board at a private Montessori school in WV. I continue to bring that to our staff meetings at my current Montessori school and also use this in the classroom with my students. This is also how the leadership for the Doula Program at Denver Health move issues forward.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am very interested in statistics and evidence based research when it pertains to health care and education. I also know how important research is to discussing problems. It is imperative to consult with experts in any field and to have varying opinions in order to represent the community. When working with individuals, we must develop relationships and develop an ear to listening in order to have all of the puzzle pieces and make sound judgments based on fact and not opinion. When talking to Community real life speaks volumes and multiple avenues must be entertained in order to get adequate documentation.
