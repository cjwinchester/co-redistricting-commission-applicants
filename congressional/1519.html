Organization and Advocacy Experience

As the Alamosa County attorney and a member of various associations and committees I have had the opportunity to advocate for, and consider the interests of, not only a diverse background of elected officials, but also individuals and groups who were unable to advocate for themselves regardless of politics. For the last 8 years the Board of County Commissioners has consisted of both republicans and democrats which has allowed me to develop the ability to analyze issues from a neutral perspective and provide arguments both for and against issues facing the Board.

Statement

There is little question that our state, and even more so, our country is incredibly divided. I want to have the opportunity to help shrink that division by providing a way in which the voice of not only individuals, but communities are adequately represented. I firmly believe that the system we have for choosing our representatives is one of the greatest, if not the greatest in history. However, everyone needs to have an equal part in that representation, not just a select group of individuals/groups.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In serving as an attorney for an organization as well as in leadership positions on various boards and committees I recognize that my way is not the only way. In addition, I recognize that everyone has certain biases that we have to overcome. By recognizing this, it allows me set aside personal preferences and work in a way that is beneficial to the whole group and build consensus.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

The hallmark of a good attorney is the ability to look at an issue and the facts; analyze the issue under the framework of the law; and then develop a solution to the problem. My profession provides me that opportunity on a daily basis. Redistricting is a complex issue and I am committed to putting forth the time and effort to ensure that all Coloradoans are fairly represented.
