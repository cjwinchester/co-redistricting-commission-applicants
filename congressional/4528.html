Statement

Fair and equal representation is the foundation of democracy. Widespread gerrymandering has reduced Americans' confidence in their government. In order to stem the loss of Americans' faith in their democracy, we must strive to ensure that redistricting is fair, justifiable, and transparent. I would like to play in part in that process, as a way to support democracy in my home state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My intent in applying for the commission is to help ensure that the outcome is fair. I feel that gerrymandering is a scourge, and I would work to achieve consensus on a fair outcome. My career as a scientist involved collaborating with a wide variety of collaborators and co-authors, as can be seen in my attached resume. Much of my work involved collaborating across different disciplines and backgrounds -- I trained as a theoretical computer scientist, then worked first in telecommunications and then in conservation biology and ecology. Working with collaborators with wildly varying educational backgrounds and interests has been effective training for promoting consensus among people with different backgrounds and viewpoints but a common goal.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I worked for 20 years as a data scientist, where varied analytical skills, logic and reason were required daily. Most relevant to redistricting is my work in computation ecology, which involved the development of new analytical methods, algorithms and code for spatial modeling, analysis and optimization of species geographic distributions. This work arose from my concern about the extinction crisis -- many species are likely to be driven to extinction in our lifetimes -- and the desire to apply rigorous mathematical and computation methods to make a contribution to addressing the crisis. My software for species distribution modeling is used by scientists worldwide, and has been cited in more than 10,000 scientific papers.
