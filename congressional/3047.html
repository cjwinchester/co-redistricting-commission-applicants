Organization and Advocacy Experience

I work in the government relations office of the largest university system in the state, and one of Colorado's largest employers. We work on the federal and state level to influence public policy in the interest of higher education and the University system. This includes lobbying at the state and local level, hosting events with elected officials, coordinating outreach trips for CU leadership to visit with folks across the state, and working in a bipartisan fashion. In my previous job, I worked in a congressional office as the State Scheduler for U.S. Senator Cory Gardner, which helped me gain a strong understanding of the various congressional districts and issues across Colorado. In my role, I coordinated the Senator's schedule and his visits to all 64 counties, provided research and briefings, and met with various constituent groups to discuss their issues.

Statement

I believe it is critical to have a young, working professional female voice on the commission. The state has become overwhelming diverse since the districts were last drawn, and I hope to contribute to redistricting that is fair and equitable. My current and past work experience has given me the chance to listen and work with constituent groups across all political spectrums, attend town halls with elected officials, and participate in stakeholder groups. I believe my knowledge and expertise will serve the commission well.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Developing legislative strategy that is fair and bipartisan is a key component for my current role as Director of State Relations. I am an unaffiliated voter, and have a strong desire to bring equality to the redistricting of our state. Colorado's population has grown and shifted significantly, even during the short 6 years I've lived here and I believe it's time our congressional seats reflected this change. I have strong negotiation and persuasive skills, which have been honed through my work at the government relations office.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

The main function of my current role is policy analysis, which requires strong critical thinking skills. It's my job responsibility to review legislation that may affect the university system, ask our campus stakeholders for feedback, and compile their comments into an accessible fact sheets for legislators and lobbyists. I am able to take complex issues and boil them down to quick, accessible talking points while using policy analysis and research to strengthen my argument.
