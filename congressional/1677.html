Statement

I want to serve on the commission to be a part of the effort that allows our state to draw congressional lines to ensure that all of our residents have their voices represented within their districts. Specifically, I think it is very important that communities of color, as well as rural communities, can have a seat at the table where issues specific to their communities are part of the policy discussion. This is important work that is not partisan but focused on ensuring the values of all of our state residents are considered in how we are governed. I want to work to create congressional districts that support more just and equitable congressional districting that also reflects how we are governed across the state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In my last corporate effort, I ran a line of business that had evolved through a series of acquisitions. As a result of these acquisitions the teams were combined but not developed so there were no true norms and the efforts were based on how large the customer base each of the teams were responsible for, which meant smaller acquisitions felt as though they had no power and the best talent from these teams rarely stayed with the organization. When I was hired, I had to build the team that would support the full business. This required me to learn all of the solutions, identify all of the competencies needed, and learn the strengths and developmental areas of each of the team members. Since this work couldn’t be done in a vacuum, I had to find team leaders that could work across the whole and not focus on saving their own members. Before starting this work, I did a team building exercise where I asked each team member to tell me the concerns they had with the team as a whole. We ananomatized this information and used it to share with the leadership team as a whole how the organization was feeling as a whole and asked them to help me figure out the best way to restructure the organization demonstrating grace and operating with empathy. I implemented co-partners for each function that had to learn from each other and jointly determine the team competencies and structure. We development team values and operating normals and published them--we reiterated them at the beginning of all of our operational reviews. We forced discussions to be rooted in data and facts - but allowed the use of anecdotes to zero in on a particularly difficult issue. What I believed helped the team function fairly was that requirement that we had to had pros and cons to the particularly difficult issues and intentional/disciplined decision making to solve problems. While it wasn’t perfect, it allowed us to work through the difficulties of building a real team quickly and allowed us to support our customers in a way that nearly doubled our Net Promoter Score. The combination of my engineering and legal analytical skills enable me to think differently than most and to solve complex problems that anticipate many unintended consequences and build better solutions.
