Professional Background

Dyslexia - Did a 22-minute TEDx speech advocating for the rights of dyslexic kids (7/6/16, entitled: "Remove your mask of disability"), also worked on the Outreach team while I was on the Board of The Liberty School, which raised money to eventually build a permanent school. Today, The Liberty School is one of the best schools in the country for dyslexic and gifted kids. In my past, I also worked as part of a team to organize minority community bone marrow drives throughout northern California (one of my relatives was dying from leukemia, and we realized that the bone marrow registry for hispanics was a fraction of the size of the general market bone marrow registry, which meant that his prognosis wasn't good). Founded and served as first president of a 10+ member hiking and investing club for women in Silicon Valley that lasted for years until it disbanded in the early 2000's. Purpose was to educate women through monthly meetings about researching and buying stocks. Former president of Durango Earlybird Toastmasters. From '11 through '14, I advocated for the right to do short term rentals. I failed, but after months of door-to-door canvasing and adding more owners to my short term rental tent, we did the unthinkable: got a majority of owners to agree to a proposal, which was then killed by the City of Durango. More recently, I won a traffic appeal, which almost no one ever does. I felt compelled to fight this racial profiling case in court.

Past Political Activity

I attend Southwest Republican Women events, but have heretofore not given money or significant time to the cause. This year, however, I made three videos to be distributed via social media explaining my support for the Constitution 1) a rap, for young people, 2) in Spanish, for Hispanic voters, and 3) 45 minute video in English, explaining why I walked away from the Democrat party.
