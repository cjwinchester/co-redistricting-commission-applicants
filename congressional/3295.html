Statement on Working with Consensus

Servant leadership involves a leadership style promoting community needs above your own, including members of your team/committee. Acknowledging perspectives of the team, involving them in decisions, and supporting them where needed specifies how I choose to work with committees. The application of these ideals, I believe, leads to innovation, trust and higher engagement. For instance, with redistricting, the root is to ensure fair representation for all Coloradans, but the main issue may be geographic distribution of population. Listening to all Coloradans from the Western Slope to the Foothills, to Ski Country, to Farm and Ranch country is necessary. Distilling those voices into a resolution will involve really hearing the input and managing the information so all know they were heard even if not all ideas are integrated. Explaining the underlying goals and methods to achieve them should allow those who were heard, but not incorporated, to feel confident in the outcome because they were part of the process. Promoting consensus begins with finding a uniform goal, which is outlined in the legislation that created the redistricting committee process. Building from there, the committees can find processes suitable to all for the collection of information, coordination of the information gathered, and analyzing what is collected. Everyone has an equal voice and must be afforded respect while presenting their positions, this is where listening skills are highly regarded. When we hold the goal as the purpose for the group, not any one memberâ€™s personal desired outcome, success shall be achieved. As my listening skills have improved, I have found that my persuasive skills have also improved. People want to be heard. When you truly listen, they know. You learn from them because you have listened and heard, and so you acknowledge what you have learned and how that information fits into the processes previously outlined. Buy in by members is what leads to consensus. Assent is possible when members can trust you to have listened, heard, and acknowledged them. This occurs when the processes permit, even promote, sound discussions in which all members participate fully and are heard equally and equitably. I believe that my skills described above, especially listening, and adherence to the goal of fair, inclusive, and competent redistricting is why I shall remain fair and impartial.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Research is the basis upon which critical thinking and analytical skills can produce positive results, and I possess critical research skills and excellent reading, writing and listening skills, which support analytical thinking. Often, identifying the problem is challenging, and this is where listening skills are invaluable. There may be multiple perspectives of a problem, including that there is no problem. Once a problem is identified, gathering information about the source of the problem leads to creative solutions as the root cause is addressed rather than merely a symptom. Next, one must put the solutions into writing in a clear, concise format easily understood by the audience for whom it is intended. Such writing skills will persuade committee members and communities at large of the benefits of the solution and create consensus for the solution. I am confident my skills in analysis, listening, writing, persuasion, problem solving and being generally respectful of others will greatly benefit the committee if I am selected.
