Statement

Two weeks ago, I probably would not have taken the plunge to volunteer for this, but right now, I feel like our political system/s and framework are vulnerable to chaos, distrust, and ambiguity. The only way I can see to either change my perception or begin to be part of the solution to fix whatever might be wrong is to get involved. Then I saw your emails looking for volunteers. I've hesitated to apply for the past few days, but today it feels like the right thing to do.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Since I'm not exactly sure where or how conflicts will arise, I don't think there's a specific answer to this request. What I can say is that I promise I won't yell; I won't throw a temper tantrum; I have friends of all persuasions, so I don't have premeditated 'taboos' assigned to anyone of any persuasion; I refer, first and foremost, to data and written word/regulation/requirement; I have 2 kids (girl age 17, boy age 12) and neither think that I'm fair when I settle a dispute between them, so I figure I'm on the right track; and I frequently refer to the poem by Edwin Markham, 'There is a destiny that makes us brothers. No one walks his way alone, for all that we send into the lives of others, comes back into our own.' I like that poem; it's true.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Again, I'm a data-head and a reg-head. I wallow around in mounds of data and figure out what to do with it and what it means; then I write the report/s so that someone else that isn't a data-head or reg-head can understand it. I write; I've written many, many documents. Some I actually know how to construct from the beginning, and some I don't, but even if I don't, I find the way and I've not missed a deadline yet. The hardest part of any project is just getting it started, so whether the first phases of any project end up scratched or not, just getting started is a victory in-and-of itself. And lastly, I know (oh, so well) the 80/20 rule, and it's real. We will spend 80% of our time, energy, and effort to finish and perfect the last 20% of the details of this project. This is logic I am quite certain of.
