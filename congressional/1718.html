Statement

As you can likely tell from my previous answers, I am new to civic engagement. When I was younger I was always interested in and followed politics but due to frequent moves and other major life events just didn't have time to dedicate to volunteering or activism in any significant way. However, due to the current political environment and the birth of my child, I feel a greater call to personally get involved in shaping the future of our country for the better. I know that redistricting is a crucial to ensuring equal representation in our government, but I worry that it perhaps isn't as exciting as other opportunities for people to get involved leaving a vacuum of passionate people. I want to bring my passion for government and politics to this project and use my enthusiasm to make a difference in the future of Colorado and the country.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I was raised as a conservative, and my parents are still staunch conservatives while my husband and his family is very liberal, so on a personal level, I am used to straddling the line of impartiality often. I consider myself to be a true moderate. I see value in the ideas that both parties have and truly believe that most people regardless of party really do want the best for our country. I really enjoy debating the nuance of policy and while I may defend my position passionately, I feel that at the end of the day coming away with a compromise that everyone feels is fair is far more important than winning. I think that bringing my personal experience and philosophy to the commission would be a great asset.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

For me as an artist, my work is all about problem solving. How do you take a reality and recreate it to a two dimensional form? How do you synthesize information into an easily digestible format? To do this you have to gather all the relevant information (reference photos for my work, census data for mapping). Then analyzing this information, which for me involves taking the whole and breaking it down into smaller manageable pieces and considering it from all angles. Then from that analysis you form your conclusions and that information to build the final product. I think my experience as an artist has a lot of overlap with and would be a useful asset to the redistricting project.
