Statement

I believe the legitimacy, integrity, and effectiveness of our democratic institutions require honesty and transparency at the most foundational levels. I believe that districting is one of those levels that influences not only the ability to measure and respond to the true 'voice of the people', but will also restore and promote confidence in representative processes. I believe that when people believe their voice and vote matter, they are more likely to participate in representative government to the improvement and betterment of all.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I try to be both an independent thinker as well as a team player. I believe that constructive communication can happen in all situations. Constructive disagreement can lead to creative and often better results. Divergent perspectives can not only enrich a process, but may be necessary in order to reach its greatest potential. I value listening over speaking, and really try to not only consider, but understand another's point of view. I try to sense, or "hear" the essence or foundation of another's ideas, and will often try to work together to creatively address or incorporate other's views into the resolution or outcome. If the ingredients and processes are sound, so will be the outcome, whatever it may be.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I seem to possess analytical, logical and reasoning skills in abundance (to the frequent dismay of my wife.) I am often able to evaluate, organize and sequence apparently random situations and processes. I have been told that I am effective at explaining / teaching complex concepts in a way that is simple and meaningful. To the extent this is true, it is because I seem to be able to reduce situations, ideas, or arguments to their most basic components. I like to see not only what works, but to understand why it works. Or doesn't. I seek to expand my own views by thoughtful and reflective consideration, and by remaining open to my own limitations and places of narrow thinking. I start with the assumption that a positive outcome is possible, and the challenge is to find the path that leads to success, while remaining open to new ideas and information along the way. We might not end up where we thought we wanted to be, and if so, it will be because we came to an even better place.
