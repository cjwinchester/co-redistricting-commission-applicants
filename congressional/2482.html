Statement

I believe that with my extensive life experiences, as well as a 24 year military career, I can offer the commission a broad, well-rounded perspective. I have lived through and witnessed several state-wide redistricting efforts, both in Colorado and other states. The pluses and minuses that I witnessed will serve well in providing a balanced perspective. I hope that I would be able to ensure the interests of the voters at large, and not a particular political party, are served by the commission’s work.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe that I can promote consensus and contribute to creating agreements because I will listen to all inputs from the various organizations having a vested interest. I will carefully weigh the merits of each, and examine how the merits presented by one group could impact other groups, both positively and negatively. From these often divergent inputs, I will work toward building an approach that ensures all groups “have their day in court”, but the decisions will consider the voter needs as utmost.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a trained, experienced engineer, well versed in applying logic and reason to solve complex problems. The work of the commission will involve examining a number of conflicting viewpoints, diverse demographic factors, competing goals/desires of special interest groups, all with the goal of achieving a redistricting plan that addresses voter, not political party, desires. As an engineering, I was often called upon to examine a range of factors and data points, some were fixed givens, but often I was forced to take into account conflicting perspectives or desired outcomes. Ultimately, my teams and I were able to arrive at solutions that satisfied the majority of “stakeholders’, while still considering fixed factors such as cost, schedule, and technology. I am also a retired military officer. During my career, I was often called upon to make tough decisions, and issue commands that may not always have been completely understood, or agreed upon. I am not a stranger to making the “tough calls.”
