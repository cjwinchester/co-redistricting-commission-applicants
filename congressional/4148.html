Statement on Working with Consensus

Throughout my career with the USDA Forest Service I was involved with the search for consensus between diverse groups with a passionate interest in the management of the nation’s public lands. As a land management agency with a multiple use mandate, the Forest Service requires of its managers and specialists an ability to listen respectfully to colleagues and the public, and to incorporate, given current laws, regulations and budgets, multiple management objectives for the National Forests and Grasslands. I believe this background has given me the ability to listen actively to others and to find ways to compromise.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As an economist, I am familiar with the analytical tools and datasets of social science. I have relied on both quantitative analysis and qualitative description to illuminate disparate sides of issues in order to form a common information framework to support discussion and decision making. My work as a regional economist relied on data drawn from many US government sources such as the U.S. Census Bureau’s American Communities survey, the Bureau of Economic Analysis, Bureau of Labor Statistics, as well as state data sources such as the Colorado State Demographer. I have used ESRI products for many years. For an example of my work, please see: https://usfs.maps.arcgis.com/apps/MapJournal/index.html?appid=325d69db77e44c0fbe99140439793b49
