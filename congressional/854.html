Application for Congressional Commission for Lori Smith Schell

Contact Information

Full Name

Lori Smith Schell

Party Affiliation

Occupation

I am a self-employed energy economist based in Durango, doing business as Empowered Energy (www.EmpoweredEnergy.com) since August 2002. As seen in my attached resume, I specialize in the markets for renewables, natural gas, power, and emissions. I am a "numbers guy" who has built my professional reputation on providing objective, unbiased analyses. I perform independent, detailed economic analyses of emerging technologies and energy markets in support of the most efficient and least cost transformation of the electric grid. My endgame is to arm decision makers with objective, defensible numbers they can rely on to make well-informed policy. I will manage my project-based workload to make the significant time commitment necessary to serve on this commission.

Educational Background

I hold a Ph.D. in Mineral Economics and Operations Research from The Pennsylvania State University. My bachelor's degree is in Economics (Honors) from The University of Washington. I am an Energy Risk Professional ("ERP"), certified by the Global Association of Risk Professionals ("GARP"). I am currently preparing to take the exam for GARP's certificate in Sustainability and Climate Risk ("SCR").

Past Political Activity

I ran for a position on the deeply divided La Plata Electric Association ("LPEA") Board of Directors in 2016 "to encourage real dialogue to find areas of agreement or compromise" but was defeated. Statement

I am a non-partisan, unaffiliated voter, deeply troubled by the growing political divisions in our local, state, and national communities. I tend to analyze issues with a critical eye, which has limited my ability to "take sides" and allowed me to try to build bridges between competing interests. The perverse impacts on our democratic institutions brought about by the distorted results of gerrymandering is of great concern to me. I would bring a balanced outlook and deeply analytical mind in service to this commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My natural tendency is to see both sides of an issue, one reason that I have never been willing to affiliate with a political party. I try to reconcile different viewpoints and build consensus by being sensitive to group dynamics; I strive to ensure that each individual has a chance to be heard and feel valued in a group setting. I found this practice particularly important as President of the U.S. Association for Energy Economics and would apply the same principles as a commissioner. I have no hidden agenda or axe to grind, which I believe will enable me to be fair and impartial. My objective is to advocate for redrawing voting district lines that would strengthen democratic representation to the benefit of all Coloradans.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a consummate "numbers guy" who has applied my analytical operations research training to projects throughout my nearly 35-year career. I am highly computer literate, fluent in Microsoft Excel and Access, and providing economics-related coding in MATLAB. My early quantification of rooftop solar photovoltaic attributes is widely attributed as moving the California Public Utility Commission forward in providing $2.1 billion in incentives under the California Solar Initiative. My past expert witness work has required me to organize, analyze, and draw conclusions from huge transaction-related data sets covering multiple years. I am not intimidated by challenging problems, particularly when the results are as important as those addressed by this commission.
