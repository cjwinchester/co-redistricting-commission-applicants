Background

Past Political Activity

I am a lifelong Democrat. In 2016, I served as an election observer for the Democratic Party during the general election as well as working the party's voter information hotline in the weeks prior to the election. My wife and I have also hosted various fundraisers for candidates for national, state, and local office. According to the FEC website I contributed $550 in two gifts to Warren for President in the 2020 primary cycle and other gifts to ActBlue totaling $375 since 2015.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

o Chair, American Bar Association Criminal Justice Standards Committee (Effective 2021) o Chair, American Bar Association Task Force on Marijuana Enforcement (2018-present) o Member, Denver City Council Social Consumption Area Task Force (2018-present) o Member, City of Denver Social Consumption Advisory Committee (2017-2018) o Member, ABA Criminal Justice Standards Committee (2012-present) o Member, Legal Panel of the ACLU of Colorado (2004-2018) (for a complete list please see attached curriculum vitae)

Organization and Advocacy Experience

I have been a member of the ACLU of Colorado since moving to Colorado in 1999. I have served both on its board of directors and legal panel, helping the organization choose which cases to take on. I have also worked to abolish the death penalty in the state, testifying a number of times before both chambers of the legislature, most recently in support of the successful 2020 repeal bill. I have written or co-written amicus briefs in a number of cases before both state and federal courts.

Statement

No issue is more important today that restoring America's faith in its electoral system. Non-partisan redistricting commissions such as this one play an important role in that process. Colorado is a model of openness and inclusivity in its electoral processes and I would like to be a part of that ongoing endeavor.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have been appointed to a number of boards and commissions over the last decade and have had to develop consensus -- both as a member and as chair -- on controversial issues of public policy. I have worked to forge agreements between prosecutors and defense attorneys on the ABA Criminal Justice Standards Committee and between opponents and advocates of marijuana legalization on state and local regulatory boards. I have seen first-hand that a willingness to consider others' views in good faith is crucial to reaching lasting agreement. I would bring that open-minded approach to the Commission's difficult work.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have an empirical, quantitative background, having majored in physics as an undergraduate and done statistical research both as a graduate student and a professor. While I am not an election law expert, I am a constitutional law scholar and able to grasp and help others understand the complicated legal rules that govern Congressional apportionment. I am also an educator, with more than two decades of experience helping students master new and difficult legal concepts.
