Professional Background

In my current position as an Assistant Director of Advising at the University of Colorado Boulder, I support students and advisors, advocating for their interests to administrators and faculty. The organizing I do through my work takes into account the many identities of students who come to the University of Colorado from across the state, including the context of each individual. As an advisor and supervisor, I am also highly trained to understand the various groups of students and staff and the unique needs of those groups. As the University of Colorado Boulder is a public institution, the students we serve are representative of the state population to some extent, and my staff have needs and interests that I represent to those in Human Resources and Administration.

Statement

As someone who advocates and organizes in my daily work, I could be an asset to the commission, utilizing problem solving, policy analysis, and strategic thinking skills that come naturally to me. I live in Colorado with my husband and two children. I want to ensure that Colorado remains an incredible place for all people to live.

Statement on Working with Consensus

One of the challenges I deal with at my work with the University of Colorado Boulder is calming angry and frustrated students and their families. This anger or frustration can come from misunderstanding the policies of the University or finding them unfair or inequitable. Therefore, I have become adept at working with students and families to come to a suitable resolution or simply to thoroughly explain the options and rules. I have an obligation to apply the University and College policies equitably to all students, assisting me in developing my judgement on what is fair. Because of these experiences in my daily work, as a commissioner, I would work toward consensus with the group, while remaining impartial.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a strategic problem solver, I often think several steps ahead when analyzing options for a potential solution. This allows me to see in advance the pros and cons of a particular option and present those to others. In my leadership role at the University of Colorado Boulder, I am able to offer unique perspectives and solutions to challenges we face because of this ability to see many options. This ability is my primary strength in my work, and I would welcome the opportunity to use it for this commission process.
