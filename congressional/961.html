Background

Past Political Activity

Volunteered on the campaign of local School Board candidate, and worked as a General Election Warehouse Judge with the Pueblo County Clerk's office.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

NAACP Pueblo Branch, MLK Heritage Center & Museum Board, Juneteenth Org. of Pueblo, El Pomar Foundation Emerging Leaders, YWCA Pueblo, Pikes Peak Baptist District, General Missionary Baptist Conference, National Missionary Baptist Fellowship of Churches.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

Under my leadership, I have planned, organized many events, to include, the annual MLK Jr. March, Program & Luncheon, NAACP Freedom Fund Banquet, Financial Empowerment seminars, Justice rallies, various forums/discussions, to include School Safety, Conversation with Law Enforcement. I have advocated for education reform within District 60, supporting the Teacher's strike, the underserved communities, organized food/clothing drives for the homeless, and conducted a school supply drive for five of the Title 1 elementary schools in the Pueblo City School District 60. I have planned various events, to include, Anniversary programs, Black History programs, and Veteran's Day programs in my capacity of a community activist.

Statement

I want to be a part of creating a nonpartisan, fair, honest system of representation of all people of Colorado, and be the voice of Southern Colorado's diverse population. To ensure interest of the people are paramount and inclusion is evident. I understand the importance of this work and am willing to work hard to ensure proper boundaries throughout the state are correctly divided proportionally to represent our diversity and interest.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I'm a component of working with all people for the betterment of the whole. I feel everyone is valuable and have something to bring to the table in accomplishing the final goal. I believe in being informed to make the best decisions. I have had to reach out to individuals, organizations in the Pueblo community in dealing with an array of issues. Most recently, as president of the NAACP, I had to respond to an individual who reached out for assistance from the organization. I conveyed the procedures and what needed to be done on her part, I then discussed the issue with her and made a determination that your issue was one that possibly met the criteria of being a civil rights issue. Once information was received, I contacted the individuals in which she was making the complaint against, on her behalf, to share her concerns and to gather more information to possibly resolve the issue. After going through the process, the issue was resolved to the satisfaction of all parties.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

The way I resolve problems, are to take inventory of the issue being addressed, complete an investigation, gathering facts, and then make a plan to address the issue. Recently, during the Black Lives Matter issue, I planned and organized a Community Justice Rally. I contacted other community leaders, activists, concerned citizens and city and county officials, after brainstorming and collaboration a successful event was realized bringing calm to the community.
