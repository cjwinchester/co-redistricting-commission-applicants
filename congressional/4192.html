Statement

I believe that the commission needs to have diversity of gender, age, and disability, and read that applicants so far are overwhelmingly 65+ males.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I come from a basis of thinking about the people in the community that we have drawn-in together. Are we drawing a community that represents homogeneity, or one intended to be diverse? Each of those has consequences. I will approach consensus in a way that focuses on the community rather than individual ego, and encourage colleagues to do the same.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a person who has been disabled for over 10 years, I make a pittance on Social Security. This means that to live any semblance of a normal life, I have to be able to creatively solve problems, often without resources. This has led me to a way of problem solving that I describe as "Don't imagine the tool you need, imagine the function you need. Then work backward to find a wider variety of tools that could fit the job. You might even find that you could use a different function/tool altogether in the process." As someone trained in journalism, I have also been trained to seek multiple angles. The old adage of three scholars, each feeling the legs, trunk, and flank of an elephant, is not the journalist's way. As annoying as it makes us, we are the ones who will feel around the whole elephant.
