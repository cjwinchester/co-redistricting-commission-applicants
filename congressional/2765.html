Organization and Advocacy Experience

Much of my experience organizing, representing and advocating has been during my time as a Active Duty Army then Dept of Defense civilian physician. Although those efforts were directed to improving patient care in military settings, the skills I learned there will carry over to projects I take on anywhere. As noted above I was a active member of CMTRA for many years. There I coordinated numerous trail improvement projects with the US Forest Service. I also applied for off-highway vehicle fund grants from Colorado Parks and Wildlife for funds to help maintain and create motorized trails.

Statement

Now retired after serving my country and my patients for 39 years I still have a strong desire to serve. Representative government is a core element of our republic. Key to this are representatives elected from a diverse population, politically and economically. Also rural areas must have their voices heard. I would like to serve on the commission to help see these goals of representation achieved.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a physician I have learned to be non-judgmental and have learned the importance of gaining the agreement of my patients in their medical care. Gaining consensus was also required in my leadership positions and many performance improvement initiatives during my career. I feel eliciting input and listening are key skills I have that will help achieve consensus.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a physician I have maintained my fund of knowledge through extensive ongoing reading and learning. I am able to study complex subjects, understand them and coherently present them simply as possible.
