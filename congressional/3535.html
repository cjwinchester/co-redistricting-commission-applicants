Organization and Advocacy Experience

I am currently serving on the Health Equity Coalition for the state of Colorado run through the state public health association (CPHA, see question above). I am a white woman, so can not speak for folks of color, however I can advocate for their community and health needs as I hear them through my work. I am managing a statewide workforce of 20 people who live in regions throughout the state, they are called Regional Health Connectors (RHCs). RHCs work to connect primary care, public health, and community assets together to promote overall health for their communities. They have many local level stories to share about what is needed for their communities, and I am able to use what I hear, bring to a statewide committee and advocate for better health resources for them, ultimately leading to more awareness of issues and community needs, as well as who to reach out to in the community to support.

Statement

Fair maps that greater represent the diversity of Colorado's people, places, and communities are needed to create more equity among communities across Colorado. More voices need to be heard and represented in the Congressional districts, and I can bring forth my own knowledge of what is needed in diverse communities across Colorado because of my work at a state level. Further, this will give me an opportunity to use my knowledge of geography and allocation of health districts in the state, aggregated by statistical and demographic data.
