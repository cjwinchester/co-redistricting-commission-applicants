Statement

I believe that there is a responsibility of people to see that the rural population and the urban population both have equal representation to the state and that an imbalance of jerrymandering and redistricting based on political parties should not be how the state is represented. I believe there should be a fair and impartial balancing of the districts in the state so as to hear the voice of all people of the state and not just that of the powerful political groups.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe that intelligent and reliable information should be obtained by the commission, and that with candor and honesty we can speak freely, yet with respect to one another on issues for the benefit for all Colorado people. There is an old adage that says, "A soft answer turns away wrath, but grievous words stir up anger." I believe that reasonable people can come to a table, and if they truly are looking out for the good of all Colorado voters, they should be able to come up with wise and discerning decisions which will benefit the people of Colorado.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Having been a minister for nearly 50 years, as well as a financial advisor. I have had to maneuver through the courses of family issues, legal and business dilemmas, financial difficulties of business that needed solutions, and apply the most wise decisions to bring about answers that needed to be solved. I also believe that if we lack wisdom, we can as for a higher power to give us a blessing of answers that would not be able to be solved by any other council. If we analyze a problem correctly, there is always a solution which can become acceptable to all parties involved, with reason, if those parties are open to wise council.
