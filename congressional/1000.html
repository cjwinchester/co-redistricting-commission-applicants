Background

Professional Background

It is my life's work to advocate for the oppressed. Currently I do so in my role at the Laboratory to Combat Human Trafficking(LCHT) for survivors of exploitation as well as in my role on the board of directors for the child advocacy center. In my role at LCHT, I have spent a great deal of time traveling around the state educating and organizing anti-trafficking task forces and coalitions, there are currently 18 in Colorado. I recently hosted a convening of the leadership of all 18 of these task forces/coalitions and will continue to do so quarterly moving forward. In my position at LCHT I have also received a sub-grantee award from the Colorado Dept. of Human Services to consult on a project that supports anti-trafficking advocates in rural and frontier regions across Colorado. I am also a professor at Metropolitan State University, teaching students about human trafficking and oppression that leads to exploitation.

I've never donated money to a campaign. I am an active and informed voter.

Statement

I believe in democracy. I have spent about a decade working in countries in Africa, the Middle East and Asia. I've been lucky enough to be present when citizens of Iraq were able to vote for the first time in decades (for example). I do not take our right to vote for granted and believe it is our most important right in the US. I want our elections to be fair and I want the people of Colorado to be represented in a way that is just and not partisan.

Statement on Working with Consensus

It is my job to bring people together. Anti-trafficking work requires collaboration, and collaboration requires trust. I value integrity and truth and lead by example. Additionally, I am empathetic and an active listener, always willing to grow and learn from others. I look forward to this opportunity.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

At my core, I believe in research and evidence-informed action to solve problems. I will always rely on data (if it was obtained legitimately and ethically) that was conducted in a methodological way to inform my decisions. But even when it comes to data, sometimes research can be biased. I will always ask, who is at the table and more importantly, who is not. Representation should be inclusive and intentional.
