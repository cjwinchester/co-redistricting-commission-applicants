Statement

In creating the independent commissions in 2018, Colorado voters made clear their views to reform the process by which state redistricting maps were historically drawn. This was a bold and innovative step toward a more transparent and accountable process, guided by public engagement. After a career in public service at the national level, the inaugural congressional commission offers an exciting opportunity for meaningful public service in Colorado; I would be pleased to help turn the transformative vision of Colorado voters into a reality for the future.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a senior diplomat representing U.S. interests around the world, I have worked as a catalyst with allies and adversaries alike toward constructive changes in areas such as human rights, political reform, global health, and peace and security. I believe that full participation, open exchange of ideas, and constructive advocacy of alternatives are critical to foster a climate of mutual respect and trust, cooperation, and collaboration. Moreover, I believe that fairness, and impartiality -- as well as transparency -- must anchor the foundation of any goal-oriented endeavor promulgated in the public interest.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

A clerkship nearly forty years ago to the supreme court chambers of one of Colorado's most eminent jurists was my most memorable exposure to rigorous analysis. My professional experiences since then in advocacy and negotiation as a lawyer and diplomat have underscored the necessity of critical, analytical thinking to solve problems and resolve seemingly intractable policy issues. In my view, three complementary principles are critical to any challenge: integrate a wide range of information and experiences to inform thinking, recognize where information or analysis is incomplete, and define complex issues in terms which permit them to be dealt with in a practical way.
