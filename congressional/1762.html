Making this redistricting a better place where the district represents the communities.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My understanding of Consensus building is that it is a conflict-resolution process used mainly to settle complex, multiparty issues. Consensus decision making is a creative and dynamic way of reaching agreement between all members of a group. In place of simply voting for an item and having most of the group getting their way, a group using consensus is committed to finding solutions that everyone can actively supports, or at least can live with. This ensures that all opinions, ideas and concerns are considered. By actively soliciting all communities of interest, LISTENING and educating these communities and by being educated by these communities of interest, through this interactive process and listening closely to each other a redistricting proposal can be formulated that work for everyone. I bring no agenda to this process; I am willing to listen, make every effort to be fair, ensure that all opinions, ideas and concerns are taken into account and work hard at finding solutions that address everyone's concerns rather than ignoring or overruling others opinions.
