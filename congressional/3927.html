List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

I am a mentor with Big Brothers Big Sisters of Colorado, and have done that since 2014. I'm on the board of Colorado's State Internet Portal Authority (SIPA). I served on the board of Colorado HealthOP, Colorado's cooperative health insurance provider set up under ACA.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

Serving on the boards of SIPA and Colorado HealthOP has given me a greater appreciation for the difficulty of weighing the goals of different constituencies and making choices that might not be optimal for everyone, but that hopefully do the most good for the most people. Our state must balance large urban and suburban areas of the front range with extremely rural parts of the state, and I've found it incredibly useful to actually get to know individuals impacted by decisions to help inform those decisions.

Statement

I think it's important for preserving our way of life that our representatives actually represent the people of Colorado. Obviously there are tradeoffs when drawing any map, but we should attempt to do so in a way that disenfranchises the fewest people and gives Coloradans a voice in the people who will serve our state and country. This is critically important work and it's important to have people who are knowledgable, willing to listen to others' points of view, and come to a consensus.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I think it's critically important to work hard to find common ground, especially in this deeply divided political environment. I will get to know my fellow commissioners and work hard to understand everyone's motivations and interests. There are very real tradeoffs in any map, and acknowledging those and seeking to work to give the most Coloradans a voice as possible is my goal. Regarding my ability to be fair and impartial, I do my best to evaluate the facts as they exist, understand and evaluate complex tradeoffs, and argue for the fairest decision. Ultimately this process is not about providing advantage to one party over another. It's about giving Coloradans of all political stripes the ability to make their voice heard in choosing their representatives. When you serve in the military, you don't do so for the benefit of any political party. You serve for the country because ultimately we are stronger together than we can ever be apart. Finally, from 2016-2018 I worked as a Team Lead and Product Manager at 18F, an organization that helps federal agencies build more user-friendly software. I was a federal government employee across Democratic and Republican administrations and always took the approach that my role was to serve our country, not to act as partisan in any manner. I intend to take this same approach in serving on the Commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have a strong finance background having passed all three levels of the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) exam and have worked in product development and engineering since getting out of the Navy. I've also served on two Colorado statewide boards in SIPA and Colorado HealthOP, and I bring varied experience that will help me to quickly understand the complexities and make recommendations that can help us solve them together.
