Background

Past Political Activity

My past political activity has been centered around nonpartisan voter registration and civic engagement. I have not donated to any political campaigns in the past 5 years.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

Before studying Traditional East Asian Medicine, I had extensive experience in community organizing including directing membership and communications for two nonpartisan statewide coalitions that helped constituents learn how to participate in local, state, and federal government. As a Traditional East Asian Medicine provider I have been an active participant with my statewide association, serving on the Board of Directors for two years.

Statement

As a female business owner in a rural area, I believe I have a unique voice to offer to the process of redistricting. As a wife and mother of two young daughters I am passionate about doing my civic duty and showing my daughters that democracy functions best when we participate in it. Although I have a very busy practice, I believe it is essential that the Commission reflect the diversity of this state and as a queer, female business owner in a rural area, I believe I have a duty to offer my time to ensure the process is representative of the diversity that makes this state great. I have always had a passion for civic engagement and would love the opportunity to put this passion to work again.

Statement on Working with Consensus

During my work with the statewide coalitions we often encountered issues of building consensus among our members and I played a key role in listening to people’s concerns and finding common ground to build agreement among seemingly oppositional positions. As a medical provider I have extensive experience in remaining impartial when helping patients navigate health problems, whether they are physical or mental. My personal philosophy is that the root of tackling a problem, be it a difference of opinions or a complex health issue, begins with listening. I believe listening to understand is a forgotten skill in our modern culture and that the foundation of building consensus begins with this critical step that subsequently informs further education, discussion, and comprise to reach a common goal.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

From a young age, I have always had a passion for problem solving. As child I always selected pass times that involved problem solving such as doing puzzles, and nearly drove my parents to madness with my version of data collection, which presented as a consistent string of asking, “Why?” I believe it was these traits that ultimately lead me to the medical field where I help people problem solve many times a day. Weather it is referring a patient to the emergency room for an infection they didn’t realize was serious, or helping a patient with a pinched nerve find pain relief through acupuncture, my day is filled with analysis and problem solving. These are skills I am excited to bring to the Colorado Independent Redistricting Commission.
