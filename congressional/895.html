Statement

This commission represents a wonderful opportunity to spotlight Colorado’s commitment to equity in representation. Fair mapping is critical to democracy, and it would be a privilege to bring my unique set of skills to this work. As I have mentioned, I am an active member of the Fort Collins community and choose to do so because the work genuinely enriches my life. I am happiest when I am of service and feel like my work is making a difference in the lives of others. The opportunity to support the efforts to ensure that the people of Colorado are fairly represented for the next decade and be part of the group that lays the groundwork for future commissions would be a privilege.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am highly intuitive and quickly able to read and respond to individual and group dynamics while building trust and holding space for thoughtful and solution-based collaboration. I am an adept communicator, always with the express intent of ensuring that all participants feel valued and heard. My past work as a commissioner for the Human Services Commission for the City of Loveland and my current appointment on the United Way of Larimer County Community Impact Committee have provided ample opportunities to practice collaboration and reaching consensus. Both groups are comprised of community members who come together to determine how best to support the efforts of local human services agencies and not-for-profits, using either state funding or private impact philanthropy. It likely isn’t a surprise that the total requests for funding far outpace the amount of award dollars available in most funding cycles. Thus, the distribution of these awards always requires thoughtful, careful consideration on the part of committee members, and then collaborative and productive conversations to reach consensus on how best to award grants strategically to achieve the best possible outcomes for our community.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am highly organized with a sharp eye for detail and have a variety of past experiences that highlight my analytical skills. I have undertaken multiple projects at work that require strategic planning, collaboration, and project management. I am currently managing a complex fundraising strategy for emerging infectious disease research in the College of Veterinary Medicine and Biomedical Sciences at Colorado State University. This project involves many different stakeholders and collaboration across multiple college and central university units. I am also pursuing a Master’s degree in Organizational Leadership, which offers a significant opportunity to further develop my critical thinking, research, and communication competencies.
