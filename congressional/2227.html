Statement

I truly believe your vote is your power and is your chance to have your voice heard and represented by leaders who share your values and who you can hold accountable. However, voters can be silenced or suppressed in gerrymandered districts where politicians choose their voters instead of voters choosing their representatives. This is an issue that benefits both Democratic and Republican incumbents, undermining democratic representation at the expense of their constituents. In order to have a healthy and functioning democracy in our state, we need to ensure that all Coloradans are fairly represented. I am excited by the prospect of working with other Colorado citizens from different political backgrounds to redraw our Congressional and Legislative District maps so that the voices of all Coloradans are heard and represented.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I understand that not everyone shares my values and agrees with me politically – and that’s OK. I believe the goal of the commission is draw a district map that fairly represents all of our communities, regardless of their political affiliation. If the entire commission can approach this task with the intention of ensuring that the new districts reflect the communities in the region, I am positive that consensus will be reached. Colorado is a big and diverse state, from the Western slopes, to the Eastern plains; we are the proud home to many distinct communities that all deserve to be heard and represented in our state politics. If I were to serve on the commission, I would do my best to ensure that everyone leaves their biases at the door and focuses on serving the interests of all Coloradans. While I personally support progressive tendencies and values, I am open-minded and willing to hear different perspectives and engage in productive dialogue. Additionally, as a professionally trained scientist, I have spent years learning how to approach problems and ideas with a critical eye and evaluate them based on fact rather than emotion. A large component of my job as a scientific researcher is to examine data impartially and extract un-biased truths. Further, I often have to review scientific studies conducted by other research groups and evaluate them based solely on their execution of the scientific method and known literature, placing whatever personal feelings I might have for the individuals involved to the side. I am confident that I will be able to bring the same ability to be fair and impartial to the commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As someone with a STEM Ph.D., I have spent the better part of a decade honing and refining analytical and critical thinking skills. As a biologist, I need to be able to synthesize and understand complex content and consider the big picture of my project while managing details. Rarely do experiments work the first time, so I also need to consider alternative approaches and thus am constantly problem solving. Additionally, sometimes experiments do work but the outcome is not as expected, which requires an open-mind and critical thinking. Essentially, my daily job is built on analytical thinking and problem solving, and I am confident that I will be able to bring these strengths to my role if selected to serve on the commission.
