Organization and Advocacy Experience

Previously served on Board and Executive Committee of Colorado Medical Society. Previously served as Governor of the Colorado Chapter of the American College of Cardiology. Through both organizations, I have made testimony before Colorado State Legislative Committees. I have extensive experience in service to my hospital and hospital system for more than 4 decades.

Statement

My advocacy work through the American Medical Association, the Colorado Medical Society, and the American College of Cardiology has convinced me that issues and the principles exhibited by elected officials are more important that political affiliations. I am aa student of Supreme Court history and history of the Civil Rights Movement. I want to serve so as to advocate for electoral fairness that will encourage all elements of society to exercise their right to vote.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My experience in advocacy and in serving my professional and community organizations has always been non-partisan. My leadership style has always been built upon consensus-building and respecting all opinions. I have extensive experience in leadership as well as being just a committee member. I understand the challenges of allowing all opinions to be be heard. Similarly, I have skill in bringing divergent opinions together.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

The practice of medicine during my career has increasing required the need for analysis of data and the application of data to solving problems. I am comfortable with reading and digesting analytic reviews and synthesizing concepts based on data provided. My service to the AMA/Specialty Society RVS Update Committee (RUC) has enhanced these skills. The RUC is a unique multispecialty committee dedicated to describing the resources required to provide physician services which the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) considers in developing Relative Value Units (RVUs). Working with that environment has taught me much about deliberations, compromises, and successful resolutions.
