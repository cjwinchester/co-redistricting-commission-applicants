Organization and Advocacy Experience

Learning how everything works together - or doesn't work in some cases - was very engaging. At Foothills Animal Shelter I trained new volunteers, participated in the implementation of new programs, and while on the volunteer leadership council identified and made suggestions to improve different processes for animals, staff and volunteers. Being on a trail crew for the City of Lakewood working and sweating side by side teaches more teamwork, and develops an appreciation for the upkeep of the parks. Helping educate park users at Bear Creek Lake Park (Lakewood) by assisting in different programs I have learned to balance both the informational nature side and the upholding of rules side. All of the experiences were worthwhile and educational to me, and were hopefully a positive experience for the other participants as well.

Statement

I feel it is very important to have transparent and equal representation in our political process. If people believe their opinion matters, I think more people will listen and participate in the process. Equal participation is the key to understanding what a community needs. I am proud of Colorado for this important step and would like to be a part of this process.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Pausing to understand why someone holds the opinion they do is vital to long-term success. Questions and conversation are key, not uncompromising black and white statements. In my opinion, our system works best when all sides are heard and we can agree to meet in the middle. I have experience in being the person that listens to party A and then translates/explains to party B and vice versa.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

A large part of my job is problem solving in a variety of ways: insurance appeals, working with patients on understanding the suggested treatment, managing the associated cost, explaining insurance limitations, preparing and reinforcing reasonable expectations, etc. I expect applying these day to day skills to this situation would look like this: Having a set goal to focus on and reorient to is very helpful when wading into a problem. Recognizing emotion in an issue and addressing it in a constructive manner is a good first step to reaching the ability to be logical and thus reaching said goal. Obviously, some subjects are more hazardous than others. Acknowledging the root cause for the emotion, and then appealing to logic in a fair and measured manner tends to be a successful path. Of course involved parties have to want a resolution, if one is not willing to participate in the process, a solution will be unlikely. This process can take time as people need to be able to walk away with parts of a conversation, process it and return to the table for further discussion.
