Statement

In the work that I do, it is imperative that voices of every community are considered in the decision making of our legislators. Equity among those being represented can only be assured if the districts in which they live are are fairly represented through responsible division of districts in the state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Much social justice work requires active listening, weighing information without assumption or bias, and serving as a community liaison who is able to productively compromise with those whose perspectives may differ from mine. I will employ the skills necessary to support equitable solutions in decision making on the Commission. I believe that the ability to be fair and impartial is a skill I've fine-tuned over the years. Equity can only be attained if all voices are heard and respected.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

In the role of Executive Director--a role I've held for 11 years--the ability to think analytically and apply logic and reason is vital to success. The current pandemic has most recently tested my skills in a situation not experienced before. Health Department information was received, analyzed, applied to our particular staff/client/agency needs, and carefully adopted to assure minimal risk to employees and clients. The Emergency Shelter is an essential business, which had to remain in operation to keep victims safe. We adhered to all precautions, reduced the number of staff onsite at any one time, asked residents to limit interactions outside the shelter, reassessed each resident as they returned to shelter, asked residents to minimize interaction with one another, limited number of residents admitted, established quarantine rooms, and carefully oversaw the use of handwashing, social distancing, and mask wearing while residents were in common areas. Our public office, on the other hand, provides services that can be done remotely, so advocates and administrators at that site were ask to work from home. We had lifted some of the restrictions in the public office, as recommendations suggested. With the rise of COVID-19 numbers, we've reduced staff in the public office to 25% and will close it completely if any staff test positive. During this time, analysis, logic, and reason prevail in maintaining safety.
