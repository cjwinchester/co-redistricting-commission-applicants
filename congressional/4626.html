Statement

As a life long native of Colorado who grew up on the Western Slope and has lived in the Denver area for the last 20 years, I bring a understanding, from lived experience, of all the varying perspectives of Colorado citizens. I want to serve on this commission because I am a fair minded and highly principled person who is interested in participating in the process to ensure that a fair and balanced perspective is brought to the process so that we can draw a map to represent all the citizens of Colorado fairly and equitably. Colorado has been my home for almost 38 years and I want to participate in keeping Colorado the great state and great place to live that is has been my whole life.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As someone who would be part of the unaffiliated portion of the commission, I believe I would play a unique role in bringing a balanced perspective from outside the two parties. I would work to create consensus from all the commissioners on a redistricting map that would most fairly represent all the citizens of Colorado, regardless of their voting affiliations. I believe creating consensus in any group of people is about first forming relationships and opening the lines of communication with all members of the group to be able to create a space for honest discussion about the issue at hand. Once all parties are communicating, then I bring facts and data to the table to properly understand the issue and determine the best solution. Being fair and impartial on an issue like this is about being able to understand and empathize with the many different perspectives that Colorado citizens have in order to determine a map configuration that represents everyone fairly. As a person, I am logical and analytical and have no allegiance to either side of the political spectrum. I can bring a lot of fairness to this commission because my only goal is to represent Colorado and the citizens of Colorado well.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am a professional software engineer, which means my job is to apply logic and reason to the process of building, running, and enhancing a distributed computer system with the goal of providing a stable and reliable service for the users of the system. This profession requires daily application of logic and reason to be able to take a wide view of the whole system to diagnose and fix issues whose cause may not be immediately obvious. I also have a lot of experience analyzing data to be able to ask and answer questions about the data so that meaningful and accurate conclusions can be drawn.
