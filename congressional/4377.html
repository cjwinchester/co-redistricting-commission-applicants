Organization and Advocacy Experience

In my experience as a previous County Commissioner and scientist, I have sat on numerous local-regional-state-and federal policy committees and local committees working on watershed improvements. I work to understand the nuances of different interests and why they are interested in certain outcomes over others. I like to work outside of "bubbles" and find common ground to best meet common goals. As a scientist I understand that public policy and political will can depart from the best available science, however, I work to make informed data-driven decisions in line with the commission's given authority. When I was an Ouray County Commissioner, I found we were not in compliance with Commissioner Districts -- they were not roughly equal in population or voters at all and made compliance with state statute in redistricting a priority. I work across party lines, which is in part why I was appointed to be Chair of the National Association of Counties Rural Action Caucus in 2015 by the association president, who was Republican.

Statement

I am a consensus builder and I enjoy diffusing ideology to get to successful outcomes for the greater good. I believe my experience with geospatial data analysis and understanding of districts will be valuable. I advocated to use our voter and population data to have districts that were practical for the clerk to administer, coherent with other districts (schools, fire, library, etc) and population centers. I think it is important to have rural and western slope representation on the state redistricting commission.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I will listen closely to data being presented and to commissioners' statements and rationales. I find that in understanding what and why someone values something, common ground can be identified for building consensus toward common goals. I am a creative problem solver and good at bridging divides and looking for creative solutions that minimize the sense of loss to stakeholders. I will work to put the commission and its charter above personal preference or bias to achieve a fair and practical outcome for the State of Colorado.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am known for my technical skills in data analysis, hypothesis testing, and creative problem-solving. I am experienced in synthesizing large amounts of geospatial data and using analysis tools to turn it into information for informed decisions whether it is to define an environmental contamination plume or create public policy.
