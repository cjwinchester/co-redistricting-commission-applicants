Organization and Advocacy Experience

As a "citizen-activist" for the past 40 years, I work with all kinds of groups to identify and work towards common goals. During my campaign, I interacted with many differsed groups with different political views, and got to know the Latino community and leaders. So, I believe I would make an excellent representative for the Western Slope because I have been door-to-door, in forums, involved with different entities and government leaders the past eight months.

Statement

Because I was in Democratic leadership roles in 1991, 2001, and 2011, I was involved with the old system of redrawing legislative, congressional and county commissioner boundaries. Several cycles, Garfield County was split in two. Since I have lived in Garfield County since 1974, I have institutional knowledge of the different boundaries 3CD has gone through. I feel I would be a strong voice for Western Colorado to make districts more competitive.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Because of my background in redistricting efforts in the past, I would bring this experience to this new commission. I am relieved of the effort by the legislature to make this process more fair and balanced. Because I am retired, I can devote full-time into this redistricting effort. I am familiar with the political landscape of the state.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Again I relate to my previous experience on the campaign trail, I had to be prepared to answer questions about COVID, water, green energy, childcare, etc. And defend my views under cross-examination. I was prepared to talk about a $125 million dollar county budget. In representing the Grand Valley Citizens Alliance, I have given testimony and compromised on issues regarding the COGCC rulemaking. In the "old days" of redistricting, the saying was "If everyone left mad, we did a good job." This time, I hope that participants will leave "glad."
