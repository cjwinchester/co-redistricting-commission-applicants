Statement

I think that it's important that legislative districts be fair and sensible. This helps to ensure that the representation we receive from our elected officials is actually representative of the populous. Additionally having equitable legislative districts can help avoid political polarization by preventing the creation of districts with skewed populations, which might allow for elected officials with more extreme policies.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I recently had a chance to participate in a jury trial, and it offered some insights into building consensus. At the beginning of deliberations we were pretty much evenly split between acquittal and conviction, even though the facts of the case came down on one side. By focusing on our duty and responsibility, though, we were able to focus on the facts and evidence and eventually come to a unanimous decision. I know a big part to how effectively we were able to come to a decision was everyone's willingness to be impartial. All the jurors were willing to acknowledge the evidence, even when it contradicted their initially desired outcome. Because of that we were able to come to a consensus. And for me that enforced the importance of being fair, and being willing to see the other side of the issue. It allowed for all the reservations of jurors to be addressed, and they were willing to be convinced by the evidence.
