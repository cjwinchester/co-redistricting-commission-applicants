Professional Background

My most direct experience comes from the equity and representation advocacy work that I am helping lead at the K-12 school where I work. The school is currently working through a multi-year equity and oppression audit, and my role has been to help design and deliver professional development for teachers that is aimed at dismantling the oppression of students by race, class, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, citizenship status, etc. My work involves considering the wider body of academic knowledge related to equity and inequity in public education, and helping teachers develop their own senses of equity literacy and building inclusive, anti-oppressive learning communities, environments, and curriculum.

Statement

My own academic history in mathematics and statistics, and the data literacy and statistical numeracy that I possess would be an invaluable tool to this commission that aims to create independent and fair redistricting processes and maps for the state of Colorado. Further, I'm interested in addressing the incredible population growth and demographic changes that have taken place all over the state (not just on the Front Range, but on the Western Slope, in mountain towns, and in southern Colorado's and eastern Colorado's plains communities. There has been such change in the makeup and sizes of our communities, that it's incredibly important to be able to address the diversity of our state's population, in terms of place, race, class, sex, gender identity, and so many other demographic factors.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As an educator in public schools, my day-to-day existence is characterized by being able to guide a classroom of diverse teenagers, with unique and particular home lives, guardians' views, and cultural knowledge, to a class consensus in order to move the group forward in their scientific understanding. As a teacher of environmental science, too, I am well-practiced at facilitating conversations and guiding a diverse group's analysis of this scientific field that is still considered "controversial" in many social circles, despite the overwhelming scientific consensus. There are lots of personal, cultural, and economic identities tied up in the issue of the climate crisis that are present in the student population that I work with, and my experience and leadership in that environment demonstrates a commitment to data and evidence that bolsters my ability to be impartial and not to get wrapped up in emotional or socio-psychological concerns that are present around political issues.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Please refer to my years of postsecondary education in the fields of math, statistics, science, and data aggregation and analysis (via work with climate data during graduate research).
