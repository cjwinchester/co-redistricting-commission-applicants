Statement on Working with Consensus

The highest likelihood of achieving consensus comes from acknowledging that we all have inherent biases, that the people who are not from your political affiliation have legitimate interests that may oppose your own legitimate interests and that all political processes are driven by compromise. Compromises are attained through negotiation, and the way to "win" a negotiation is to maximize the value that each side receives for its concessions. I believe that the process of voting is sacrosanct to a democracy. In much the same way that if you don't believe in freedom of speech for those who oppose you, then you don't actually believe in freedom of speech, if you believe that those who oppose you should be denied their vote (or have their vote wasted), then you do not believe in democracy. The objective of redistricting is to represent all people, and to disenfranchise someone for any reason would be a personal ethical transgression against my firm belief in democracy.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a professional software engineer and manager over the last 24 years, I have spent more time analyzing and solving problems, both technical and interpersonal, than any other activity in my waking life. The ability to apply technical analysis to information is central to my career. The drawing of boundaries can be thought of as a trade study, where each competing interest, whether it be protected classes under the Voting Rights Act, existing community boundaries or any others, can be evaluated against the proposed boundaries then traded against each other to create the optimal benefit, while meeting all required mandatory criteria. Such a system would likely not result in a singe plan being decided upon, but it allows the speedy elimination of inappropriate plans and identifies the relative merits of good plans, allowing further discussion to center on those areas.
