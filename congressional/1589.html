In addition I instituted or enhanced strong parental & community involvement & decision-making groups in these schools. While I have been retired for many years, I still hold a passion for community empowerment in government/education decisions that have most impact on quality of life.

Statement

My recent experience with "Getting Out the Vote" within HD7 has confirmed for me that there are many voices that are empowered when appropriately represented as citizens especially through the election regulations and process. I believe they are more likely to follow the local, state and national government processes and decisions as well as to vote.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a community member on the Commission, I would aim to look for bridges where we have issues, needs & hopes in common. I believe that I readily serve as a facilitator within my large family, as a volunteer within my church, and as a volunteer within formal meetings such as caucuses or formal community groups. This has taken the form of constructive strategy design experiences regarding addressing general issues of Latinos in our state.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

For my PhD dissertation, I collected, aggregated and analyzed 10 years of employment data within Colorado public schools and drew conclusions based on patterns that I derived. In my teacher training experience, I taught K-12 teachers and teacher aides how to understand and teach higher order thinking. I enjoy analyzing complex information to look for trends, especially over time using open questions to interpret the data in helpful ways. I would also use active listening in order to determine qualitative conclusions derived from the group. As a community member of the Commission I would respect the information & preliminary conclusions of the experts, yet draw on my knowledge & experience of living most of my life in Park Hill & Central Park neighborhoods, fully aware of the dynamic nature of neighborhoods.
