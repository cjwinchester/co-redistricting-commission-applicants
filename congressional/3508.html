Organization and Advocacy Experience

Recently, as the CEO of an investment platform for a Denver-based family office, I led the development of a proposed project to supply drinking water to certain communities in Northern Colorado. The effort required a great deal of work with state, municipal, and private actors, all of them potential stakeholders in the project. And, because it was, after all, a water supply project, it was very important to meet everyone's needs. After about 18 months of work, we were forced to shelve the project. However, the experience was typical of similar projects I have been involved in over the course of my career, which always required integrating the interests of multiple stakeholders in complex infrastructure projects.

Statement

The de-politicization of the redistricting process is a crucial and achievable step along the path to bringing more unity to our nation and addressing our polarized politics. This is one of the most important issues facing our country, so an opportunity to participate directly in the process would be both humbling and exciting.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I learned how to listen and take on-board the opinions of others, and to build consensus among stakeholders, from a career that included roles as a company founder and president, as a senior executive in multi-national engineering firms, as a professional staff member in the U.S. Senate, and as the executive in charge of developing energy, water, and infrastructure projects in the U.S and internationally. In all these roles, it was necessary to craft strategies, execute, and deliver projects, policy initiatives, and outcomes that best met the needs of all stakeholders. I learned early that if you don't bring all of the players along, sooner or later you'll face barriers that could have been avoided, and I've been trained in team and consensus building not only by experience, but also in more formal corporate training and third-party specialists. Regarding fairness and impartiality, it is clear that whether the issue is mail-in voting, or the impartial drawing of Congressional and legislative maps, Colorado can be an example to the nation in terms of bringing more access and fairness to our political process. The redistricting commissions, by definition, have an important responsibility to execute this work transparently and impartially, so that all stakeholders can respect the outcome.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have worked as a corporate executive for global engineering firms, as a policy analyst in well-known Washington, DC think tanks, as the leader of integrated teams developing, designing, and financing major infrastructure projects, and as a legislative aide managing major policy areas for a U.S. Senator.
