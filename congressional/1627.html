Background

Professional Background

I am on the Indivisible Arapahoe Steering Committee and have helped organize public events, postcard to voter campaigns. I have lobbied (as a private citizen) at the Colorado Capital for NARAL (now Cobalt), in favor of Paid Family Leave and on behalf of numerous legislative efforts that would impact our Black and People of Color communities.

Past Political Activity

I am a member of Indivisible Arapahoe, Women's Lobby of Colorado, Colorado Black Women for Political Action. I have donated to the following campaigns: Phil Weiser, Jason Crow, Jena Griswald, Amy Padden, Diane Mitsch Bush, Stephanie Rose Spaulding, Naquetta Ricks, Jaime Harrison, Ilana Spiegel, Jon Ossof, Jeff Bridges, Chris Kolker, Tom Sullivan, Omar Montgomery.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Women's Lobby of Colorado, Colorado Black Women for Political Action, Indivisible Arapahoe, Women's Spiritual Leadership Alliance, Englewood Chamber of Commerce.

Statement

I believe in one person/one vote regardless of a person's political affiliation. I am interested in helping to make sure that our government is truly representative of *all* of its citizens and that they each have a voice through their vote.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a small business owner for the last 24 years, I have been responsible for managing my staff as well as the expectations and needs of our customers. In order to be successful, you must have a clear communicating style and the ability to listen and to compromise. Although I am registered as a Democrat, I was an independent for most of my voting life. I registered Democrat in order to be able to vote in the primaries (although Colorado has changed that requirement in the last few years). I grew up in a Republican household and am familiar in a positive way with the values that 'Republican' is supposed to represent. My interest is in creating an environment that contributes to the greater good for everyone.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Again, as a successful business owner of a retail business, everyday I must analyze what stock I need to order, how long it will take to arrive, anticipate how much I need, etc. How to juggle time, needs of my staff, and how best to anticipate what my customers will be interested in next. I think that experience would translate to the redistricting process - what are the needs, where are the resources and how best to fairly allocate them.
