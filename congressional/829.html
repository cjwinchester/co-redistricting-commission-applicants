Statement

I worked for the Census Bureau in Washington, D. C. for 10 years. I understand the development and appropriate use of statistics for non-partisan purposes. I saw with dismay how the last congressional redistricting went in Colorado and am relieved it is to be non-partisan this time. I understand the issue of Census undercount and how that might affect the Census numbers available to the Commission. I am a Colorado native and have lived here all my life except for grad school and my stint in Washington, D. C. I love this state and all it represents.

Statement on Working with Consensus

First and foremost, I believe in listening to “the other side,” even persons with whom I may not agree. I am a firm believer and practitioner that more voices improve the final product. Two experiences come to mind. I recently joined the non-profit “Braver Angels,” which seeks to unite “red” and “blue” in building a better tomorrow. I've taken seminars from them that are guides in listening respectfully. In addition I have practiced the scaled version of degrees of consensus many times: 5 = “I agree and will advocate for this;” 4 = “I agree with this;” 3 = “I am neither for nor against this;” 2 = “I am opposed to this;” 1 = “I am strongly opposed to this; it’s a show stopper.” Using this format allows for discussion with those who are not for an idea. When every decision-maker reaches at least a 3, the resulting measure is adopted by consensus. It works.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have degrees in mathematics and statistics. I spent 30 years as a professional statistician before beginning a second career as a secondary math teacher. I have much experience applying data to the real world. I worked on the Demographic side of the Census Bureau for 2 years, on the Economic side for 2 years and in the Research Division for 6 years. I approach a problem by looking for the relevant facts, understanding the facts, looking at the broader context, forming an assumption, and then checking that assumption with reality. I am able to distill complex analytical ideas into easy-to-understand lay terms. I love the field of statistics; as I told my Metro State classes--statistics is telling a story with data. It's up to the user to be informed to tell that story truthfully, and not bend the statistics to a point of view.
