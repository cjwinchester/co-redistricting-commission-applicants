Statement

I believe a non-partisan effort to redistrict is the only fair method of truly apportioning districts. The changes every 10 years based upon which party is in the majority is basically unfair to all voters. Non-partisan, professional methods are, my belief, recommended.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe in hearing every viewpoint and focusing first on those areas of agreement to promote a sense of achievement and cohesiveness. Then try to work on those areas of disagreement incrementally so we can meet consensus. I have worked in the past in both my professional life as an educational facilities planning consultant and now as a community association leader to forge consensus on oftentimes, contentious issues. Impartiality is key to obtaining results. Also, I choose to be an unaffiliated voter so as to be able to hear both sides of an issue or office and make a selection that is best for my family and community.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I've founded an educational facilities consultant firm that worked with hundreds of schools, teachers, administrators and citizens to improve the facilities of school districts. These skills are especially important when trying to realign school district boundaries to accommodate school enrollment shifts using cohort survival analysis. Oftentimes these efforts involved closing schools or finding land to build new schools in congested urban areas that required numerous community forums and meetings to reach consensus. Analyzing emotions and the rationale behind people's opinions also helps in trying to develop solutions.
