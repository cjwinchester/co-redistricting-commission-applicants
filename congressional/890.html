Professional Background

Chair, Colorado Association of Commerce & Industry Air Quality Committee (1993-2014); Chair, American Lung Association of Colorado (1998-2002); Member, Denver Regional Air Quality Council appointed by Governor Bill Owens (2001-2008); Member, Colorado Climate Change Advisory Committee appointed by Governor Bill Ritter, Jr. (2009-2012); Chair, Rocky Mountain Oil & Gas Association Air Quality Committee (1992-1999); Member, Colorado Petroleum Association Environmental Committee (1999-2014); Member Colorado Air Toxics Task Force appointed by Governor Roy Romer (1995-1996); Member, Grand Canyon Visibility Commission's Market Based Forum appointed by Governor Roy Romer (1994-1996); Member Nebraska State Board of Education appointed by Governor Bob Kerrey (1986-1988). All of these committees held public meetings and achieved consensus decisions on policy issues. I firmly believe in achieving consensus and working on issues in a bi-partisan, collaborative fashion.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Senior Policy Advisor, Center for New Energy Economy at Colorado State University; Chair, Bridge House Board of Directors; Member, Board of Directors, Spirae, LLC.

Statement

I oppose gerrymandering Congressional Districts and fully support an independent redistricting commission fairly drawing districts to ensure the legal principle "one person, one vote."

Statement on Working with Consensus

As the chair of several diverse industry groups (CACI and RMOGA) I focused the members and, sometimes after many meetings, formed consensus around Colorado air quality policy for three decades--air quality continuously improved at minimal economic cost. As chair of a regional homeless services provider (Bridge House) I focused diverse members to improve services for the homeless in both Boulder and Aurora. As chair of American Lung Association of Colorado we were able to achieve consensus on educating and improving public awareness of smoking, air quality impacts and lung cancer in the State of Colorado. I have been appointed by both Republican and Democratic Governors to policy positions based on my reputation as a credible, fair, and impartial advocate. I believe my experiences and skills can help create agreement among 12 diverse commissioners and result in a fair redistricting plan.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Although I am not an expert in the Voting Rights Act, I have experience reading legislation, researching policies/technologies and applying basic equitable principles to solve thorny environmental problems. Since retiring I've assisted former Governor Bill Ritter (pro bono) in convenings with western utilities and western states and other stakeholders to improve air quality, reduce greenhouse gas emissions and help just transitions in impacted communities. One example--in 2019 former Governor Ritter's Center for New Energy Economy convened stakeholders with TriState Generation & Transmission Cooperative to assist in meeting Colorado and New Mexico's climate goals. The resulting consensus agreement led Tri-State to announce its Responsible Energy Plan, with planned closure of the Craig coal units and coal mine before 2030.
