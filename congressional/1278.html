Professional Background

About 2 years ago, the local library asked me to apply to be on the Board of the Library. I applied and the Mayor of Littleton called me and asked me if I would consider moving my application to the Licensing Commission. The Licensing Commission issues new licenses in the City of Littleton for any on site consumption of alcohol and reviews current licensure. In addition, the Licensing Commission issues new and renewal licenses for medical marijuana dispensaries. We also are the administrative body that reviews any sort of licensure issues, including failure to comply with compliance checks, etc.

Statement

I had never served on a public board and I wanted to learn about how citizens can interface in the governmental process. It has truly been one of the most rewarding experiences. For me, I have been able to witness Robert's Rules of Order in operation. I have been able to benefit from a great Chair who has been able to engage all of us in respecting true "meeting" procedures.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I am a health care attorney. As a health care attorney we are often thrust into situations where people are at their most vulnerable. No one wants to be at the hospital. Patients and their families are often scared, nervous, angry and often grateful...all at the same time. People in their most emotional of times often struggle with decision making. As an attorney, I'm often called on to be an intermediary between health care providers and patients/families. Now with COVID restrictions, we are even more sensitive to the challenges of having a health care event. Every day I have to navigate differing opinions, differing "rights", considering nuance of privacy law, and protecting the institution. Each day ultimately, my job is to insure that patients and families are able to continue their journey with as much dignity as possible.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I am an attorney and have been for over 20 years. As an attorney, my instinct is analysis. Every problem is, in my opinion, 3-dimensional. We have to consider it from every angle. My philosophy is to look for the "gaps". What information are we missing? and Why? I find that the search for "negative" space really changes the perspective on any problem.
