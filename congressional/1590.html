Also, we need to carefully review many of the other rural demographic diverse region around the state to ensure every group of Americans, including Latinx and low income groups, throughout the state are at the table of decision-makers. Statement on Working with Consensus

As a minority, who has worked in many administrative positions in government, private industry and universities/colleges, I have experienced first-hand the abuses of government (often unintended, but the damage is NO LESS). We need to get the right people around the table, that has simply not been the case. One can only built consensus when social justice prevails and all groups believe they are fairly represented; in essence, I have been lucky/or unlucky to serve and witness first-hand to see "how consensus" was not done, and it was a learning experience... We need to build from where we are, and work toward "consensus building." It will not be easy, but nothing I have ever achieved was easy. ... JUST NECESSARY. Change takes time, but on this issue of redistricting, it is way past time for change and do it right. We need to ensure "the voices" of different constituencies--rural, Latinx, and non-traditional groups ARE VALUED. They represent the best of " all our tomorrows and all those tomorrows will make the state and country a better place from which to LEAD."
