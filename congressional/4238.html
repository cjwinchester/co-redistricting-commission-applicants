Statement

I believe this sort of community service would be interesting and very satisfying.

Statement on Working with Consensus

While it may initially seem counterintuitive, my years of law practice counseling with clients’ decision making and dealing with my clients and opposing counsel in employment litigation have regularly involved developing consensus, often with creative solutions. Most cases settle, but whether in settlement or going to trial I have consistently worked at and achieved as much consensus/agreement as could be achieved. A key part of this is reviewing the situation and providing independent, reasoned judgment in addressing a matter. Critical to this has been tempering advocacy with analysis of the facts. That necessitates a sense of fairness in reaching agreement. It has also required me to “step back” and look at a situation impartially as a court might do in addressing client and opposing party concerns.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have helped clients address and solve technical and practical operational and interpersonal problems. This has required careful fact gathering, fact analysis and creativity to consider novel solutions. Those skills would be of service with this task.
