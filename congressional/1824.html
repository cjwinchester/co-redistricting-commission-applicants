Statement

I believe that there is work to be done to serve our families and our communities. I believe that the success of our democracy depends on our ability as citizens to continue to build a society in which the rights many of us take for granted are secured for all of our people, and all of our children - regardless of race, ability, gender/sexual orientation, economic background, education, or cultural and linguistic background. I also believe that it is essential for us to find common ground amidst the current divisive political climate, if we are to serve the future of our democracy.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I believe that it is essential to begin our work together from a position of shared humanity. There are similarities we all share - families, life cycles, neighborhoods, emotions like hope and fear, the communities we've built, the lands we roam. My husband and my best friend do not share my political affiliation, and yet I know we share values. I work hard to understand their positions, to find common ground, and to accept our different view points with grace, compassion, and respect - and I politely insist that they treat me with the same consideration.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a classroom teacher, I relied on data subsets to determine growth goals, plan instruction, maintain accountability, and analyze program efficacy. I rely on guidance from experts to ensure that I accurately interpret data with which I am unfamiliar.
