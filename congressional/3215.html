Background

Past Political Activity

I am "political" in the sense that I believe all issues are inherently relevant politically (i.e., "the personal is political") and I vote regularly. However, I have not been involved in direct campaigning and I do not believe I have donated any money to campaigns or political organizations since 2015. My charitable donations tend to revolve around information communication (e.g., Wikipedia, NPR/CPR), non-profit women's shelters and services, and those feeding the hungry (e.g., Rocky Mountain Food Bank).

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

Professionally, I am a member of the American Psychological Association and regularly summit documents, etc. to the State Licensing Board for Psychologists. I am also a volunteer in Spark the Change Colorado's mental health arm which provides pro-bono mental health services to those in need (and pro-bono supervision to volunteer therapists who are unlicensed). Most recently, I provided volunteer case management to women experiencing homelessness at the makeshift COVID-19 shelter at the Denver Coliseum. Other organizations that I have loose associations with or volunteer experience include The Gathering Place; Envision: You; Rocky Mountain Association of Diversity Officers in Higher Education; Southwest Institute for Research on Women.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

Most of my organizing and advocacy work has been on college campuses, including Regis University, where I have focused on mental health access and stigma, interpersonal violence and rape prevention, and queer/LGBTQIA+ inclusivity. This includes bringing speakers to campus, conducting DEI trainings, mentoring faculty and students, and creating/supporting cross-campus coalitions to address these issues.

Statement

I was so proud of Colorado for passing Amendments Y & Z in 2018 and I believe we have an opportunity as a state to be a model to other states for how this process can be effective and non-partisan. However, this only works if people apply and volunteer to serve on the commissions. So, in that sense, I believed it my civic duty to apply (similar to my civic duty to vote).

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a full-time professor and licensed therapist, I have professional mediation and facilitation skills that will be helpful as the commission works to create agreement. I regularly and successfully lead conversations in the classroom and in my personal life that involve differences of opinions, pre-existing knowledge, and goals. I believe fully that redistricting should be a representative and non-partisan process. It would be my stated explicit goal to find a solution that is fair and just for all. This goal necessarily requires consensus.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have a Ph.D. in psychology which requires advanced statistical coursework and application. I also currently teach undergraduate statistics which is based on logic and reason. I am the author or co-author of 17 peer-reviewed articles, book chapters, and encyclopedia entries in scientific outlets which is evidence of both my ability to use and apply logic and reason but also my ability to translate deductive conclusions to everyday language and people.
