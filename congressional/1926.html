Statement

At my core I love fairness. Gerrymandering goes against my sense of fairness and I hate the thought that redistricting is done with a bias to help ensure a party wins.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I manage 25 people at my workplace and do it from a sense of fairness and respect and try to get buy-in for what needs to be done and by whom, rather than just to tell people what to do with no context. Not everyone agrees on things in this polarized world, but it's still important to find out where you have consensus on things and where some is still needed and then strive to get there. I don't always get my way, even as a superior to others in an organizational chart, because sometimes they have to try things I don't think will work in order for them to buy in to what I believe. I think this same willingness to compromise will serve me well when working on this commission.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Besides the fact that I actually took a class in logic in college, I'm an engineer and a man and to some, I'm overly logical at times (just ask my wife). I feel redistricting can be done from a starting point of dividing up the state in areas containing the same people where those areas are all rectangular and determined by a straightforward algorithm. Then the only thing left for the commission to do is to tweak boundaries so that they make sense from a perspective of geography and governance (it may not make sense to cut a little town in half, for example). I think if districting is done from a set of rules instead of from opinionated political puppetry, persons can be assured that it was done with fairness in mind.
