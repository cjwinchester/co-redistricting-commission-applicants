Statement

i have been active in redistricting reform issues since i authored congressional legislation in the mid-1980s to create national standards when i was the director of the house wednesday group. as a political scientist by training, i have followed this issue closely at the state level for almost 40 years.

Statement on Working with Consensus

i start with the idea that partisan redistricting is not in the interest of fair and effective governance. districts should be drawn around notions that do not guarantee a political outcome, but follow standards such as geographic compactness and contiguous districts, among others. Throughout my career, first in government, and then in the private sector, i have worked to build consensus decisions and policies because i believe they are usually the most equitable but they are also the most sustainable. I have followed the belief found in the words of senator henry clay, who reminds us, "do not despise compromise, it is the cement that holds the union together." although spoken about 180 years in the past, they remain worthy guideposts today in our nation of competing interests. My professional and personal long track record of working with people of many political and philosophical viewpoints speaks directly about my ability to be fair and impartial.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have over a 40 career in public policy, and business leadership. i have published scholarly papers, written in virtually every major national publication, advised personally the ceo's in major companies, including the washington post, priceline, barclays global investors, texas instruments, deloitte, mitsubishi motors, among others. As a graduate student at the university of chicago, i did several quantitative projects in conjunction with the national opinion research center. i am also a former guest scholar at the brookings institution, a member of the council on foreign relations, and an advisor to the president of committee for economic development (the think tank for fortune 500 ceos and university presidents).
