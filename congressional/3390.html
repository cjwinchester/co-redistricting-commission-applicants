Statement

I want to help ensure America's most sacred right is protected and equitably administered. The right to vote is critical to our democratic republic and we need to ensure that electoral districts are delineated in a way that allows Colorado's collective voice to be heard and accurately represented.

Statement on Working with Consensus

One of the reasons I moved to Colorado in 2010 was because of its pragmatic approach to governance. For Colorado, it is not about blue or red, it is about finding workable solutions to political issues. I think I can contribute to the commission by applying that same approach and working with all the commissioners to develop a districting system that simply works and represents its citizens in a fair and balanced way.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My studies focused specifically on applying logic and using quantitative data to solve qualitative problems. Since then, I have applied my ability to objectively review information and determine the most logical course of action in all of my positions in the U.S. government. Whether it was working directly with Afghan leaders in Jalalabad, developing a whole-of-government approach and system of metrics to U.S. foreign policy in war-torn and destabilized countries, or developing an interorganizational plan (from scratch) for U.S. Northern Command to execute in order to better coordinate defense activities with non-profit organizations (e.g. American Red Cross) and businesses (Fortune 500 companies, drug stores, big box stores, etc.) during national disasters, I have used analytical rigor and objective analysis. And today, I work with communities across CO and UT to apply for federal grants that will result in high skilled, high paid job creation. As a result, I have not only built a strong trust with those communities, I have also fine tuned my ability to work across political spectrums and interests.
