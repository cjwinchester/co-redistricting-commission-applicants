Statement

I am passionately committed to preserving the integrity of democracy and ensuring all Coloradan voices are heard. I am committed to equity and inclusivity, and hope to ensure Colorado continues to be a place where voters decide our future and elect our leaders and are fairly represented.

Statement on Working with Consensus

With my volunteer work, I facilitate difficult discussions to deepen people's abilities to resolve conflicts and find consensus. Through my volunteer and professional work, I regularly practice and teach active listening, the process of consensus, and effective communication. I will apply these skills in the commission and work to understand what my peers are saying and where they are coming from, and clearly state my views, and work to identify the compromises and agreements between them. I practice fairness and impartiality in my life regularly, including taking steps to fully understand others' points of view. For instance, when arguing with friends or partners, I often do a mental exercise with a third party where I take the argument of my "opponent" and let the third party argue my side. This allows me to more completely understand their perspective and adjust my POV or argument to better resolve the conflict. This, along with active listening and a deep care for others and all humanity, has provided me with the skills to manage and develop all sorts of relationships. Although, I am a registered democrat, both of my parents and much of my family are ardent republicans and we regularly have civilized discussions and come to agreements on topics that we initially disagreed upon.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Whenever approaching a problem, I consult existing reputable and peer-reviewed research and knowledge on the topic, talk to peers, and reflect on my own experiences (and how that might color my perspective). Considering all of these pieces, I often also conduct my own research, through surveys, comparisons, or other methodology, to ensure I am on the right track. I firmly believe in a data-driven approach to applying best practices. That also means that if the data shows something to be ineffective, then I make adjustments, no matter how committed I was to the process originally. Additionally, I always try to look at the complete picture before forming decisions, not jumping to conclusions or making assumptions.
