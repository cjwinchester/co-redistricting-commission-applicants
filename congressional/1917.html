Past Political Activity

As a child, my parents were active in the community and local government. They taught me and my siblings the importance of our vote. and of doing our parts to be good citizens. At 16, I was a volunteer at the Cincinnati HQ for the Clinton campaign in 1992. Not even old enough to vote yet, I still felt like my canvassing and phone bank calls to voters made a difference. I have been active with my local party for every election since. Be it Registering people to vote, to giving classes on local measures, I believe that it is my civic duty to inform. I have remained neutral and haven't giving to any campaign or organization.

Statement

I believe that I will be a great asset and addition to the Redistricting Commission, because I have a love for fairness and equality. My focuses in university allowed me to be diverse in my approach to serving the public, while understanding the role of Government, and how to make it all work together. My experience in management has allowed me to experience working under pressure and meeting deadlines. And having being an Educator I understand that everything we will do will be to the benefit of the future, and the legacy passed on to generations down the road. Being part of this Commission, I will bear all of this in mind.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Whether it is stated, or not, we as Commissioners will be delegates, bringing with us our experiences, and reasoning based on our make up, and our environment. This is a good thing. We are all human beings, and citizens of Colorado at the end of the day. And while we may disagree on some matters, the objective and aim will be the same. And we will always find common solutions. I will listen to all sides of a matter, and be fair and seek council, even from those with an opposing view, to come to a clear solution.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I base much of my reasoning on a legal term called RPP, or Reasonable Prudent Person. In many situations, while I may tend to overthink, I will ask myself, "what would a reasonably prudent person do in this situation?" and If I am armed with all of the facts I usually come to a favorable conclusion, for the prudent. It is a simple approach, but it works more than it doesn't.
