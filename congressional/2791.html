Statement

I believe Colorado's third district would benefit from a careful, community driven look at redistricting.

Statement on Working with Consensus

After living for 18 years in Steamboat Springs and now sinking roots in Grand Junction I believe I can be an asset toward consensus building. I've worked for years developing relationships in communities that often don't embrace similar political ideologies or economic backbones. I've seen some communities thrive while others not 30 minutes away struggle. This had led me to a unique understanding of our social fabric that often can slip through the cracks. Every voice matters, I can help with that.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

One way to illuminate my process would be an analogy of skiing in the backcountry. This may conjure images of powder and fun sliding down a slope but it takes an entire process to get there. You start by looking at relevant information (avalanche report and weather), then checking how that squares with the reality in my location. We're all driven by desires and expectations but that can get a skier into big trouble and the same applies here. My experience in the public process of federal resource management builds experience in reason, understanding a stakeholders desires and how they square with the overarching goal.
