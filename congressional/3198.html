Organization and Advocacy Experience

During college I interned witht hen Senator Wayne Allard, and was active in both his and State GOP GOTV efforts. I have worked as a volunteer coordinator in Washington DC and Denver/Boulder, advocating for resources and tools for underrepresented BIPOC populations. I have worked to expand access for women to health care, especially those from low-income households (Rocky Mountain Planned Parenthood) and in my current position support both direct and bridge services to youth and families in Northeast Denver.

Statement

I understand that this commission is lacking in diversity -an ongoing challenge for our community. I am a Black woman, and a third-generation native of Denver. I believe that my education, experience, and dedication to representation at all levels of government will provide more equity to those who have been traditionally disenfranchised by the current districting practices. I think I can be a voice to improve how we are represented.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I would like to hear the perspective of all commissioners, and that while I hope for a diversity of thought will always focus communication on solution based actions that result in next-steps that are beneficial to all. I truly believe that as Coloradans, there is more that we have in common than that which divides us, and that with intentional conversation we can achieve a positive outcome.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As a fundraising professional, I often use complex sets of data to drive our giving campaigns to reach a targeted group for maximum impact. Moving numbers into narrative is one of my skills, and I hope to employ it here. Data, however, only tells half of the story, and I use inference and additional research to inform my thinking for an impactful course of action.
