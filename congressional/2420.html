Statement

Even though I'm an 'older white male,' I am also an out gay man so I have a little experience being a minority and being discriminated against. But, more importantly, I understand broadly the importance of equal representation and fair treatment of all people, regardless of background. I truly believe in the promise that America (and the states individually) of equality and equity and I know that that starts with ensuring that each portion of each state is fairly represented. Drawing fair and equitable district maps is the first step in ensuring this and I would be excited and honored to be a part of it.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a physician, I have to be data and fact driven. I cannot 'believe' something may work for my patients, I have to know that it will based on unbiased research, facts and verifiable information. If chosen to be a commissioner, I would do my best to reach the goal of fair and equitable representation of the congressional districts based on a fair interpretation of the facts. I am also open to new information which may challenge my initial beliefs and am open to hearing other sides to the story.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I have to apply logic, reason, knowledge and my own personal experiences every day when I approach a patient who may have breast cancer. I have to analyze a mammogram and apply logic and reasoning to the finding. I then have to make a decision about the test which may alter the trajectory of the patient's life. Then, if a diagnosis is made, I have to use all of these skills in conveying not only the information to the patient, but also the implications of that information. Only in this way can the patient be armed with the correct information in order to make the best choices for their treatment.
