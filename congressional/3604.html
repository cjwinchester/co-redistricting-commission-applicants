Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Framing the issues is the first step to effectively address complex social problems. Peter Drucker identifies the key criteria to efffectively solve problems as the What, the Why, and the How. Sounds simple. Not so. He would tell us that you cannot come up with a means to fix a problem (a How to), until you know for certain Why something happened. Then, Peter asked us how can you determine Why" "it" happened if you do not have a clear idea What happened. It was Peter's way of teaching us to not get ahead of yourself in the problem resolution process. Without clarity on What happend and Why, you will never resolve the problem; or your solution will not work. I am using that simple assessment tool as an example of applying logic and reason to frame problem resolution. In public policy analysis per se they use another framework, referred to as the social architecture model which frames problems around the following: public goods, externalities, natural monopolies, social insurance, and social equity; PENIE. In the for profit sector or NGO, they use frameworks like SWOT: strengths, weaknesses, opportunites, and threats. Each of these are common models for problem analysis and resolution, that apply logic and reason to a given situation. When a well focused group works together to accomplish a clearly defined set of objectives, they must agree on frameworks like the above, to engage in the process of applying logic and reason to a particular problem. I would argue that it starts with the mission and focus of the group, in this case the goal of electoral redistricting. This criteria, your mission/objectives/goals, determines outcomes. I am sure that you are aware that colleges and universities are similarly engaged in long-term problem resolution, like student retention, one of the most common objectives of academia. I have been part of nine-year longitudinal studies on problems like student retention as well as several other common focus groups, like: becoming a Hispanic Serving University (took us 15 years); improving first year student retention; improving racial equity in faculty recruitment and retention; this list goes on ad-inifinitum. What it suggests, is that we were successful when we worked together with clearly defined missions, goals, objectives, and desired outcomes. Transferrable analytical skills and abilities were employed using logic and reason to resolve clearly defined problems.
