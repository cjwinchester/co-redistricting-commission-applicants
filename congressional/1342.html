Organization and Advocacy Experience

As a long time public educator, I have worked on many committees, held many offices and constantly pursued excellence as an educator and the many professional organizations. During my full-time professional years, I planned and coordinated trainings and conferences for teachers across the state. I also worked on district and state committees to update curriculums. I worked with the Colorado Community College System, other states and private foundations to facilitate funding and activities for educational workshops. In addition, I worked as a team teacher both at the high school and college level. Team teaching takes a great deal of communication and time in order to produce a quality educational experience for students as well as a positive collegial working relationship. In 2015 my husband stepped up to run for the Board of Education in Jefferson County. I worked actively with his campaign, as well as the campaigns of the other BOE candidates. I listened to constituents, organized outreach efforts, helped track campaign donations and a variety of other tasks as needed. He served as board president and I have supported, advised and attended many school districts functions and activities. In 2017 I joined the work of the Community Grief Center of Greeley to help the Family Trek Program. As a facilitator I was trained to conduct eight sessions with different age groups to assist families with the loss of a loved one. The services were free to families and I volunteered my time to train and work with the teen groups. In recent years I have volunteered through our Homeowners Association to welcome new neighbors and assist in planning and conducting social/get-acquainted activities.

Statement

I am a Colorado native and very proud of our beautiful state. As a longtime unaffiliated voter in a state with a growing number of independent voters I am very interested in seeing equity in representation of Coloradoans across the state. I recognize that balancing numbers and equity issues is important in electing public officials that will represent their constituents not just a political ideology. I am interested in this committee and at a time in my life where I can devote the time and energy to the process which hopefully results in fair and balanced districts across the state.

Statement on Working with Consensus

1) It is important to do your homework, read and study the data provided, consider the various geographic areas and needs across the state. 2) Listening is a key skill to working effectively within a committee. Ask questions, listen and respect opinions. 3) Points or arguments should be made based on facts and legitimate needs not a political platform. 4) In the last four years, I have leaned more to one party than the other. However, that has not been true throughout my life. I have supported candidates from both parties and causes that lean both conservative and liberal. I do not have a need to gerrymander a district to represent a particular party, but would rather see a balance as much as distance and populations of the area will allow.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Over my career and life experience I have served on many problem solving and decision-making committees. The ultimate goal has always been to arrive at an outcome based on consensus that is acceptable to all members. I understand and value data and statistics. I welcome discussion and reasoning to arrive at decisions, and have excellent listening skills.
