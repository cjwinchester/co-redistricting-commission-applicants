Organization and Advocacy Experience

I've held leadership positions in the private sector, with Veterans groups, and in community. As a certified Six Sigma Black Belt, my facilitation efforts are undergirded by working to set the vision, garner the required stakeholder commitment (to include sponsorship), set the necessary priorities, identify our gaps in achieving those priorities, then doing the work. I've done this time and again. Most recently, I consulted to Second Chance Center as Project Manager for a 50-unit Permanent Supportive Housing Development, in the heart of Aurora. This year, during the time of COVID, I consulted to Colorado Criminal Justice Reform Coalition, to establish a c4 arm, then engage in District Attorney races in the 2020 election cycle.

Statement

As a military Veteran, I believe in service to country, service to community, and service to family. This service is the least we can do as a resident of the nation. This commission is necessary work, and I'd like to bring to bear my education and experience to inform the effort.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I'm prepared to offer objective facilitation tools into our process, should we get stuck. It is sometimes necessary to caucus offline, identify the WIIFM (What's In It For Me?) for everyone in the room, then start there to garner the consensus necessary to move forward, no matter where we may be stuck.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

I endeavor to maintain a healthy pragmatic and objective point of view. It is possible to facilitate through a process using facilitation tools, and take some of the ego and personality out of a decision making process. I'm inclined to lean in on those tools, and less likely to engage in personality driven dialogue, which is rarely helpful.
