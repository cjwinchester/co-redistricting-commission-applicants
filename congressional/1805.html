Statement

Access to fair and impartial voting is a fundamental part of any democracy. One of the toughest parts of voting is the feeling among many of my peers that voting doesn’t matter, and that their vote won’t count. Helping ensure that the districts accurately represent a population removes a barrier to that mentality, and increases the likelihood of residents participating in their local elections. In addition, younger voices are often underrepresented in the decision making process. As a person in my 30s, I can help bridge that gap.

Statement on Working with Consensus

Over the course of my life, I have lived in rural and urban areas, and in conservative and progressive. My experiences have taught me to listen to what someone is saying before making a judgement, and to look for common ground even when it’s hard to find. In the case of this commission, I’m starting with the base assumption that we’re all looking for fairness. If we’re all working towards the same goal, it becomes much easier to talk about the pros and cons of a specific path to get there.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Much of my professional experience has required me to achieve goals while working with disparate populations. Working in education, and now as a small business owner, logic and reason have been invaluable tools to determine where to best leverage my resources. I have had to make hard financial decisions as a business owner, particularly this year during the covid pandemic. Creating a clear plan based on facts allowed me to weather the COVID restrictions this season without laying off employees or cutting salaries.
