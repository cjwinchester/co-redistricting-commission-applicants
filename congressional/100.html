Statement

As a long-time data professional, I believe that I have valuable and unique analytical skills that will be useful on this commission. A fair and robust democratic process is vital to our continued success as a state and as a nation. I have lived in Colorado for ~40 years, both in rural areas and in Denver. Colorado is my home and this is a unique opportunity to give back to the state I love.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have a fairly unique ability to find, understand, and disseminate data relevant to a situation. I am able to see natural trends in data and build visualizations to help others see and understand those trends. I am a curious puzzle-solver by nature, someone who takes far more pleasure in learning than I do by thinking I know. I enjoy discussion, good-natured debate of relevant issues, and giving people the benefit of the doubt. Several years ago, I served as the foreperson on a jury in Colorado for a felony case - we ultimately came to unanimous verdicts on all charges (some innocent, some guilty). During that process, I changed my mind about one of the charges because of well-reasoned arguments by my fellow jurors.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

When I first joined Microsoft, I sold intellectual property to the company related to analytics and data warehousing. To this day, I have an opportunity to work on hands-on analytics projects on a regular basis. In the past year alone, I have three significant data and analytics projects on record. I built a COVID-19 dashboard that tracked a variety of metrics through 22 July 2020 at http://aka.ms/us-covid19-megamap. From this, I wrote a detailed statistical analysis of COVID-19 in late April with predictions that have (unfortunately) aged remarkably well at https://medium.com/@michael.v.acosta/give-me-liberty-and-give-me-death-a-science-experiment-in-six-days-67758059e5af I created one of the most comprehensive safe voting location databases for the 2020 election at http://wevotesafely.org. The story behind the building of this site (a personal statement, as it were) can be found at https://medium.com/@michael.v.acosta/the-story-behind-wevotesafely-org-a533e4c2083d I have built a detailed geospatial technical walkthrough for a site selection scenario at http://aka.ms/APGS. I built this simply because I love maps/geography and wanted to know more about how modern geospatial data systems work. My success with data over the years comes from not only understanding it, but helping others to understand it as well. My full professional history is available at https://www.linkedin.com/in/michaelvacosta/
