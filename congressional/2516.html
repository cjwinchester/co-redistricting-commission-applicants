Professional Background

Beginning in 2009 I chaired the South Metro Denver Health Care Policy Task force. Our task force wrote a proposal for consideration by Colorado's 208 commission on healthcare reform. This proposal was later written as legislation that was presented in the Colorado legislator in 2009 and 2010. We worked with business and community organizations and health care trade associations to articulate the significant issues in our healthcare system. This advocacy for rational reform in healthcare, which would actually reduce the cost of the system, and the cost in the system to governments, business and individuals, has been revived this year. In 2010 I cofounded and chaired the Coalition for Effective Healthcare a 501C4. In 2012, at the request of the South Metro Chamber Board of Directors I wrote a white paper on the significant challenges for business of Colorado's very complex sales tax system. That white paper was presented to the then Colorado State Senate President. That led to my personal advocacy with mayors, DOR and some legislators. Ultimately, after soliciting the involvement of American Furniture Warehouse and Colorado Automobile Dealers Association, we interested businesses and trade organizations in joining the effort to fix Colorado's Sales Tax System. We formed the Coalition to Simplify Colorado Sales Tax. The Coalition, working with legislators, has participated in the writing of and advocacy of 5 bills that have all passed the legislature and are step by step moving Colorado toward a far more user friendly sales tax system, that is revenue neutral, at worst, and very well may generate more sales tax revenue for the state and home rule cities.

Statement on Working with Consensus

In my sales tax simplifications advocacy I have met with and been in meetings with all stakeholders in the sales tax system - the Coloroado Department of Revenue, Colorado Municipal League, individual Home Rule cities, counties, Colorado legislature, business trade organizations and individual businesses. We were able to listen, respond to disparate interests, find common ground and common objectives and move the ball progressively toward a much better sales tax system for the state of Colorado. With my healthcare reform advocacy, we engaged political leaders, business organizations, and even healthcare trade associations. This is a really difficult issue to rally interested parties toward a common solution. The actual fix to the healthcare system problems, is not that challenging. Aligning the various players is very challenging. The businesses of healthcare themselves are the most difficult to move as the American Healthcare industry is the largest industry on the planet with significant players who have a major financial stake in the structure of our system. We were able to achieve alignment with many at the table including advocates for a single payer healthcare system, with whom we found significant common ground. As part of my work in this area, I was a significant contributor to the legislation that enabled the Colorado healthcare insurance exchange.

Our outlook for the future is strong as we have made and implement many decisions that have preserved our company and positioned us for future prosperity.
