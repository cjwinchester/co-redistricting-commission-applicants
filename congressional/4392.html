Organization and Advocacy Experience

As a Public Defender, a central part of my work, is advocating for the poor and voiceless members of our communities. My clients are often victims of neglect, abuse, trauma, and mental illness. My job as their advocate is to listen, unearth mitigation and present their circumstances to prosecutors and judges persuasively. Also, as a board member for Full Circle Restorative Justice, I advocate to broaden restorative versus purely punitive practices in our schools, courtrooms, and communities.

Statement

I would like to serve because it is a unique opportunity to be engaged in a democratic process that ensures that diverse communities have equal access to political representation.

Statement on Working with Consensus

As a an advocate, I learned the art of listening. I strive to understand and resist the urge to judge. In my work, I have learned to discipline myself to engage opposing parties, including prosecutors, victims, law enforcement, and witnesses. To understand the often nuanced dynamics of a case it is necessary to resist the urge to jump to condemnation of any one of the courtroom participants. Likewise, a commissioner must be open-minded and prepared to listen to the viewpoints, concerns, and positions of their colleagues. Committee members are serving to make sure all citizens have equal access to political representation- that goal is only achieved via civil discord.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My profession demands that I apply apply case law and statutory law to a particular set of facts in order to determine what the likely outcome will be. The ability to analyze a case demands that logically work through all the potential scenarios and advice my client accordingly. Analysis and reasoning are skills that I have been honing on a daily basis for the last 20 years of my career.
