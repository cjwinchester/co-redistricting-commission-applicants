Past Political Activity

During the 2020 campaign season, I donated to the campaigns for Amy McGrath (KY senate) and Bernie Sanders.

List of Political and Civic Organizations belonged to

In 2017, I served on the Boulder city Community Culture and Safety committee, which analyzed and recommended applications for funding from a sales tax renewal.

Organization and Advocacy Experience

On the Community, Culture, and Safety committee, I reviewed applications for capital expansion funding, from within the city departments and non-profit organizations. This process required considering the needs and interests of the individual applicant groups, their constituents (e.g. non-profit members, city staff), and the taxpayers of the city in general. I had to advocate for what I believed was in the best interest of all of these groups in order to help our committee agree on a final stack ranking of projects to fund.

Statement

I am excited to serve because it is a focused effort to help make a fair and equitable decision on a critically important civic matter. Historically, I have desired greater civic engagement, but cannot make the long-term commitments required of positions within the city/county government (e.g. boards and commissions here are ~5 year terms) because of family obligations and work travel. Like the Community Culture and Safety committee, this is a short-term and focused effort which I can prioritize and concentrate on. I am excited to participate in a process that comes only once per decade and can be so impactful to how Colorado elects its representatives for 10 years.

Statement on Working with Consensus

My professional life is all about finding consensus among diverse groups with divergent interests. No one reports to me, yet I have to convince an entire organization of 1000s to direct resources towards some projects over others. Thus, I have to understand the needs and interests not just of my customers, but of every team and individual whom I partner with to deliver. This is constant negotiation based in empathy, mutual understanding, and problem solving. While I have my own opinions, we cannot make decisions together without each individual being willing to objectively assess their own position, consider the arguments from other team members, and be willing to find a middle ground. I would bring this experience and attitude to the committee. Ultimately, the redistricting process is intended to maximally align the congressional seats with the population so that we have Congressional representation proportional to our interests. I believe this goal is independent of how any one person feels about those interests, and so I would work with the committee to align on goals that are non-partisan to frame our work.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

My professional background is one of problem solving, analytics, and logical reasoning. Every day I am seeking answers to questions like "how can I help bring a group to consensus", or "how should we rank and prioritize projects", or "can I design metrics and frameworks to help make decisions". These questions lead us to multi-quarter/year project plans that span numerous organizations. Optimizing across these teams and over time requires deep logical reasoning and analysis. I have strong analysis experience from work in corporate finance and investment analysis, as well as data analysis in my current role. I used these skills in the Boulder Community Culture and Safety tax committee to provide novel assessments of the cost/benefit potential for each project.
