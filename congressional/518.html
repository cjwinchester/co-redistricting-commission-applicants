I have advocated for world language education with state and national legislators or their aides several times. At the Arapahoe County Fair, I have represented the Colorado Master Gardener program and have been a presenter and newsletter writer of several aspects of gardening. Also, as Women's Ministry Leader of my church, I have planned, organized and carried out women's events on a variety of topics, occasionally with other churches.

Statement

This is of utmost importance and will affect Colorado for many years to come. I believe I can listen to all sides, consider all aspects of the dialogue, be fair and non-partisan, and make wise decisions.

Statement on Working with Consensus

First, I think listening to everyone's ideas and viewpoints while taking notes is important. Next, determine which ideas are similar and which thoughts have no agreement. Then work on wording of ideas to create consensus. As a retired high school teacher with three decades of experience, I had to be impartial when students were arguing, had disagreements, and then come to a resolution that all sides could live with. Also, I have led socratic seminars with students and adults to ensure that everyone has a voice and could state their opinions.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

Again, I believe it is important for everyone to state their opinions and give reasons why they think they are correct. Then, are the reasons factual or emotional? Where are the opinions coming from? Is the source reliable, from a social media platform, or a third party opinion? Is the source recent or from 10-15 years ago? All these are pertinent questions to ask when resolving a problem. From this information and probably others, I then make my decision.
