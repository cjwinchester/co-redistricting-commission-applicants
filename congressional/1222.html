Statement

I have worked in Graph Theory, Optimization, and Game Theory and a large number of publication/patents, at least 69 total, of which ~55 fall into these categories, and even one on Mathematical Political Science (Condorcet voting). Since working at Raytheon, I have created prototyped algorithms for Satellites Control systems to schedule and model systems using large dataset. Fairly partitioning Colorado into 8 or so equal population contiguous districts is a very important problem, and I would be honored to be involved in it.

Statement on Working with Consensus

A approach would be to iteratively develop general principles upon which tentative prototype districtings can be developed and analyzed. I am also an aware of how to gerrymander (they were an example in my graph theory class), so I point out inputs cannot be part of a fair solution.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

This is my forte. I have devoted my life to analytic problem-solving and method development. And I am well-versed at using rapid prototyping to drive out undesirable consequences, and to propose alternatives.
