Statement

I remember learning about redistricting in my high school government class and appreciate Colorado's fair and equitable approach. I would love to have the opportunity to work with a bi-partisan team of citizens to lay out an approach that serves Coloradans well for the future.

Statement on Working with Consensus

I have taken training in organizational leadership, collaborative work, and deliberative decision-making as part of my role as department chair - including ways to bring folks from marginalized positionalities into conversations and encourage communicative equity through a "step up / step back" approach. I believe that no person can be entirely impartial or objective, BUT that we can work to be fair through self-education and through cultivating our self-reflexivity. I will work to be fair in this process by reading any assigned materials, engaging actively and thoughtfully with commission experts and commission members, and working as appropriate to gain insights from community members.

Relevant Analytic Skills for Commission

As an academic, I operate with an evidence-based framework in both my research and in the methodologies and approaches that I teach to students. No person is 100% logical all the time, but I will look for evidence-based processes to address problems that the commission might find, and evidence-based solutions.
